# BY-LAWS OF THE LOUISVILLE-JEFFERSON COUNTY DEMOCRATIC PARTY

Source: https://www.louisvilledems.com/ljcdp-bylaws
Fetched: 2026-04-20

---

## SECTION 1 — VESTED POWER
**1.1** The local Democratic Party's official name shall be the Louisville-Jefferson County Democratic Party. The Executive Power of the Louisville-Jefferson County Democratic Party shall be vested in the County Executive Committee.

## SECTION 2 — EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE MEMBERSHIP
**2.1** The County Executive Committee shall be comprised of:
- Legislative District Chairs
- Legislative District Vice Chairs
- County Chair
- President of the Council of Club Presidents
- President(s) of sanctioned Jefferson County Democratic Women's Club(s)
- President(s) of sanctioned Jefferson County Young Democrat Club(s)
- One At-Large member selected by and for the Democratic elected officials of Jefferson County
- A number of At-Large members equal to the number of State Legislative Districts wholly or partially within Jefferson County

**2.2** The LD, Vice LDs and Members at Large must sign a Non-Disclosure Agreement at the beginning of their term on the County Executive Committee and any subsequent NDA required by the local party. Each member has a fiduciary relationship with the Democratic Executive Committee.

## SECTION 3 — EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE VOTING MEMBERSHIP
**3.1** Voting members:
- Legislative District Chairs
- Respective Legislative District Vice Chair from the same district
- One At-Large member selected by and for Democratic elected officials
- One At-Large member mandated by State Central Executive Committee for 2000-2004 term (expired at 2004 Reorg)
- A number of At-Large members equal to the number of State Legislative Districts wholly or partially within Jefferson County
- President(s) of sanctioned Jefferson County Democratic Women's Club(s)
- President(s) of sanctioned Jefferson County Young Democrats Club(s)

**3.2** Democratic Women's Club president(s) shall have one vote amongst themselves, each partition of the one vote being equal.
**3.3** Young Democratic Club president(s) shall have one vote amongst themselves, each partition of the one vote being equal.
**3.4** Women's Clubs and Young Dems Clubs shall be sanctioned by a majority vote of the voting members of the CEC, with said vote occurring 6 months after the club(s) having made application.
**3.4.1** Clubs of club presidents serving as of the date of adoption of these By-Laws shall be considered as presently sanctioned.

## SECTION 4 — ELECTION OF LD CHAIRS, LD VICE CHAIRS, LD AT-LARGE MEMBERS
**4.1** The election of LD Chairs and Vice-Chairs shall be governed by the State Party By-Laws. Election for Jefferson County At-Large members shall be governed by State Party By-Laws and these By-Laws.
**4.2** Candidates shall make a Declaration and Notification Filing to the CEC. Filings for LD Chair, LD VC, and LD At-Large shall be made at the LJCDP Headquarters.
**4.2.1** Filings: LD Chair, LD VC, and LD At-Large Member are not required to prefile but may do so between October 1 of the year prior to the quadrennial Reorganization, and up to the close of business or no later than 5 p.m. on the final filing date of the year of the quadrennial Reorganization for candidates for local partisan offices who must file with the Jefferson County Clerk. Nominations may be nominated from the floor at the district convention.
**4.2.2** LD Chairs, LD Vice-Chairs, and LD At-Large members may not file together; candidates must file independently. Section does not prohibit candidates from soliciting votes together or advocating for a slate.
**4.3** All candidates must, at the time of their election, whether for a full term or unexpired term, be a resident of the District they intend to serve.
**4.4** LD Chairs and LD Vice Chairs are elected on a date set by the State Central Executive Committee. **LD At-Large members are elected by the LD Chairs** on a date set by the CEC, within 30 days of the Reorganization.
**4.5** Terms for these offices are from one quadrennial Reorganization Day to the next quadrennial Reorganization day.

## SECTION 5 — DUTIES OF THE COUNTY EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE
**5.1** Govern the Democratic Party of Louisville and Jefferson County.
**5.2** Have all responsibility, jurisdiction, and supervision over all matters relating to or affecting the Democratic party in Louisville and Jefferson County and its organization, finances, candidate nomination, campaign management, and public relations.
**5.3** Make rules, by-laws, and regulations as deemed necessary to promote the progress of the Democratic party, so long as not inconsistent with KY and National parties.
**5.4** Hire staff and define their duties.
**5.5** Maintain offices for the LJCDP.
**5.6** Provide for efficient and effective operation of the party.
**5.7** Take other measures as deemed necessary for support and election of Democratic candidates.
**5.8** Appoint a Chair Pro-Tempore for a specific meeting when both County Chair and VC are absent. No person may be appointed by the County Chair or VC.
**5.9** Govern themselves under the laws of the Commonwealth of Kentucky.

## SECTION 6 — DUTIES OF LEGISLATIVE DISTRICT CHAIRS
**6.1** Conduct the affairs of the Democratic Party within their respective LD.
**6.2** Maintain a Party organization of captains, and others as needed, within the precincts of their LDs.
**6.3** Comply with KRS in providing appropriate Election Day workers for the precincts in their LD.
**6.4** Comply with the requirements provided in the by-laws of the local, KY, and National Democratic Parties.
**6.5** Serve on the various committees of the CEC whenever possible.
**6.6** Elect LD At-Large members, one from each LD wholly or partially within Jefferson County, residents of the LD, for a term equal to LD Chairs and LD VCs.
**6.6.1** LD At-Large members shall be chosen from among those who have properly filed a Declaration and Notification within the filing period.

## SECTION 7 — DUTIES OF LEGISLATIVE DISTRICT VICE-CHAIRS
**7.1** Serve in the capacity of the LD Chair at meetings of the CEC at which their respective LD Chair is absent.
**7.2** Repealed 2022.
**7.3** Assist **under the direction of the LD Chair** in the affairs of the Democratic Party within their LD.
**7.4** Comply with the requirements provided in the by-laws of the local, KY, and National Parties.
**7.5** Serve on the various committees of the CEC whenever possible.

## SECTION 8 — DUTIES OF LEGISLATIVE DISTRICT AT-LARGE MEMBERS
**8.1** Assist **under the direction of the LD Chair** in the affairs of the Democratic Party within their LD.
**8.2** Comply with bylaws of local, KY, and National Parties.
**8.3** Serve on various committees of the CEC whenever possible.

## SECTION 9 — DUTIES OF NON-LEGISLATIVE DISTRICT AT-LARGE MEMBERS
**9.1** Comply with bylaws of local, KY, and National Parties.
**9.2** Assist under the direction of the **County Chair** in the affairs of the Democratic Party within Jefferson County.
**9.3** Serve on various committees of the CEC whenever possible.

## SECTION 10 — ELECTION OF COUNTY CHAIR AND COUNTY VICE CHAIR
**10.1** The County Chair shall be elected by the LD Chairs elected in the quadrennial Reorganization, in accordance with the State Party By-Laws. May be any Democrat registered to vote in Jefferson County, KY.
**10.2** Following election of the County Party Chair, the LD Chairs shall elect a County Vice Chair of the opposite sex of the Chair. Said VC must be a newly elected LD Chair.
**10.3** Terms from one quadrennial Reorganization Day to the next.

## SECTION 11 — DUTIES OF THE COUNTY CHAIR
**11.1** Call a meeting upon written request of 25% of CEC members.
**11.2** Hold at least one meeting per quarter, and additional meetings as deemed necessary.
**11.3** Within 30 days of election, appoint a Secretary and a Treasurer, who shall be ex officio non-voting members of the CEC.
**11.4** Serve as the Chief Executive Officer of the CEC; responsible for executing and administering the rules and regulations governing the CEC.
**11.5** Serve as spokesperson for the LJCDP, or otherwise appoint someone to such duty.
**11.6** Appoint members of the CEC to any Standing, Regular, Select, Special, Ad-Hoc, or any other committees of the CEC.
**11.7** Serve as an ex-officio voting member of all Committees of the CEC.
**11.8** Designate CEC members with titles of honoraria upon approval by majority vote of voting members.
**11.9** Any other duties as may be approved by majority vote of voting members.

## SECTION 12 — DUTIES OF THE COUNTY VICE CHAIR
**12.1** Serve in the capacity of the County Chair at meetings of the CEC at which the County Chair is absent.

## SECTION 13 — DUTIES OF THE SECRETARY
**13.1** Serve as ex-officio non-voting member of the CEC.
**13.2** Maintain record of all Democratic organization personnel (official and unofficial) including names, addresses, phone numbers, and email addresses of all Precinct Committee members, LD Chairs, LD VCs, At-Large members, and other CEC members.
**13.3** Maintain record of minutes of all CEC meetings.
**13.4** File all minutes of CEC meetings with the Chair of the State Central Executive Committee within 10 days.
**13.5** Make information available upon request to any Democrat registered to vote in Jefferson County, KY.
**13.5.1** CEC reserves right to charge for materials requested.
**13.6** Serve in capacities as directed by County Party Chair not in conflict with bylaws.

## SECTION 14 — DUTIES OF THE TREASURER
**14.1** Serve as ex-officio non-voting member of CEC.
**14.2** Keep all financial records of LJCDP and CEC.
**14.3** Prepare and present a monthly financial statement to CEC.
**14.4** Serve in capacities as directed by County Party Chair not in conflict with bylaws.
**14.5** Maintain complete and separate accounting of donations designated by donor as restricted to "Headquarters Maintenance" and / or "Headquarters Purchase" and spend for those activities only.

## SECTION 15 — REMOVAL OF OFFICERS
**15.1** Any Party Officer shall be removed for failure to support nominees of National or KY Democratic Party, after hearing and declaration by majority vote.
**15.2** Any Party Officer shall be removed for disavowal of allegiance to National or KY Democratic Committee, after hearing and declaration by majority vote.

## SECTION 16 — REMOVAL OF COUNTY CHAIR
**16.1** County Chair may be removed at any time during term without cause by majority vote of voting members.

## SECTION 17 — DECLARATION OF VACANCY IN OFFICE OF COUNTY CHAIR
**17.1** The office of the County Chair shall be declared vacant should the County Chair fail to hold at least one meeting per quarter of the CEC.

## SECTION 18 — REMOVAL OF COUNTY VICE-CHAIR
**18.1** County Vice Chair may be removed at any time during term without cause by majority vote.

## SECTION 19 — DECLARATION OF VACANCY IN OFFICE OF COUNTY VICE-CHAIR
**19.1** Vacant when a person of the same sex as the incumbent County VC is elected to serve out an unexpired term of County Chair.

## SECTION 20 — DECLARATION OF VACANCY IN OFFICE OF OTHER CEC MEMBERS
**20.1** The office of any CEC member shall be declared vacant by failure to attend, either in person or by written proxy, two successive meetings or 50% of the annual CEC meetings.

## SECTION 21 — FILLING OF VACANCIES
**21.1** Vacancies due to removal, resignation, or any other cause, in the office of County Chair, County Vice Chair, LD Chair, LD Vice Chair, and At-Large member, shall be filled by majority vote of voting members of CEC within 30 days.

## SECTION 22 — QUORUM
**22.1** At all meetings of CEC, attendance by 40% of the total of LD Chairs, LD VCs, and LD At-Large members, in person or by proxy, shall constitute a quorum.
**22.1.1** Proxies cannot be counted to establish quorum for election or dismissal of any Party officer.

## SECTION 23 — PROXIES
**23.1** Voting by proxy is permitted at all CEC meetings, except during election or dismissal of any party officer.
**23.2** An LD Chair, LD VC, or LD At-Large may assign their proxy to another CEC member. Proxy shall be in writing or electronic and shall describe the specific meeting for which assigned and the specific member to whom assigned. No other CEC member may assign their proxy, including the President of the Jefferson County Young Democrats Club and the representative of the Jefferson County Democratic Women's Club.
**23.2.1** A proxy also may be assigned for voting upon a limited purpose during a specific meeting.
**23.3** A proxy must be present at the local Democratic Headquarters by the start of the meeting for which assigned.
**23.3.1** Proxy procedure shall be set by the Chair and approved by majority of voting members.
**23.4** Repealed 2022.

## SECTION 24 — TIE VOTES
**24.1** A tie vote shall be broken by a vote of the State Central Committee.

## SECTION 25 — NEW BUSINESS
**25.1** New business may be introduced at any regular meeting for consideration at the next meeting.
**25.2** New business may be introduced, discussed, and acted upon at the same meeting only after approval by 2/3 of the Legislative Districts wholly or partially within Jefferson County.
**25.2.1** Said voting members may be LD Chairs, LD VCs, LD At-Large members, or a combination.

## SECTION 26 — STANDING COMMITTEES AND THEIR DUTIES
**26.1** Standing Committees shall be:
- Bylaws
- Facilities
- Finance (oversees fundraising)
- Events
- Volunteering
- Youth
- Communication
- Labor
- Training
- Candidate Recruitment

**26.1.1** Duties of the Standing Committees are determined by the County Chair and the Standing Committee Chair for each Standing Committee.
**26.2** Finance Committee shall adopt a Fiscal Policy, reviewed annually in January.
**26.3** Finance Committee shall oversee all purchases.
**26.3.1** Purchases of $500 or less — prior approval of County Chair.
**26.3.2** Purchases $501–$999 — prior approval of County Chair, Treasurer, and Finance Committee Chair.
**26.3.3** Purchases $1000+ — prior approval of Finance Committee AND majority vote of voting members of CEC.

## SECTION 27 — ELECTRONIC VOTING
**27.1** Voting may be taken by electronic means such as email.

## SECTION 28 — APPLICATION OF ROBERT'S RULES OF ORDER
**28.1** Robert's Rules of Order shall apply in any case not covered by these By-Laws.

## SECTION 29 — AMENDMENT, ALTERATION, OR RESCISSION OF BY-LAWS
**29.1** By-Laws may be amended, altered, or rescinded at any meeting at which a quorum is present in person and voting in the majority to so act, provided that written notice of proposed action has been mailed at least 2 weeks prior to the meeting, or such action has been introduced at a previous meeting.

## SECTION 30 — SUPERSESSION OF PREVIOUS BY-LAWS
**30.1** These By-Laws, upon adoption and approval, shall replace and supersede all previously passed By-Laws of the LJCDP and shall become effective immediately.
