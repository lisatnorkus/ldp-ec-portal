# Bylaws Reference for ldp-ec-portal

**Sources:**
- **KDP Bylaws 2025** — `docs/bylaws/kdp-bylaws-2025-full-text.txt` (ratified June 26, 2021; last amended by SCEC March 1, 2025)
- **LJCDP Bylaws** — `docs/bylaws/ljcdp-bylaws-full-text.txt` (Louisville-Jefferson County Democratic Party)

Both full-text files are committed to the repo. This reference tells you where to find what.

**Hierarchy rule:** Where KDP bylaws and LJCDP bylaws conflict, KDP governs (LJCDP §5.3 says local rules "shall not be inconsistent with those of the Kentucky and National Democratic Parties"). Document any drift you spot but do NOT silently resolve it in content — surface the question.

## Answers to the six open questions from the v2.2 role one-pagers

### 1. Has KDP formally set 2028 as back-on-cycle?

**YES — explicitly.** The 2025 reorg was a one-time shift to align with presidential years. Going forward, reorganization is on a four-year presidential-year cycle: 2028, 2032, 2036.

**KDP Article I Section R** (`kdp-bylaws-2025-full-text.txt`, page 6):

> "Reorganization. Reorganization, as defined herein, shall take place in the same calendar year as presidential elections. In order to accommodate the revised Reorganization, which the SCEC moved from the year following presidential elections (year 2025 only) to presidential election years (in connection with its historical practice), all Party Officers and Party Officials, at all levels currently serving pursuant to the rights and powers granted in these Bylaws, whether elected or appointed, shall remain in their respective offices until the conclusion of the 2025 State Convention... This Article I, Section R shall sunset and be removed from these Bylaws at the completion of the 2025 State Convention."

**KDP Article II Section A** restates it: "Every four years, during presidential election years, the Kentucky Democratic Party shall elect Party Officials..."

**Portal copy safe to ship:** "The 2028 reorganization cycle is on a presidential-election year cadence per KDP Bylaws Article II.A."

### 2. Who calls / chairs a Precinct Convention?

**The County Executive Committee Chair** calls the convention. **A Party Officer serves as Convention Chair** for their applicable Convention Level. If that Party Officer is unavailable, the **County Party Chair appoints** a Convention Chair for Precinct or Legislative District Conventions.

**KDP Article II Section B.f** (advertising/notification):

> "Not less than seven (7) nor more than thirty (30) days before a Convention, the CEC Chair and the Chair of the SCEC shall advertise as widely as possible the time, place and purpose for such Convention(s)."

**KDP Article II Section B.g** (Convention Chair):

> "A Party Officer will serve as Convention Chair for their applicable Convention Level. In the event a Party Officer who would otherwise preside over a Convention is unavailable, a Convention Chair shall be appointed by the County Party Chair (for Precinct or Legislative District Conventions) or the State Party Chair (for County or Congressional Conventions)."

**Note on Precinct-level:** KDP Article II Section C.c is specific — after the Precinct Committee is elected, the committee itself chooses a Precinct Chair from among its members. That's *after* the convention, not who chairs the convention.

**v2.2 one-pager language to update:** The LD Chair one-pager says "You call, advertise, and chair your LD's Precinct Conventions in spring 2028." Per the bylaws, it's more accurate to say "The CEC Chair calls and advertises; LD Chairs (as Party Officers) preside over the Precinct Conventions in their LD as Convention Chairs." LD Chairs execute, but the formal convening authority is the CEC Chair.

### 3. Advance-notice requirements for Precinct, LD, and post-LD joint conventions

**General rule — KDP Article II Section B.f:** Not less than **7 days** and not more than **30 days** before a Convention. Applies to Precinct Conventions and Legislative District Conventions.

**Advertisement requirements:** The CEC must use **at least 3 of 6 notification methods** — newspaper, radio/TV, social media, website, Mobilize/Facebook Events, or email/US Mail/phone to CEC members and registered Democrats on the county email list.

**Waiver process** (Article II Section B.e, for date changes): CEC can request a waiver from the SCEC Chair for extraordinary circumstances; SCEC responds within 7 days (or 24 hours for late-notice).

**For the post-LD joint meeting** (where incoming LD Chairs + VCs elect the 18 At-Large members): KDP Article II Section D.c governs this — "At close of the Legislative District Convention, newly elected LD Chairs and Vice Chairs shall meet to elect eighteen (18) at-large members of the County Executive Committee and Committee officers." The bylaws don't set separate notice requirements for this meeting because it happens *immediately at the close of* the LD Convention. LJCDP §4.4 separately says At-Large are elected "within thirty (30) days of the Reorganization" — that's a LJCDP layer that conflicts with the KDP "immediately upon close" language. This is the kind of drift worth flagging for a §4.4 amendment conversation.

**Portal copy safe to ship:** "Advance notice for Precinct and Legislative District Conventions: between 7 and 30 days, per KDP Bylaws Article II.B.f. The CEC must use at least 3 of 6 notification methods."

### 4. Filing window for PC candidates in Jefferson County

**LJCDP §4.2.1** (Jefferson County-specific, `ljcdp-bylaws-full-text.txt`):

> "Filings: Legislative District Chair, Legislative District Vice Chair, and Legislative At-Large Member are not required to prefile but may do so between October 1 of the year prior to the quadrennial Reorganization, and up to the close of business or no later than 5 p.m. on the final filing date of the year of the quadrennial Reorganization for candidates for local partisan offices who must file with the Jefferson County Clerk."

> "Nominations for Legislative District Chair, Legislative District Vice Chair, and Legislative At-Large Member may be nominated from the floor at the district convention."

**Important: this covers LD Chairs, LD Vice Chairs, and Legislative District At-Large members — NOT Precinct Committee candidates.** PCs are elected at the Precinct Convention itself (KDP Article II.C) — registered Democrats residing in the precinct can attend and be nominated from the floor. There's no county-level prefiling window for PC candidates; they're elected at the precinct level on convention day.

**Portal copy safe to ship:**
- For LD Chair / LD VC / LD At-Large candidates: prefile optional between Oct 1 of the year before reorg and 5pm of the filing deadline in reorg year, or nominate from the floor at the LD Convention.
- For Precinct Committee members: no prefiling — elected at the Precinct Convention by eligible voters in the precinct.

### 5. Did the 2025 postponed cycle run the "joint Chairs + VCs elect At-Large" step?

**The bylaws say it should have.** KDP Article II Section D.c is unambiguous: "At close of the Legislative District Convention, newly elected LD Chairs and Vice Chairs shall meet to elect eighteen (18) at-large members of the County Executive Committee and Committee officers."

**Whether it actually ran that way in the 2025 Louisville cycle is a question for Brook Benningfield (Secretary) or Logan Gatti (Chair).** The bylaws language plus the portal's v1 content (which lists 18 At-Large Chairs elected in June 2025) is consistent with the process running per Article II.D.c.

**LJCDP §4.4 conflict:** Louisville's local bylaws still say At-Large are elected "within thirty (30) days of the Reorganization" — not "immediately upon close." This is almost certainly how LJCDP §4.4 fell out of sync with KDP and why v2.2 flags it for amendment. Ask whether the 2025 cycle used the KDP "immediate" timing (superseding) or the LJCDP "within 30 days" timing.

**Portal copy safe to ship:** Cite KDP Article II.D.c as the governing rule. Flag LJCDP §4.4 as drift pending amendment. Do not assert the specific 2025 sequence without confirmation from a Secretary or Chair.

### 6. LJCDP §8 vs §9 — which applies to the current At-Large class?

**Both apply — they govern different types of At-Large members.** This is a real distinction, not drift.

**LJCDP §8 — Duties of Legislative District At-Large Members** (one per LD, elected by the LD Chair per §6.6):

> "8.1 To assist under the direction of the Legislative District Chair in the affairs of the Democratic Party within their respective Legislative District.
> 8.2 To comply with the requirements provided in the by-laws of the local, Kentucky, and National Democratic Parties.
> 8.3 To serve on the various committees of the County Executive Committee whenever possible."

**LJCDP §9 — Duties of Non-Legislative District At-Large Members**:

> "9.1 To comply with the requirements provided in the by-laws of the local, Kentucky, and National Democratic Parties.
> 9.2 To assist under the direction of the County Chair in the affairs of the Democratic Party within Jefferson County.
> 9.3 To serve on the various committees of the County Executive Committee whenever possible."

**The difference:** §8 At-Large serve their LD under the LD Chair. §9 At-Large serve the whole county under the County Chair.

**Which governs the current 18 At-Large Chairs?** KDP Article II.D.c says the 18 At-Large are elected jointly by LD Chairs + VCs and serve the County Executive Committee countywide. That pattern matches §9 (non-LD, county-serving) more than §8 (LD-serving, elected by individual LD Chairs per §6.6).

**BUT:** LJCDP §8 still exists as an active section and §6.6 still says "each Legislative District Chair shall elect" their LD At-Large. This is the drift v2.2 flagged — §4.4, §6.6, and §8 together describe a pre-2025 structure where each LD had its own At-Large. The 2025 reorg replaced that with 18 countywide At-Large per KDP rules, but LJCDP bylaws haven't caught up.

**Portal copy safe to ship:** Current At-Large class is governed by KDP Article II.D.c (18 countywide members elected jointly by LD Chairs + VCs). LJCDP §§4.4, 6.6, and 8 describe the old LD-specific At-Large model and need amendment. The 18 current At-Large members' role most closely resembles the §9 "non-LD At-Large" duties — countywide service under the County Chair — though they also have the §3.1 voting rights that §9 doesn't explicitly name.

## Other key reference pointers for the portal

### Standing Committees — the §26 drift v2.2 flagged

**LJCDP §26.1** lists 10 standing committees: Bylaws, Facilities, Finance, Events, Volunteering, Youth, Communication, Labor, Training, Candidate Recruitment.

**In practice (per v2.2), LDP runs 8 standing + 3 ad hoc:**
- Standing: Bylaws, Candidate Recruitment, Communications, Events, Facilities, Finance, Training, Volunteer
- Ad hoc: Branding, Endorsement Process, Platform

**Drift:** Youth and Labor are listed in §26 but not running. Branding, Endorsement Process, and Platform run but aren't in §26. Naming also drifted (Volunteering → Volunteer, Communication → Communications). §26 needs amendment.

**§26.2 — Finance Committee specific duties:** Adopt Fiscal Policy, reviewed annually in January. Not optional, bylaw-mandated.

**§26.3 — Purchase approval chain (bylaw-mandated, do not paraphrase loosely):**
- $500 or less — prior approval of the County Chair
- $501–$999 — prior approval of the County Chair, Treasurer, AND Finance Committee Chair
- $1000+ — prior approval of the Finance Committee AND majority vote of voting CEC members

### Quorum, proxies, and votes

**§22.1 — Quorum:** 40% of LD Chairs + LD VCs + LD At-Large members, in person or by proxy.

**§22.1.1 — Proxies do NOT count toward quorum for elections or dismissal of Party officers.**

**§23 — Proxies:** Permitted at all CEC meetings EXCEPT during election or dismissal of a Party officer. Written or electronic proxy required; must specify the meeting and the assigned recipient. Proxy must be present at HQ by meeting start. Women's Club president and Young Democrats president **cannot** assign a proxy.

**§24 — Tie votes:** Broken by a vote of the State Central Committee.

### Vacancies

**§21.1 — Filling vacancies:** County Chair, County Vice Chair, LD Chair, LD Vice Chair, and At-Large vacancies are filled by majority vote of voting CEC members within 30 days.

**§20.1 — Declaration of vacancy:** Any CEC member's office is declared vacant if they fail to attend (in person or by written proxy) two successive meetings OR 50% of annual meetings.

**§17.1 — County Chair vacancy:** Declared vacant if Chair fails to hold at least one meeting per quarter.

**§19.1 — Vice Chair vacancy:** Declared vacant when a person of the same sex as incumbent VC is elected to serve out an unexpired term of County Chair. (Gender-balance rule.)

### Removal

**§15 — Removal of any Party Officer:** Majority vote for failure to support Democratic nominees or disavowal of allegiance to National/State Democratic Party, after a hearing.

**§16.1 — County Chair specifically:** May be removed at any time without cause by majority vote of voting CEC members.

**§18.1 — County Vice Chair specifically:** May be removed at any time without cause by majority vote of voting CEC members.

### Endorsement votes

**Not in bylaws directly** — the 60% threshold, secret ballot via ElectionRunner, and no-proxies rule for endorsement votes live in Endorsement Process Committee policy, not the bylaws themselves. v1 portal describes this in the Rock Star Playbook and Canvass Tools. When documenting this in the portal, cite "LDP Endorsement Process policy" not "LJCDP Bylaws §X."

### Amendments

**§29.1 — Bylaws amendment:** Majority vote at a meeting with quorum present, with EITHER two weeks' written notice to all CEC members OR the action having been introduced at a previous meeting.

**§25.2 — New business adopted same-meeting:** Requires 2/3 of LDs represented in voting members present (Chairs + VCs + LD At-Large).

### Non-Disclosure Agreement requirement

**§2.2:** LD Chairs, LD VCs, and At-Large members **must sign an NDA** at the start of their term. Each has a **fiduciary relationship** with the EC. This is worth surfacing in the role one-pagers if it isn't already.

### Robert's Rules

**§28.1:** Robert's Rules of Order apply in any case not covered by these bylaws.

## Two standing rules for Claude Code when citing bylaws in portal content

1. **Quote sparingly and cite precisely.** When the portal surfaces a bylaw rule, cite it as "per LJCDP §X.Y" or "per KDP Bylaws Article X.Y" so users can verify in the source. Do not paraphrase rules where precision matters (vote thresholds, quorum counts, deadlines, dollar amounts).

2. **Flag drift, don't hide it.** Where LJCDP and KDP conflict (or where LJCDP hasn't caught up to a KDP amendment), the portal should name the drift as a known issue rather than silently picking one version. Example: "LJCDP §4.4 and KDP Article II.D.c describe different At-Large election timing — KDP governs, but §4.4 needs amendment." That's the honest answer and it surfaces the amendment need for whoever chairs the next Bylaws Committee meeting.
