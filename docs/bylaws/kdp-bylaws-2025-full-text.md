## ARTICLE I — General Rules and Provisions

**A. Supreme Power in Party Members, Exercised According to Law:** The supreme power of the Kentucky Democratic Party (hereinafter "KDP") rests in the members thereof as exercised by them in State Convention assembled. The organization and conduct of Party affairs shall be in accordance with the Constitutions and laws of the United States and the Commonwealth of Kentucky (Public Laws) and these Party Laws.

**B. Defined Terms:**

**a.** "Kentucky Democratic Party," or the "KDP" shall refer to the organization of the state Democratic Party. The KDP trains and supports (i) Democratic candidates for partisan office, and (ii) Democratic volunteers, including, without limitation, State and County Executive Committees, and Precinct and Legislative District Committees, while promoting policies that adhere to the most recent version of the Democratic Party Platform, as approved by the Democratic National Committee.

**b.** "At-large" shall refer to a position elected to serve an entire area (either at the county or state level, as applicable) rather than one of its subdivisions.

**c.** "County Convention" shall refer to a Convention held in all counties that contain six (6) or fewer legislative districts, wholly or partially, within its boundaries, the purpose of which is to elect Party Officials.

**d.** "County Executive Committee" or "CEC" shall refer to the committee that oversees County Party affairs, as further described in Article III herein, and that was elected at the Legislative District or County Conventions, as applicable, and as described in Article II herein.

**e.** "County Delegate(s)" shall refer to delegate(s) elected by precinct delegates or legislative district delegates, as applicable, at a County Convention and who then attend the State Convention, where they will elect members of the State Central Executive Committee.

**f.** "Legislative District Convention" shall refer to a convention held in only those counties with seven (7) or more State House legislative districts, wholly or partially, within its boundaries, the purpose of which is to elect the Legislative District Chair and Vice Chair, who then sit on the County Executive Committee.

**g.** "Party Official" shall refer to a member of a Precinct Committee, County Executive Committee, Legislative District Committee who is not the (i) Chair, (ii) Vice Chair, (iii) Secretary, (iv) Treasurer of such Committee, or (v) a member of a Nominating Committee, as defined herein.

**h.** "Party Leader" shall refer to a Democrat who holds an elected statewide office, a Democratic member of Kentucky's US Senate or Congressional delegation, a Democrat who holds a seat within the Kentucky General Assembly, the Chair of the KDP, the Executive Director of the KDP and any voting member of the State Central Executive Committee who does not hold the title of Chair or Vice Chair.

**i.** "Party Representative" shall refer to a member who sits on the State Central Executive Committee in either an ex-officio status or as a representative of an affiliated Democratic organization, council or caucus.

**j.** "Party Officer" shall refer to a Chair, Vice Chair, Treasurer and Secretary of the State Central Executive Committee, a County Executive Committee, a Legislative District Committee or a Precinct Committee.

**k.** "State Central Executive Committee" or "SCEC" shall refer to the committee of elected members, chosen by State Delegates at the State Convention, which adopts Party policy to be executed by the Chair, Executive Director and staff of the Kentucky Democratic Party, who manage day-to-day Party business and affairs on behalf of the SCEC. The SCEC includes representative members of certain affiliate organizations, caucuses and councils and appointed ex-officio members.

**l.** "State Delegate(s)" shall refer to a Democrat who is elected at either a Legislative District Convention or a County Convention, as described herein, and whose role is to elect State Central Executive Committee members from the State Delegates' Congressional District and, additionally, to elect At-Large members of the State Central Executive Committee.

**m.** "Reorganization" shall refer to the process in which Precinct Committees, Legislative District Committees, County Executive Committees and the State Central Executive Committee elect new members and officers, as described further in Article II herein.

**n.** "Youth" shall refer to any registered Democrat under the age of 40.

**C. Registered Democrats are Party Members:** Membership of the KDP shall consist of all persons who are registered to vote as Democrats in the Commonwealth of Kentucky.

**D. No Discrimination in Registration:** The KDP, at all levels, shall support the broadest possible registration without discrimination based on age (if of voting age), gender identity, religion, economic status, sexual orientation, ethnic identity or physical disability.

**E. No Discrimination in Party Meetings:** All public meetings at all levels of the KDP are open to all members of the KDP regardless of age (if of voting age), gender identity, religion, economic status, sexual orientation, ethnic identity or physical disability.

**F. No Tests or Oaths Based on Discrimination:** No test of membership in, nor any oaths of loyalty to the KDP, shall be required or used which requires prospective or current members to acquiesce in, condone, or support discrimination on the grounds of sex, race, color, creed, or national origin.

**G. Publication of Party Election Procedures:** The KDP shall publicize fully and in such manner as to assure notice to all interested parties a full description of the legal and practical procedures for selection of Democratic Party officers and representatives on all levels.

**H. Publication of Qualifications for Party Office:** The KDP shall publicize fully and in such manner as to assure notice to all interested parties a complete description of the legal and practical qualifications for all officers and representatives of the State Democratic Party.

**I. Democratic Primaries**

**a.** No Democrat Committee governed by these By-Laws, or any Democratic Party Officer acting in their official capacity, shall endorse or support one Democratic candidate over another Democratic candidate in a Democratic Primary Election.

**b.** No assets of the Democratic Party shall be used in a Democratic Primary Election unless they are made available equally to all Democrat Candidates in that specific primary election, except when action is taken by the State Central Executive Committee per Article I, Section H(1)(a).

> **i.** In the event of an incumbent Statewide Kentucky Constitutional Officer, the State Central Executive Committee may vote, by a three-fourths (3/4) majority, to endorse an incumbent Statewide Kentucky Constitutional Officer for reelection and may provide this incumbent the support and resources during the Primary Election generally available to a Democratic nominee during the General Election. The vote on such a motion should be made by secret ballot. Upon passage of such a motion, the Secretary shall notify the chairs of the County Executive Committees. Any Party Officer who maintains or provides support for other candidates in the primary shall not be subject to the disciplinary action processes outlined in Article I for non-support. If a non-endorsed candidate for Statewide Kentucky Constitutional Office wins the Primary Election, and therefore the nomination, all applicable provisions in these Bylaws shall automatically apply.

**c.** Democratic Party Officers who are candidates in a Democratic Primary Election are not prohibited from listing their party position or party office in their campaign materials. Also, this does not prevent any Democratic Party Officer from supporting or endorsing the Democrat of their choice in a primary election as long as they are not acting in an official party capacity.

**J. Removal of Support for a Party Nominee or Elected Official.** The State Central Executive Committee may, by a three-fourths (3/4) majority, vote to remove party support from, or disassociate with, a Democratic nominee or a Democrat who currently holds elected office. The vote shall be taken upon the motion of a member of SCEC, which must include the cause(s) for the removal of support. Upon passage of such a motion, the Secretary shall notify the chairs of County Executive Committees located within the district impacted by the decision, or all County Executive Committees, if applicable.

**K. Dismissal of Party Officers** — covers intentional disclosure of confidential party info, intentional failure to comply with bylaws, failure to support KDP nominees, disavowal of allegiance. Hearings at County level for Precinct Officers; SCEC for all other Party Officers. Appeals per Article V. Dismissed officers prohibited from holding Party office for two years.

**L. Minutes of Meetings:** All minutes of any meeting at every level of the Democratic Party are open to inspection by any registered Democrat upon request.

**M. Adequate Notices of Times and Places for Party Meetings:** The time and place for all meetings of the KDP at all levels shall be publicized fully, and in such a manner as to assure adequate notice to all interested persons.

**N. House and Senate Caucuses.** Decisions of the Democratic Senate and House Caucuses are not governed by these By-Laws.

**O. Selection of Delegates to the National Convention.** Delegates to the Democratic National Convention are elected pursuant to a Delegate Selection Plan, as adopted and approved by both the SCEC and the Democratic National Committee.

**P. Democratic National Committee Members:** The SCEC shall, at the meeting prior to a Democratic National Convention, elect two voting members to the Democratic National Committee of different genders in accordance with the rules of the Democratic National Committee.

**Q. Rules of Order.** Robert's Rules of Order shall apply in any case not covered by these party laws or national party rules.

**R. Reorganization.** Reorganization, as defined herein, shall take place in the same calendar year as presidential elections. In order to accommodate the revised Reorganization, which the SCEC moved from the year following presidential elections (year 2025 only) to presidential election years (in connection with its historical practice), all Party Officers and Party Officials, at all levels currently serving pursuant to the rights and powers granted in these Bylaws, whether elected or appointed, shall remain in their respective offices until the conclusion of the 2025 State Convention, or such preceding Congressional District, County, Legislative District or Precinct Convention, as applicable to the respective office, which shall occur in the following Presidential Election year. **This Article I, Section R shall sunset and be removed from these Bylaws at the completion of the 2025 State Convention.**

## ARTICLE II — Reorganization: Local and State Conventions

**A. Purpose:** Every four years, during presidential election years, the Kentucky Democratic Party shall elect Party Officials, as defined in Article I herein, at the precinct, legislative district, county, congressional and state levels (each a "Convention Level") who will be responsible for carrying out the business of the Kentucky Democratic Party.

### B. General/Common Provisions

**a. Scope:** Each Convention Level shall follow the rules of this Article.

**b. Position Term:** Registered Democrats elected to serve as Party Officials or Party Officer shall serve a four (4) year term unless death, removal, or resignation occur, or until the conclusion of the following precinct, legislative district, county, congressional or state convention at which such position is elected.

**c. Eligibility to Run:** Any Democrat registered to vote within the applicable Convention Level (e.g. an individual running for a Precinct Committee position must be registered to vote within the Precinct in which they are running) can run for a position as a Party Official or Party Officer.

**d. Convention Levels:**

- **Precinct Conventions** elect Precinct Committee members (two people of different genders and youth of any gender)
- **Legislative District Conventions** (if applicable — 7+ LDs in the county) elect Legislative District Committee members (Chair and Vice Chair of opposite genders)
- **County Conventions** elect County Executive Committee members + delegates to Congressional District and State Conventions
- **Congressional District Conventions** elect CD delegates to the State Central Executive Committee
- **State Convention** delegates elect at-large SCEC members + ratify Chair/Vice Chair election; DNC delegate selection happens concurrently

**e. Time, Place, Location:** Dates/times approved by SCEC. Locations for Precinct, LD, and County Conventions set by CEC Chairs. Locations for Congressional District and State Conventions set by SCEC Chair.

> **Waivers:** CEC may petition SCEC Chair for waivers due to extraordinary circumstances (act of God, declared state of emergency, conflicting major event). SCEC Chair responds within 7 days (24 hours for late-notice emergencies).

**f. Advertisement/Notification of Conventions:** Not less than seven (7) nor more than thirty (30) days before a Convention, the CEC Chair and the Chair of the SCEC shall advertise as widely as possible the time, place and purpose for such Convention(s). The applicable County or State Executive Committee shall utilize a combination of at least three (3) of the following methods:

1. Public service notice or news item in local newspapers
2. Public service announcement or news item on local radio/TV
3. Notice on the County Party's social media pages
4. Notice on the County Party's website
5. Web-based organization and event builders (Mobilize, Facebook Events, etc.)
6. E-mails / US Mail / Telephone calls to CEC members and county Democrats on the party's email list

**g. Convention Chair:** A Party Officer will serve as Convention Chair for their applicable Convention Level. If the Party Officer is unavailable, a Convention Chair shall be appointed by the County Party Chair (for Precinct or Legislative District Conventions) or the State Party Chair (for County or Congressional Conventions).

**h. Tellers:** Convention Chair appoints tellers upon request by any member of the convention.

**i. Method of Voting:**
- **Proxy Voting:** No proxy voting permitted at any Convention.
- **Open Floor Nominations:** Any delegate may nominate candidates (including self-nominations), provided the nominee is a registered Democrat in the applicable Convention Level.
- **Voting:** If two or more persons nominated, vote must be by secret ballot. Each voter gets one vote per Party Office.
- **Tie Votes:** Runoff election for tied candidates. If runoff is tied, coin flip decides.

**j. Alternates:** After delegates elected, Convention elects alternates by same process.

**k. Delegate Vacancies:** Alternates succeed. If any delegate position remains unfilled, it loses its vote.

**l. Convention Records and Reporting Requirements:** Within 10 days following Precinct/County/LD Conventions, the newly elected County Party Chair shall submit all official minutes, officer forms, delegate forms, and other requested information to the State Party Chair.

**m. Appeals/Waivers:** See Article V.

### C. Precinct Conventions

**a. Purpose:** Elect two people of different genders and a youth (of any gender) as Precinct Committee Members.

**b. Eligibility to Vote:** Any Democrat, residing and registered to vote in the Precinct.

**c. Precinct Chair:** Upon election, newly elected committee chooses one member to serve as Precinct Chair.

### D. Legislative District Conventions

**a. Purpose:** In counties with 7+ LDs only. Elect LD Chair and Vice Chair (different genders), elect delegates to CD and State Conventions.

**b. Eligibility to Vote:** Only duly elected Precinct Committee members from that LD.

**c. At-Large Members:** **At close of the Legislative District Convention, newly elected LD Chairs and Vice Chairs shall meet to elect eighteen (18) at-large members of the County Executive Committee and Committee officers.**

### E. County Conventions

Elect County Executive Committees and delegates to Congressional District and State Conventions.

### F. Congressional District Conventions

Elect four delegates (no more than two of the same gender) + one youth, to represent the CD on SCEC.

### G. State Convention

Elects nine at-large SCEC members; ratifies Chair, Vice Chair, and bylaws.

### H. Democratic National Convention Delegate Selection

Per the Delegate Selection Plan approved by both the SCEC and the DNC.

## ARTICLE III — Precinct, Legislative District and County Executive Committees

### A. General Provisions

**a. Proxy Voting:** Not permitted in election or removal to/from any office.

> Proxies CAN be used at any State or County Executive Committee meeting for regular or special business other than electing/removing individuals. Must be in writing, state how and for what purpose it will be voted.

**b. Meeting Removal:** Any non-member can be removed from a meeting for rules violations per Robert's Rules.

### B. Precinct Committee

**a. Election:** Per Article II.

**b. Purpose:** Committee of two people of different genders + a youth from the precinct. **Primary Party Officials responsible for organizing and building Democratic power within the precinct.** Duties include voter contact, volunteer recruitment, phonebanks, organizing events. Precinct Committee members serve as delegates to Legislative District Convention or County Convention.

**c. Filling Vacancies in Precinct Committees:** Notify SCEC; CEC fills within 90 days, else SCEC Chair fills.

### C. Legislative District Committee

**a. Election:** Per Article II.

**b. Purpose:** LD Committee of Chair and Vice Chair (opposite genders). Represent that state House District on the County Executive Committee. Responsible for organizing and building Democratic support within that state House District. Duties include voter contact and volunteer recruitment, phonebanks, organizing events.

### D. County Executive Committee

**a. Powers and Duties:** The executive power of the Democratic Party of each County shall be vested in the CEC, which shall have all the powers and duties relevant to the county which are given to the SCEC for the state as a whole.

**b. Election of County and/or LD Party Officers:** Different methods by county size. **Counties with 7+ LDs** elect LD Chairs/VCs at LD Conventions; those LD Chairs then elect the County Chair and Vice Chair (different genders). **County Executive Committee then elects 18 additional At-Large members within 35 days**, proportional to Dem registration in each LD.

**c. Gender and Youth Requirements:** No more than half of CEC can be same gender. Must include one person age 35 or younger.

**d. Requirements for Creating a County Committee between Conventions:** If a county fails to properly reorganize, the SCEC Chair may declare no valid CEC and appoint an ad-hoc committee for up to 120 days to rebuild.

**e. Duties of Officers of the County Executive Committee:**

> **i. Duties of Chair:** Chair serves as chief strategist and governing officer. Calls meetings, presides. Must hold at least one CEC meeting per quarter; quarterly meetings shall occur during February, May, August and November.

> **ii. Duties of the Secretary:** Maintain record of all Democratic organization personnel (official and unofficial) including names, addresses, phone numbers of all PC members, LD Chairs, VCs, and CEC members, plus minutes of all meetings. File minutes with Chair of SCEC and Executive Director of KDP within 10 days.

> **iii. Duties of the Treasurer:** Maintain finances and financial records. Ensure receipts and expenditures comply with Kentucky and federal rules.

**f. Resignation and Removal of County Party Officers and Officials:**
- **Removal of Chair:** Majority vote of entire CEC, without cause
- **Removal of CEC members for cause:** Majority vote of CEC
- **Automatic vacancy for death, relocation, disability, resignation**

**g. Automatic Vacancies:** If a CEC member fails to attend, in person or by written proxy, two successive meetings OR fails to attend 50% of annual meetings in person, their office shall be deemed and declared vacant by the County Chair.

**h. Filling Vacancies on County Executive Committees:** CEC has 90 days to fill; else SCEC Chair fills.

**i. Vacancies Caused by Redistricting in Counties Containing Seven or More Legislative Districts:** In years new legislative district maps are drawn, position of any EC member who no longer resides in the legislative district they represent becomes vacant on July 1. CEC has 60 days; else SCEC Chair fills.

**j. Tie Vote:** Tie vote of CEC broken by vote of SCEC.

**k. Quorum:** 40% of the elected committee members must be present (in person or by proxy). Proxies cannot be counted to establish quorum for an election or dismissal of any Party officer.

## ARTICLE IV — State Central Executive Committee

**A. Executive Power:** The SCEC shall have jurisdiction, supervision, and control over the party organization, state party bylaws, the calling of all conventions, and the nomination of candidates in compliance with Kentucky election laws.

**B. Powers and Duties of Chair:** Chief Executive Officer of the SCEC. Calls meetings; must call at least one per quarter.

**C. Powers of the Vice-Chair:** Presides at meetings of the SCEC in absence of the Chair. Succeeds to powers of Chair on Chair's death/resignation/incapacity.

**D. Election of Chair and Vice-Chair:** By nominating committee unless sitting Democratic Governor presents a candidate. Ratified at next State Convention.

**E. Voting Members of the Committee:** Four members + one youth per CD, elected by CD Conventions; National Committee members; nine at-large members elected by State Convention; Chair and Vice Chair; four members from General Assembly (two elected by each chamber); presidents of KY Young Democrats and Democratic Woman's Club of Kentucky; chairs of recognized caucuses and councils.

**F. Non-Voting Members:** Executive Director, Legal Counsel, Treasurer (appointed by Chair); all KY Statewide Constitutional Officers, all KY Democratic US Congresspeople and Senators (ex-officio); up to three additional appointed ex-officio.

**G. Removal of Members:** Subject to Article I.K. Automatic vacancy for relocation, death, disability, resignation; missing two successive meetings or 50% of annual meetings.

**H. Filling of Vacancies:** CD vacancies filled by that CD's caucus; at-large vacancies filled by at-large caucus; unfilled after 60 days → SCEC Chair appoints with majority vote of remaining.

**I. Election of Chair Pro Tem:** If Chair and Vice-Chair both absent, elect one of the members present to preside.

**J. Chair May Vote:** Chair, Vice-Chair, or Chair Pro Tempore may cast one vote each if they choose.

**K. Role of the Secretary:** Appointed by and serves at pleasure of Chair.

**L. Finance Committee:** Consists of Party Treasurer, Chair, Executive Director, two at-large members, and one from each of the six CDs. Prepares annual budget, recommends salaries/staffing, fundraising goals.

**M. Recognition of Organizations or Committees:** SCEC shall not recognize any organization or committee using "Democrat" title unless formed per KDP and DNC bylaws.

**N. Strategic Planning Committee:** Committee consists of KDP Executive Director, Chair of the SCEC, a Committee Chair, one SCEC member from each CD, one SCEC selected member from each CD, Chair of each SCEC standing Committee, up to 3 At-Large members.

**O. By-Laws Committee:** Chair appointed by KDP Chair. Reviews and recommends proposed amendments to SCEC by majority vote.

**P. Appellate and Waiver Committee:** SCEC Chair + one member per CD. Hears appeals and waivers per Article V.

## ARTICLE V — Appeals and Waivers

**A. PRECINCT CONVENTION AND PRECINCT ELECTION APPEAL:** Aggrieved party has 3 days to file written appeal with Chair of the CEC. CEC hearing within 3 days; all interested parties given opportunity to present. Majority vote determines. If not satisfied, appeal to SCEC within 5 days; SCEC hearing within 7 days.

**B. LEGISLATIVE DISTRICT OR COUNTY CONVENTION APPEAL:** 7 days to file written appeal with SCEC Chair. SCEC hearing within 15 days. Majority determines, final and conclusive.

**D. APPEAL FROM CONGRESSIONAL DISTRICT CONVENTION:** Filed with Appellate Committee.

**C. APPEAL FROM STATE CONVENTION:** 7 days to file with SCEC Chair; SCEC hearing within 15 days.

**D. APPEALS OF DECISION BY COUNTY EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE UNDER ARTICLE V, SECTION A:** 5 days to file with County Chair; CEC hearing within 15 days; appeal to SCEC within 7 days; SCEC hearing within 30 days.

**E. ALL OTHER APPEALS:** 5 days to file written appeal with SCEC Chair; SCEC hearing within 30 days.

**F. Exhaustion of Appeal Procedures:** No member may bring legal action until all appeal procedures exhausted. Jurisdiction and venue for any legal action: Franklin County Circuit Court.

**G. Precinct and County/Legislative District Convention Waivers:** CEC Chair or ad-hoc committee can request a waiver. SCEC Chair/Executive Director approves; Appellate and Waiver Committee reviews.

**H. County Executive Committee Rules Waiver:** CEC Chair or 40% of sitting CEC can request waiver. Submitted to SCEC Chair → Appellate and Waiver Committee reviews.

## ARTICLE VI — Committees for the Purpose of Nominating Candidates for Special Elections and Selecting Replacement Candidates

**See the verbatim file `kdp-bylaws-2025-article-vi.md` in this folder for the full text.** Summary:

### A. Congressional Nominating Committees in Multi-County Districts

Composed of Democratic Party County Chairs of each county or portion thereof within such district. Chair: county that cast largest Dem vote at preceding Presidential.

### B. State House / State Senate / Other District Nominating Committees in Multi-County Districts

Composed of voting members of the Executive Committee of each county or portion thereof who reside within such district. If <2 voting members reside: Precinct Committee Members represent.

### C. One-County Districts

**a. Congressional, State Senate, Magisterial, Constabulary, entire-county Districts in one county with 7+ LDs** (e.g. Jefferson County SD26): Composed of Chairs of every LD wholly or partially within such district. Only LD Chairs vote. Chaired by County Chair.

**b. State House Districts in one county with 7+ LDs** (e.g. any HD wholly in Jefferson County): **Nominating committee consists of precinct committee members residing within the legislative district.** Vote of each PC member = total registered Democrats in the precinct they represent. Chaired by LD Chair (or VC, or senior At-Large residing in the LD). Upon vacancy notice: LD Chair convenes organizing committee of CEC members residing in the LD to determine date/time/location/process.

**c. Metro Council / other non-entire-county Districts in one county with 7+ LDs:** Voting CEC members + LD VC residing within the LDs comprising the MC district. Proportional weighted voting.

**d. State House/Senate/other Districts wholly within one county with 6 or fewer LDs:** Voting members of the CEC who reside within such district. If <3 voting members reside: PC Members.

### D. County Commissioners

Considered county-wide officers. **Committee Nominations for Elective Office to be by Vote in Proportion to Democratic Registration.** Example: County Executive Committee Members entitled to vote will cast a vote equal to the number of registered Democrats in their county who reside within the district, divided by the total number of voting members of the CEC who reside within the district.

### E. Statewide Nominating Committee

Composed of the voting members of the SCEC. Only SCEC voting members at the time of vacancy entitled to vote. Chair: KDP Chair (or Vice Chair). Vote of each SCEC member is equal-weighted.

### F. Nominating Committees to be Governing Authority

All Nominating Committees established under this Article are the governing authority to determine how nominations shall be made and to declare all nominees not inconsistent with public or party law. By majority vote, a Nominating Committee can include other Democratic Party Officials as voting members.

## ARTICLE VII — Caucuses and Councils

**A. General Provisions:** The SCEC may, at its discretion, grant official recognition to statewide caucuses¹ and councils² of Democrats sharing similar identity, common interests, and objectives.

¹ *Caucuses:* composed of groups sharing immutable characteristics (perceived as unchangeable, entrenched, innate).

² *Councils:* composed of groups sharing common interests and objectives (labor councils, education councils, rural councils, etc.).

**B. Official Recognition:** Proposal + draft mission statement + 30 signatures (registered Democrats across 3+ CDs) → SCEC Chair → appointed review committee → Bylaws Committee reviews proposed caucus/council bylaws → SCEC approval by majority.

**C. Seat on the SCEC:** Chair of recognized caucus/council gets a seat, provided the caucus/council annually meets these standards: 30+ active members, recruiting to create statewide org, quarterly meetings, quarterly reports.

**D. Membership:** Open to all registered Democrats who meet caucus/council membership requirements.

**E. Bylaws Provisions:** Must support only Democratic elected officials and nominees. Officer-removal procedure. Fundraising requires prior written approval from SCEC Chair and Executive Director.

**F. Annual Reporting:** In first quarter of every year, each caucus/council must report membership, attendance, and activities for preceding calendar year.
