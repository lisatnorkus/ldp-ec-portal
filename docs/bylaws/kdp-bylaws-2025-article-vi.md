# KDP Bylaws Article VI — Nominating Committees for Special Elections and Replacement Candidates

**Source:** KDP Bylaws, ratified June 26, 2021, last amended by SCEC March 1, 2025.
**Article VI, Pages 30–33 of the ratified document.**
**File committed to the repo:** April 21, 2026.

Full Article VI text, quoted verbatim from the ratified KDP bylaws.

## A. Congressional, State House of Representatives, State Senate, and Other District Nominating Committees in Multi-County Districts

### a. Congressional Nominating Committees in Multi-County Districts

The Nominating Committees of Congressional Districts comprised of precincts in more than one county shall be composed of the Democratic Party County Chairs of each county or portion thereof within such district.

### b. Chair of multi-county Congressional Nominating Committees

The Chair of the county that cast the largest Democratic vote in precincts within the district at the preceding Presidential election shall be the Chair of said Nominating Committee, but if they are absent or unable to act, then the Chair of the county that cast the next largest Democratic vote in precincts within the district at the preceding Presidential election shall act as Chair.

---

## B. State House of Representatives, State Senate, and other District Nominating Committees in Multi-County Districts

### a. Composition

The Nominating Committees of State House of Representatives, State Senate, and other Districts comprised of precincts in more than one county shall be composed of the voting members of the Executive Committee of each county or portion thereof who reside within such district.

### b. Fallback when fewer than 2 EC members reside in district

If less than two (2) voting members of the Executive Committee of an affected county reside within the district, then the precincts within the affected district of said county shall be represented on said Nominating Committee by the Precinct Committee Members within such district.

### c. Eligibility to vote

Only those Democrats who are Executive Committee Members or Precinct Committee Members, as described within this paragraph, at the time the vacancy occurs shall be entitled to vote.

### d. Chair

The Chair of the county that cast the largest Democratic vote in precincts within the district at the preceding Presidential election shall be the Chair of said Nominating Committee, but if they are absent or unable to act, then the Chair of the county that cast the next largest Democratic vote in precincts within the district at the preceding Presidential election shall act as Chair.

---

## C. Congressional, State House of Representatives, State Senate, and other District Nominating Committees in One-County Districts

### a. Congressional, State Senate, Magisterial, Constabulary, and entire-county Districts wholly within only one county containing seven or more Legislative Districts

> **This is the Louisville case for State Senate vacancies.**

The Nominating Committees of Congressional, State Senate, Magisterial, Constabulary, and entire-county Districts comprised of precincts wholly within only one county containing seven (7) or more Legislative Districts shall be composed of the Chairs of every Legislative District wholly or partially within such district.

**i.** Only those Democrats who are Legislative District Chairs at the time the vacancy occurs shall be entitled to vote.

**ii.** The Chair of the County Executive Committee shall be the Chair of said Nominating Committee. If such Chair is absent or unable to act, then the Vice-Chair of the County Executive Committee shall be the Chair of said Nominating Committee. If such Vice-Chair is absent or unable to act, then the Chair of the Legislative District that cast the largest Democratic vote in precincts within the district at the preceding Presidential election shall act as Chair.

**iii.** Any presiding officer of said Nominating Committee shall not have a vote unless they are a Legislative District Chair wholly or partially within the district at the time the vacancy occurs.

### b. State House of Representatives Districts wholly within only one County containing seven or more Legislative Districts

> **This is the Louisville case for State House vacancies.**

The nominating committee shall consist of precinct committee members residing within the legislative district. Only those Democrats who are precinct committee members at the time the vacancy occurs shall be entitled to vote.

**i.** The nominating committee shall be chaired by the Legislative District Chair. In the LD Chair's absence, the nominating committee shall be chaired by the Legislative District Vice Chair. In the absence of both the LD Chair and Vice Chair, the nominating committee shall be chaired by the At-Large member residing in the legislative district with the longest tenure on the Executive Committee.

**ii.** Upon notice of the vacancy, the Legislative District Chair shall convene a meeting of the members of the County Executive Committee who reside in the Legislative District (Legislative District Chair, Vice Chair, At-Large(s) and any officer) to serve as an organizing committee to determine the date, time, and location of the nominating committee meeting and establish the nomination process.

**iii.** The LD Chair shall be responsible for notifying precinct committee members of the date, time, and location of the nominating committee meeting and the guidelines established for the nomination process.

**iv.** The vote of each precinct committee member attending the nominating committee shall be equal to the total number of registered Democrats in the precinct they represent.

**v.** In the event of a tie in the nominating committee vote, the tie shall be broken by the County Chair.

### c. Metro Council and other non entire-county Districts wholly within only one County containing seven or more Legislative Districts

The Nominating Committees of Metro Council and other non entire-county Districts that are not Congressional, State Senate, State House of Representatives, Magisterial, or Constabulary Districts comprised of precincts wholly within only one county containing seven (7) or more Legislative Districts shall be composed of the voting members of the County Executive Committee, plus the Legislative District Vice-Chair, who reside within the Legislative District(s) comprising the Metro Council or other non entire-county district.

**i.** The Vice-Chair shall have a vote on this Nominating Committee.

**ii.** All votes of the Nominating Committee members will be proportionate to the number of registered Democrats at the preceding General election that are both within the District and also within the Legislative District in which he/she resides.

**iii.** The total votes of the Legislative District shall be divided equally among the members of the Nominating Committee from such Legislative District.

**iv.** Only those Democrats who are above described party officials at the time the vacancy occurs shall be entitled to vote. Only those Democrats who are members of the County Executive Committee and registered to vote in the Legislative District(s) at the time the vacancy occurs shall be entitled to vote.

**v.** The Chair of the County Executive Committee shall be the chair of said Nominating Committee. If such Chair is absent or unable to act, then the Vice-Chair of the County Executive Committee shall be the Chair of said Nominating Committee. If such Vice-Chair is absent or unable to act, then the Chair of the Legislative District that cast the largest Democratic vote in precincts within the district at the preceding Presidential election shall act as Chair.

**vi.** Any presiding officer of said Nominating Committee shall not have a vote unless otherwise a voting member of the said Nominating Committee.

**vii.** In the event that a vote of the Nominating Committee under this Section results in a tie, the County Chair shall cast the deciding vote unless they were already a voting member of the Nominating Committee. In such event, the County Vice-Chair shall cast the deciding vote.

### d. State House of Representatives, State Senate and other Districts wholly within only one county containing six or less Legislative Districts

The Nominating Committees of State House of Representatives, State Senate, and other Districts comprised of precincts wholly within only one county containing six (6) or less Legislative Districts shall be composed of the voting members of the County Executive Committee who reside within such district.

**i.** If less than three (3) voting members reside within the district, then the Nominating Committee shall be composed of the Precinct Committee Members within such district. Only those Democrats who are above described party officials at the time the vacancy occurs shall be entitled to vote.

**ii.** The Chair of the County Executive Committee shall be the Chair of said Nominating Committee. If such Chair is absent or unable to act, then the Vice-Chair of the County Executive Committee shall be the Chair of said Nominating Committee.

**iii.** Any presiding officer of said Nominating Committee shall not have a vote unless they are otherwise a voting member of the said Nominating Committee.

## D. County Commissioners are considered to be County-wide officers

### a. Committee Nominations for Elective Office to be by Vote in Proportion to Democratic Registration

Whenever, pursuant to public or party laws a Nominating Committee shall be authorized to make any nomination for elective office or to fill any vacancy occurring in such nomination, each member of said Committee shall cast a vote, unless otherwise specified, proportionate to the number of registered Democrats at the preceding General election that are both within the district and also within the political subdivision that the member represents.

**EXAMPLES:**

- Precinct Committee Officers will cast a vote equal to the number of registered Democrats in their precinct divided by three so that the total votes to be cast will equal the number of registered Democrats in the precinct; or
- Legislative District Chairs will cast a vote equal to the number of registered Democrats in their Legislative District who reside within the district; or
- County Chairs will cast a vote equal to the number of registered Democrats in their county who reside within the district; or
- County Executive Committee Members entitled to vote will cast a vote equal to the number of registered Democrats in their county who reside within the district divided by the total number of voting members of the County Executive Committee who reside within the district at the time the vacancy occurs, so that the total votes to be cast will be equal to the number of registered Democrats in the county who reside within the district. For instance, if there are 20,000 registered Democratic voters in a county and district, and there are 20 members of a County Executive Committee who reside within the district, each County Executive Committee Member will have a weighted vote equal to 1000 (20,000 divided by 20).

## E. Statewide Nominating Committee

The Nominating Committee of Statewide Elected Offices shall be composed of the voting members of the SCEC. Only those Democrats who are SCEC voting members at the time the vacancy occurs shall be entitled to vote.

**a.** The Chair of the KDP shall be the Chair of said Nominating Committee. If the Chair is absent or unable to act, then the Vice Chair of the KDP shall act as chair.

**b.** The vote of each voting member of the SCEC shall be equal weighted.

---

## F. Nominating Committees to be Governing Authority

All Nominating Committees established under this Article V [sic — should be Article VI] shall be the governing authority to determine how all nominations within its district for special elections and selecting replacement candidates shall be made and to declare all nominees not inconsistent with public or party law.

**a.** All Nominating Committees shall have the discretion, by a majority vote, to include other Democratic Party Officials as voting members of the Nominating Committee. For example (and not by way of limitation), should the Nominating Committee be composed of the County Chairs or Legislative District Chairs, that Nominating Committee may, by a majority vote, include as voting members of the Nominating Committee those Democrats who are Precinct Committee Officers in the district at the time the vacancy occurs.
