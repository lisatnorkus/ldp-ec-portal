## Why you're reading this

Most of the current LDPEC was elected in the 2025 reorg cycle. For almost everyone, this is their first party officer role and their first time hearing the word "reorg." The next reorg is spring 2028, and it's the one this card prepares you for.

If you're an LD Chair, LD Vice Chair, At-Large Member, or Precinct Captain, the things that happen during reorg are things **you have to do**, not things that happen to you. Read this.

## What "Reorg" actually is

Every four years, in the spring of the presidential election year, the Kentucky Democratic Party rebuilds itself from the precinct level up. This is called a **quadrennial Reorganization** — "reorg" for short. Every elected party office — from Precinct Captain to KDP State Chair — has its term reset.

Terms run from one Reorganization Day to the next (LJCDP §4.5). The people currently holding LDPEC seats hold them **until** the next reorg seats new officers. There's no re-election in between.

### The 2024→2025 postponement (why this is confusing)

Normally reorg years are 2012, 2016, 2020, 2024, 2028, etc. — aligned with presidential elections.

**For the last cycle, KDP postponed reorg from 2024 to 2025.** That's why most of the current LDPEC was elected in June 2025, not spring 2024. The current term is effectively 3 years long (2025 → 2028), not 4.

The 2028 cycle is expected to run on the normal presidential-year schedule. ⚠️ *Lisa: confirm whether KDP has formally set 2028 as back-on-cycle, or whether there's any indication of another postponement.*

## The reorg chain, in order

Reorg is not one event. It's four events, in strict sequence. Every step feeds the next.

### Step 1 — Precinct Conventions (earliest)

**What happens:** Every precinct in Jefferson County holds a **Precinct Convention**. Registered Democrats in the precinct show up and elect three people: **one Precinct Captain Man, one Precinct Captain Woman, and one Precinct Captain Youth** (age 35 or under).

**Bylaws basis:** KDP Art. III.B (Precinct Committee). These three people are "the primary Party Officials responsible for organizing and building Democratic power within [the] precinct."

**Who votes:** Any registered Democrat in that precinct, at that precinct's convention.

**Who runs it:** ⚠️ *Lisa: confirm — is the Precinct Convention called and run by the LDPEC, by the LD Chair, or by the KDP itself? The state bylaws imply CEC involvement but don't explicitly name who chairs each precinct's meeting.*

**Practical note:** In a precinct with no active organizing, Precinct Conventions frequently attract almost no one — and the seats go empty. If that happens, the LDPEC has to fill the vacancy from above within 90 days (KDP Art. III.B, Filling Vacancies).

### Step 2 — Legislative District Conventions

**What happens:** Each of Jefferson County's 18 Legislative Districts holds an **LD Convention**. The newly-elected Precinct Captains from that LD gather and elect **two LD officers: an LD Chair and an LD Vice Chair, of opposite genders** (KDP Art. III.C).

**Who votes:** Only the duly elected Precinct Committee members from that LD may vote (KDP Art. II.D, Eligibility).

**What else the LD Convention does:**
- Elects delegates from the LD to the Congressional District Convention
- Elects delegates from the LD to the State Convention
- Conducts any other business SCEC assigns in the Call to Convention

**Who runs it:** The outgoing LD Chair calls, advertises, and chairs the Precinct Conventions and the LD Convention for their LD. If the outgoing Chair is absent, the outgoing LD Vice Chair runs it.

### Step 3 — Joint post-LD election of At-Large members and LDPEC officers

**This is the step most people don't know exists.**

**What happens:** "At close of the Legislative District Convention, newly elected LD Chairs and Vice Chairs shall meet to elect **eighteen (18) at-large members of the County Executive Committee** and Committee officers." (KDP Art. II.D, At-Large Members.)

In plain terms: as soon as the last LD Convention closes, all 36 newly-elected LD officers (18 Chairs + 18 Vice Chairs) convene together as one body. Together, they elect:

1. **The 18 At-Large members** of the LDPEC — serving the entire county, not any one LD.
2. **The County Chair** (LJCDP §10.1 — elected by the LD Chairs in accordance with state bylaws).
3. **The County Vice Chair**, of the opposite sex of the County Chair (LJCDP §10.2). The County VC must be a newly-elected LD Chair.

**When:** "Within 30 days of the Reorganization" per LJCDP §4.4. The state bylaws say "at close" of LD Convention — in practice this likely happens within a short window, not literally the same day.

### Step 4 — State Convention

**What happens:** Delegates elected by each LD Convention travel to the KDP State Convention. The state convention handles two separate tracks of business:

- **DNC delegate selection** — Kentucky's delegation to the Democratic National Convention is chosen.
- **SCEC elections** — the State Central Executive Committee is seated.

**Who votes at State Convention:** Elected delegates from LD Conventions statewide.

**What LD Chairs and VCs do there:** Vote in both tracks. Your SCEC seat is not elected at State Convention — **being elected LD Chair or LD Vice Chair automatically puts you on SCEC** for the term. But you vote for SCEC officers and for DNC delegates at the state convention.

## Who does what, in one table

| Reorg Event | Voter | Elects | Where | When |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Precinct Convention | Registered Dems in the precinct | PC Man, PC Woman, PC Youth | In or near the precinct | Earliest |
| LD Convention | Elected Precinct Committee members of that LD | LD Chair, LD VC, state convention delegates | Per-LD | After Precinct Conventions |
| Joint post-LD meeting | All newly-elected LD Chairs + LD VCs | 18 At-Large LDPEC members, County Chair, County VC | Countywide, at LDP HQ or similar | Within 30 days of LD Conventions |
| State Convention | LD-elected delegates | DNC delegates + SCEC officers | Statewide | Latest |

## What this means for your role

### If you're an LD Chair (2025–2028 term)

You are responsible for the Precinct Conventions in your LD AND for running your LD Convention in spring 2028. Successful reorg in your LD is the thing you'll be measured on in 2028 — more than anything else you do this term.

Start now: make sure every precinct in your LD has at least one person thinking about running for PC in 2028. If a precinct goes to convention with no candidates, its PC seats go empty and the LDPEC fills them from above — a worse outcome than community-elected PCs.

### If you're an LD Vice Chair (2025–2028 term)

You co-run your LD's Precinct Conventions and your LD Convention with the Chair. If the Chair is absent, you run them alone — you have the authority. Same responsibility as the Chair for making sure precincts have PC candidates in 2028.

### If you're an At-Large member (2025–2028 term)

Your seat does NOT continue automatically. To stay on the LDPEC after 2028, the incoming LD Chairs + VCs have to re-elect you at the joint post-LD meeting in spring 2028. If you want the seat again, the incoming class needs to know your name before that meeting.

### If you're a Precinct Captain (2025–2028 term)

You vote at your precinct's Precinct Convention in 2028. The next PC slate for 2028–2032 is chosen there. If no one runs in your precinct, the seats go empty. Recruit — including yourself, if you want to continue.

## Known bylaw issues flagged for amendment

These are in v2.2 one-pager notes as well, listed here for completeness:

1. **LJCDP §4.4 and §6.6** — both imply LD At-Large members are elected "by the LD Chairs" alone. State bylaws (KDP Art. II.D) require election by "LD Chairs AND Vice Chairs" together. State governs (LJCDP §4.1). The LJCDP sections need amendment.

2. **LJCDP §7.3** — describes LD VCs as "assist[ing] under the direction of the LD Chair." State bylaws describe the LD Committee as a co-equal 2-person Chair + VC unit. Not strictly inconsistent, but the LJCDP wording understates the VC role. Optional cleanup.

3. **LJCDP §8.1 and §9.1** — describe At-Large duties as "assist under the direction of the LD Chair." ⚠️ *Lisa: §8 is "Legislative District At-Large," §9 is "Non-Legislative District At-Large." Confirm — under the current 2025-reorg structure (18 At-Large elected countywide), which section applies to the current At-Large class, if either? This may be an artifact of a pre-2025 structure.*

4. **LJCDP §26** — lists 10 standing committees (Bylaws, Facilities, Finance, Events, Volunteering, Youth, Communication, Labor, Training, Candidate Recruitment). Practice runs 8 standing (Youth and Labor dropped) + 3 ad hoc (Branding, Endorsement Process, Platform). Name drift too: "Volunteering" → Volunteer; "Communication" → Communications. §26 needs amendment.
