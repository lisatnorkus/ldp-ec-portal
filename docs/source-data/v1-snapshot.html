Five things every Executive Committee owes its county.

Per the KDP County Executive Committee Guide
  1. Mobilize Democrats year-round. Identify and activate Democrats across all 629 precincts — not just in the four weeks before an election.
  2. Organize at the precinct level. Recruit precinct captains, build neighbor-to-neighbor relationships, and keep voter contact data current.
  3. Recruit and support candidates. Make sure every winnable race has a credible Democrat on the ballot, and back them from filing day to election night.
  4. Conduct official party business locally. Bylaws, governance, endorsements, financial compliance — the structural work that keeps the party legally and operationally sound.
  5. Partner with KDP. Uplift statewide candidates and issues, follow state party guidance, and feed local data and learnings back up the chain.

Everything on this page — every officer, committee, LD, and month-card — is how the LDP fulfills these five obligations. If an activity doesn't ladder up to one of them, it isn't EC work.

Beth runs a paid digital program every cycle: targeted Meta ads, boosted posts, candidate-aligned creative, GOTV reminders. Canvass Tools · How an LD Chair runs the work

Everything you need to put doors on the calendar.

Running a Legislative District is a year-round leadership role, not just election-season canvassing. The eight things below are the full scope of the job. The VoteBuilder playbook covers the canvass piece in depth, and the Volunteer Pipeline below shows you how to feed every contact you make into the countywide deployment system Jessica Haggy runs.

01

Fundraise & give

Every board member has a personal give/raise commitment. LDs are where the party's network of donors actually lives — your job is to activate them. See Board Commitments above.

02

Recruit precinct captains

Your LD has 25–49 precincts. Each one should have a captain. This is the biggest structural gap the LDP has — 0 of 629 are currently mapped. LD Chairs own the recruitment.

03

Run canvasses

Primary, general, off-year. This is the operations work the VoteBuilder playbook below covers in detail. Multiple canvasses per year, not just one.

04

Attend & report

Monthly Executive Committee meetings (or send a proxy). Quarterly LD reports to the Chair. File on time — attendance is how the party sees you're active.

05

Support endorsed candidates

Once LDP endorses someone in a race touching your LD, you're the party's face for that candidate in your district. Door knocks, events, networks.

06

Engage between elections

Don't go dark in off-years. New-mover welcomes, LD-level meet-and-greets, issue forums, community events. Organized presence builds the base that wins cycles.

07

Represent the party locally

You are the LDP in your LD. Community meetings, city council forums, neighborhood associations — if Democrats need a presence, that's you or your VC.

08

Feed the volunteer pipeline

Every new voter contact captures interest. Send volunteer leads to Jessica Haggy (Volunteer Chair) so they're onboarded within 7 days and matched to a committee.

The 2026 primary canvass playbook

Step-by-step for running your LD's primary canvass in VoteBuilder. The strategic plan and full docs are linked in the sidebar.

Your job, in eight steps

1

Verify VoteBuilder access today

Log in to votebuilder.com now — not the weekend of your canvass. If locked out, email the LDP Chair.

2

Find your saved list

My Folders → "26 LDP Primary Searches & Lists." Use the "26 LDP Primary — Voter Education List" (~48,825 voters). Filter by Precinct or State House District to isolate your LD.

3

Create your canvass program

Actions → Create Canvass Program. Name it "LD41 Voter Ed Walk 2026-05-03." Type = Walk. Attach the "26 LDP Pri — Voter Education" script (Spanish version available for Spanish-speaking precincts).

4

Cut turf by precinct

40–60 doors per packet, 2–3 hours of walking. Split oversized precincts using Map View. Never assign turf you haven't previewed on the map yourself.

5

Choose delivery

Strong preference: MiniVAN app (free, auto-syncs). Fallback: paper walk lists. For MiniVAN, set up volunteer accounts in Admin → Manage Users.

6

Deploy volunteers

Each volunteer gets: walk packet, Volunteer Canvass Guide, sample ballot, pen. Brief on the 6-question script. Recruit a floating troubleshooter.

7

Enter paper data within 48 hours

MyCampaign → Canvassing → Data Entry. Recruit a dedicated data-entry volunteer — don't let paper pile up.

8

Report weekly

Email the LDP Chair weekly with doors knocked, packets complete, volunteers deployed, blockers, and timeline. Flag any voter who said yes to volunteering to the Volunteer Committee Chair.

Volunteer Pipeline · Run by Jessica Haggy

Every door you knock generates a name. Get those names to Jessica within 7 days.

The Volunteer Committee is the LDP's countywide deployment system. Jessica intakes new volunteers, matches them to a committee or LD that fits, plugs them into the Facebook group and Mobilize, and routes them to the priority districts where countywide volunteer hours move the most votes — Metro Council 17 (Winkler), 21 (Ruhe), and 7 (McCraney). LD Chairs and Vice Chairs are the front door of this pipeline. If you don't pass a volunteer up, they vanish.

1

Capture at the door

Anyone who says yes to volunteer, yard sign, or events on the canvass script is a pipeline lead. Note name, contact, LD, what they said yes to, and whether they're Spanish-speaking.

2

Send to Jessica within 7 days

Email or text Jessica with the lead. Don't sit on it — week-old leads go cold. If you got a yes during a canvass, this should happen the same week as your data entry.

3

Jessica onboards + routes

Jessica adds them to the Facebook group, the Mobilize platform, and the right committee or LD activity stream. She'll loop you back in if the lead lives in your LD.

4

Volunteer gets deployed

Most go to the three priority Metro Council districts (17 / 21 / 7) where countywide volunteer time moves the most needles. Some stay in your LD if you're running canvasses they can join.

The Rock Star Playbook · Month by month

What your year looks like.

The roadmap below applies to every LD Chair, LD Vice Chair, and At-Large Chair. The work is the same — LD leaders run it in their district, At-Large leaders run it countywide across all eighteen LDs. If you're doing these things every month, you're a rock star. Monthly EC meeting is assumed throughout.

The Big Idea

Showing up consistently across the year is the single biggest thing you can do.

The party doesn't need heroics in November. It needs twelve months of steady work: precinct captains recruited, donors engaged, volunteers welcomed, voters listened to. Rock stars make the calendar their friend.
