## ⚠️ Reference-only for ldp-ec-portal

This document describes **Beth Thorpe's Communications Committee scope and roadmap**, including her separate plans for a Louisville Democratic Party **public website re-do** (e.g., work with Dixon, Campaign Deputy integration, Ballot Ready demos).

It is **NOT** a spec for the `ldp-ec-portal` project.

`ldp-ec-portal` is a separate, internal, EC-only tool being built outside the Comms Committee approval process. Beth has seen it and encouraged its completion, but it does not replace or depend on anything in this document.

**If any copy or feature in `ldp-ec-portal` starts to overlap with Beth's public-site or Comms infrastructure work, stop and flag to the maintainer** — coordination belongs out of band, not at the code level.

## Scope and inventory of previous work, plus ideas for future Comms Committee work

### Social Media

**Platforms:** Facebook, Instagram, Twitter, Bluesky, Threads

**Current functions:**

- Engagement and follower building
- Rapid response
- Graphics and video
- Live streams

**Would love to have:**

- Social media amplifier team — could be a good project for people who don't like to knock, etc. (better than postcards!)
- Social media moderators
- Developing FB's channel for rapid response — especially during the session in Frankfort

### Writing

- Press releases
- Emails to our list
- Fundraising
- Volunteer opportunities
- Advocacy

**Would love to have:**

- More article writing on various topics, could be multiple series. Examples: series on how to run for office, how to work in campaigns, deep dives with elected officials, or aligned organizations
- Toolkit for writing op-eds

### Messaging

- Donors
- Volunteers
- Voters
- Persuasion
- GOTV
- To disengaged likely Democratic voters

### Digital and Social Media Ads

- Registration, Persuasion, GOTV, and kicking republican candidates where it hurts

### Website Re-do

See Website notes of wants and needs. Possible outreach to Dixon, or others to get this done. There needs to be a higher level of skill than the norm for this website with the functionality and look desired.

### Photography

- Shoot photos for events, fundraisers, etc.
- Maintain archives of elected official photos taken — Governor Beshear, Lt. Governor, House reps, state senators, Metro Council members, along with individuals in the Kentucky Democratic ecosystem

### Emails

- Fundraising + information mass emails via Campaign Deputy
- Internal comms
- Tech and comms support to LD chairs, committee chairs and board

### Board Resource Page, files

- Calendaring
- Digital infrastructure

### Other ongoing work

- Maintain membership lists and contact info of electeds, candidates, etc.
- Google admin
- Security
- Mapping with ArcGIS
- Printed materials — booklets, invitations, flyers, etc.
- Responding to public emails and form submissions

### Merchandise

Goodstock — which ran a great online store platform for us in 2024 — has closed down until they can identify a union on-demand printer to work with them.

Beth has looked into **[Bright Blue Ink](https://www.brightblue.ink/)** — the standard Dem platform used by the Dem party:

- Start-up cost: $450
- Monthly: $40
- 8 initial items
- Beth on their contact list for when they have capacity to work with us (was told late August — they're swamped with both Goodstock and Merchblue closing down)
- On-demand catalog: `BBICatalog2024_web.pdf`

**Rejected:** Fii Marketing — too expensive on base monthly costs and individual set up of items regardless of sales.

### Data management for LDs, precinct people, committee members, donors

Partnering with Robert Kahne and Josh from Campaign Deputy.

- Process needed for LD Chairs to easily update precinct membership additions into Campaign Deputy
- Identify and manage all of our data needs over platforms. Some more complex integrations with Zapier might be able to remove a lot of manual work Beth has been doing
- Define what makes someone an active member in a committee and how they are identified in CD
- Historically, we have said anyone who filled out the form on our website is a volunteer, and sent them volunteer emails — but this data is significantly out of date and needs to be looked at
- Look-ups on our website:
  - See if there is an opening in a precinct and to apply directly
  - Look up your ballot with our endorsements
- Reached out to Ballot Ready for a demo (they probably have the most comprehensive local data — just acquired by CivicTech)
