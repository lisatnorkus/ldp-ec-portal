## 1. LD Chair

**Who you are:** The elected Chair of one of Jefferson County's 18 Legislative Districts. With your LD Vice Chair, you're a two-person LD Committee representing your state House District on the County Executive Committee. State bylaws (Art. III.C) say the two of you are responsible for organizing and building Democratic support in your LD.

### What this role actually does

- Votes on every CEC matter, not just LD-specific ones
- Runs organizing inside your LD — voter contact, volunteer recruitment, phonebanks, canvasses, LD meetings
- Works with the Precinct Captains in your LD. They're your field operation. If you ignore them, you don't have one.
- Fills PC vacancies in your LD when they come up (state bylaws require CEC to fill within 90 days)
- Elects delegates from your LD to Congressional District and State Conventions during reorg cycles
- Supports countywide CEC goals inside your LD

**Also: your SCEC seat.** Being elected LD Chair automatically puts you on the State Central Executive Committee — the statewide body that governs the KDP between state conventions. Everyone calls it SCEC. Meetings are quarterly in Frankfort, plus any called meetings to vote on something urgent. The vibe is more formal than CEC meetings. Your practice there is to show up, vote, and represent Jefferson County.

### If you haven't been doing it, start here

- Read the LJCDP bylaws and the KDP state bylaws. Your authority and your limits both live there.
- See Tour Step 3 for your LD — precinct list, who's active, who's dark.
- Meet with your LD VC. You're co-equal partners, not principal and deputy. Divide the work.
- Put one date on the calendar for your next LD meeting. Any date. LDs that don't meet don't organize.
- See Tour Step 5 for current LDPEC initiatives and pick the one your LD will plug into next.

### Your 2028 responsibility

You call, advertise, and chair your LD's Precinct Conventions in spring 2028. You run the LD Convention that elects your successor and your VC's successor. Right after the LD Convention closes, you join the incoming Chairs + VCs to jointly elect the 18 At-Large CEC members and county officers. At the state convention that follows, you vote in both tracks — DNC delegate selection AND SCEC elections. (See Tour Step 6: Reorg & Delegate Selection reference card.)

## 2. LD Vice Chair

**Who you are:** The elected Vice Chair of one of Jefferson County's 18 Legislative Districts, of the opposite gender from the LD Chair. Together you form a two-person LD Committee.

You're a full LD officer. You have your own CEC vote. You're ready to step up any time the Chair is absent, inactive, or needs backup. LJCDP §7.3 reads narrowly — "assist under the direction of the LD Chair" — but state bylaws and practice frame the LD Committee as co-equal. The LJCDP language is the floor. Practice is the ceiling.

### What this role actually does

- Carries LD work alongside the Chair — voter contact, recruitment, precinct organizing, LD meetings
- Votes on the CEC independently. You're not the Chair's proxy.
- Steps up to run the LD when the Chair is unavailable or inactive, without waiting for permission
- Is the second set of eyes on PC vacancies, precinct health, and candidate recruitment
- Supports countywide CEC goals inside the LD

**Also: your SCEC seat.** Being elected LD Vice Chair automatically puts you on the State Central Executive Committee — same body as your LD Chair. Quarterly meetings in Frankfort, plus called meetings to vote. More formal than CEC meetings. Your vote there is independent of your Chair's.

### If you haven't been doing it, start here

- Read the LJCDP bylaws and the KDP state bylaws. You're named in both.
- Talk to your LD Chair. Ask what they most want help on — or what they're not doing that you can pick up.
- Show up to the CEC. Your vote counts whether or not the Chair is there.
- If your LD Chair is inactive, don't sit on your hands. Start running the LD. You have the authority.
- See Tour Step 5 for current LDPEC initiatives and pick one to own inside your LD.

### Your 2028 responsibility

You co-run your LD's Precinct Conventions and LD Convention in spring 2028. If the Chair is absent, you run them alone. You vote jointly with incoming Chairs + VCs to elect the 18 At-Large CEC members and county officers right after the LD Convention closes. (See Tour Step 6: Reorg & Delegate Selection reference card.)

## 3. At-Large CEC Member

**Who you are:** One of 18 At-Large members of the Louisville-Jefferson County Democratic Executive Committee. You were elected jointly by the incoming LD Chairs and Vice Chairs immediately after the LD Convention closed — not by a single LD, and not by precinct men, women, or youth. Per state bylaws Art. II.D.c, the 18 of you serve the whole county.

The 2025 reorg cycle changed the election mechanism. LJCDP bylaws §4.4 and §6.6 still reflect the old process — those sections need amendment to match state bylaws.

### What this role actually does

- Votes on every CEC matter
- Thinks and acts countywide — you don't represent one LD
- Fills standing committee seats and special assignments. The 10 §26 committees rely on At-Large members showing up to do the work
- Brings a network or a skill the LD officers couldn't cover alone — that's why the At-Large slots exist
- Shows up. Quorum math depends on it.

### If you haven't been doing it, start here

- Read the LJCDP bylaws and KDP state bylaws. Art. II.D.c is where your seat is defined.
- Come to the next CEC meeting. If you've missed a few, reintroduce yourself.
- Pick a standing committee that matches what you have time and skill for. Ask the chair to bring you on.
- Find out which LD you live in, and reach out to the LD Chair + VC. You don't report to them — but you should know each other.
- See Tour Step 5 for current LDPEC initiatives and plug in.

### Your 2028 responsibility

Your At-Large seat resets at the 2028 reorg. You don't continue automatically — you have to be re-elected by the incoming LD Chairs + VCs at the joint post-LD meeting in spring 2028. If you want to stay on, make yourself valuable enough that the incoming class knows your name. (See Tour Step 6: Reorg & Delegate Selection reference card.)

## 4. Precinct Captain

**Who you are:** One of three elected officials in your precinct — two adults of different genders plus one youth (age 35 or under). State bylaws (Art. III.B) call the three of you the "primary Party Officials responsible for organizing and building Democratic power" inside your precinct. State bylaws call the role "Precinct Committee member." Jefferson County uses "Precinct Captain" — PC for short.

### What this role actually does

- Knows your precinct — who the Democrats are, who's active, who moved
- Runs voter contact inside the precinct: doors, phones, texts, lit when called for
- Recruits volunteers from your precinct for broader campaigns
- Hosts organizing events when asked — phonebanks, canvass launches, meet-and-greets
- Votes as a delegate at your LD Convention during reorg. That's how the next LD Chair and VC get elected.
- Keeps the precinct from going dark. If there's no active PC, nothing at the precinct level is happening.

### If you haven't been doing it, start here

- Read the LJCDP bylaws so you know the structure you're part of.
- Know who your LD Chair and VC are. If they haven't reached out, reach up to them.
- Ask your LD Chair or the Volunteering Committee for a VoteBuilder cut of your precinct's active Democrats.
- Pick one organizing activity this quarter and do it — a shift, a block, a drop. Don't wait to be asked.
- Show up to your next LD meeting.

### Your 2028 responsibility

You vote at your precinct's Precinct Convention in spring 2028. That's when the next PC slate (Man, Woman, Youth) is elected for the 2028–2032 term. If nobody runs in your precinct, it goes empty and the CEC has to fill it from above. Make sure candidates show up — including yourself, if you want to continue. (See Tour Step 6: Reorg & Delegate Selection reference card.)

## 5. Committee Chair + Committee Member

Covers both standing and ad hoc committees — same role structure, different lifespan. Chair and Member packaged together because they're tightly paired. Split into two cards in the build if that reads better. **(Decision resolved for build: render as ONE page with two clearly-separated sub-sections.)**

### Context (shared)

LJCDP runs 11 committees — **8 standing** and **3 ad hoc**:

**8 standing committees** (run continuously, meet on their own cadence):

- Bylaws
- Candidate Recruitment
- Communications
- Events (three subcommittees: Celebration of Democracy Dinner, Women Deliver Democracy, Dems at the Downs)
- Facilities
- Finance (also oversees fundraising; §26.2–26.3 give it specific bylaw-mandated duties on fiscal policy and purchase approvals)
- Training
- Volunteer

**3 ad hoc committees** (activate around a specific need, don't meet on a steady cadence):

- Branding
- Endorsement Process
- Platform

Per LJCDP §26.1.1, what each committee does is set by the County Chair and that committee's own Chair. Committee scope is owned by the chair.

**Who chairs / who serves:** Committee chairs have to be on the committee they chair — you can't chair from the outside. But not every CEC member has to sit on a committee. Committee service is voluntary: At-Large members often do, LD officers may or may not depending on bandwidth. Committees recruit members; members aren't auto-assigned.

**Bylaws flag:** §26 lists 10 standing committees (Bylaws, Facilities, Finance, Events, Volunteering, Youth, Communication, Labor, Training, Candidate Recruitment) — practice has dropped Youth and Labor, added three ad hoc (Branding, Endorsement Process, Platform), and name-drifted "Volunteering" → Volunteer and "Communication" → Communications. §26 needs an amendment to match what's actually running.

### If you're a Committee Chair

**Who you are:** The chair of one of the 11 LJCDP committees, appointed by the County Chair. You own what your committee does, how often it meets, who's on it, and what it delivers.

**What this role actually does:**

- Decides the committee's work — agenda, priorities, scope — in coordination with the County Chair
- Calls meetings and sets frequency. There's no meeting if you don't call one.
- Recruits and keeps committee members engaged
- Reports back to the full CEC on what the committee is doing
- Coordinates with other committee chairs so work doesn't double up
- Ad hoc chairs specifically: close the committee out when the task is done rather than letting it linger

**If you haven't been doing it, start here:**

- Read §26 in the LJCDP bylaws. If you chair Finance, also read §26.2 and §26.3 — those are your non-negotiables (fiscal policy + purchase approval chain).
- Pull your committee roster. Do you have members, or just a title?
- Decide the one thing your committee should ship this quarter. If you can't name it, neither can the committee.
- Schedule a meeting this month. Even thirty minutes.
- Bring a short report to the next CEC meeting. "We restarted" is a valid report.

**Your 2028 responsibility:** Committee chairs reset at reorg — the incoming County Chair appoints new ones. Committee WORK doesn't stop during reorg, though. Voter contact, fundraising, events, comms, training all have to keep moving through spring 2028. Document what your committee does so a successor could pick it up. If you want to continue chairing after 2028, make sure the incoming CEC knows the work. (See Tour Step 6: Reorg & Delegate Selection reference card.)

### If you're a Committee Member

**Who you are:** A member of one (or more) of the 11 LJCDP committees. You joined voluntarily — either you asked to be on it or the chair recruited you.

There are no formal bylaw-level requirements on committee members. You follow the chair's lead — show up when they call a meeting, take on what they ask you to take on. The committee is only as active as the chair decides to make it, and you contribute inside that scope.

**What this role actually looks like:**

- Show up to committee meetings when called
- Take on work the chair assigns
- Bring your network and your skills to what the committee is doing

**If you haven't been doing it, start here:**

- Read §26 in the LJCDP bylaws so you know which committee(s) you're on and what they're for.
- If you're unsure which committee you're on, ask the CEC Secretary.
- RSVP to the next meeting invite and actually be there.
- Ask the chair "what can I take on?" — if they don't have an answer ready, offer one.
- If you've been on the roster but haven't been active, just show up again. Don't make it awkward.

**Your 2028 responsibility:** Committee seats reset at reorg. If you want to continue on the committee for the 2028–2032 term, you'll need to be appointed again by the incoming CEC. Being active now is the best advocacy for being appointed later. (See Tour Step 6: Reorg & Delegate Selection reference card.)
