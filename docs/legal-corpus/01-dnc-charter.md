# DNC Charter & Bylaws — Index

National party law. Governs all state and local party law `[DNC Charter Art. II, §2]`. Where KDP or LJCDP conflicts with DNC, DNC governs.

**Verbatim source:** `/docs/bylaws/dnc-charter-bylaws-2022-full-text.md`

**Authoritative text:** *The Charter & The Bylaws of the Democratic Party of the United States, As Amended by The Democratic National Committee March 12, 2022.* Published by the DNC.

## Why JCDEC cares about the DNC Charter

Most JCDEC operations are governed by KDP and LJCDP bylaws, not directly by DNC. But the DNC Charter:

1. Sets the **non-discrimination floor** that KDP and LJCDP must meet `[DNC Charter Art. VIII]`
2. Governs **Delegate Selection** for the Democratic National Convention `[DNC Charter Art. II]`, `[DNC Bylaws Art. I]` — KDP must adopt a Delegate Selection Plan approved by DNC `[KDP Art. I.O]`
3. Establishes **National Committee structure** that includes state Chairs (and ex-officio state highest-ranking officers of another gender) `[DNC Charter Art. III, §2(a)]`
4. Provides the **Robert's Rules fallback** at the top of the hierarchy `[DNC Charter Art. IX, §14]`
5. Affirms **proxy is generally permitted in Democratic Party affairs** unless bylaws say otherwise — but **never at the National Convention** `[DNC Charter Art. IX, §11]`

For JCDEC-level reorg, endorsements, and operations, KDP and LJCDP are the operative documents. DNC governs only where stated.

## Document structure

The document has two parts:

```
CHARTER (Articles I–X + Preamble + Resolution of Adoption)
BYLAWS (Articles I–V) — adopted pursuant to the Charter
```

When citing, distinguish Charter from Bylaws.

## CHARTER — Key sections for JCDEC

### Non-discrimination floor `[DNC Charter Art. VIII]`

§2 prohibits discrimination on the basis of "sex, race, age (if of voting age), color, creed, national origin, religion, economic status, sexual orientation, gender identity, ethnic identity or disability."

KDP `[KDP Art. I.D]`, `[KDP Art. I.E]`, `[KDP Art. I.F]` mirror with slight variations. LJCDP is silent — relies on KDP/DNC.

§3 requires affirmative action programs "with particular concern for minority groups, Blacks, Native Americans, Asian/Pacifics, Hispanics, women and youth."

§6 — equal division of delegate and committee positions by gender does NOT constitute a quota violation.

### Equal gender division `[DNC Charter Art. IX, §16]`

DNC, Executive Committee, state central committees, and all national official Party Conventions, committees, commissions, and like bodies shall be as equally divided as practicable between men and women — variance cannot exceed one (1). State Parties take affirmative steps to bring law into compliance with this provision where it conflicts with state statutes. Non-binary delegates not counted as male or female; remainder equally divided.

JCDEC operational effect: drives the "different gender" requirement at every level — Precinct Committee `[KDP Art. II.C.a]`, LD Chair/VC `[KDP Art. II.D.a]`, County Chair/VC `[LJCDP §10.2]`, `[KDP Art. III.D.b.c.ii]`.

### Proxy voting `[DNC Charter Art. IX, §11]`

"Voting by proxy shall not be permitted at the National Convention. Voting by proxy shall otherwise be permitted in Democratic Party affairs only as provided in the Bylaws of the Democratic Party."

This is what allows KDP and LJCDP to permit proxies in CEC business. The DNC Bylaws further define proxy mechanics at the National Committee level `[DNC Bylaws Art. II, §8(g)]`.

### Open meetings, no secret ballots `[DNC Charter Art. IX, §12]`

"All meetings of the Democratic National Committee, the Executive Committee, and all other official Party committees, commissions and bodies shall be open to the public, and votes shall not be taken by secret ballot or use of the unit rule."

**Note:** This is a DNC-level rule about DNC meetings. State and local meetings are not bound to this — KDP allows secret ballots at Conventions when 2+ persons are nominated `[KDP Art. II.B.i.iii]` and LDP's endorsement process uses secret ballots via ElectionRunner. The DNC-level rule does not preempt those.

### Charter amendment `[DNC Charter Art. X, §1]`

Two paths to amend the Charter:
1. **National Convention path:** Majority of all delegates, then ratified by majority of full DNC membership
2. **DNC path:** 2/3 of entire DNC membership, with ≥30 days written notice and any proposed amendment provided to all DNC members and released to national news media

Also amendable by 2/3 of entire membership of any Democratic Party Conference.

### Bylaws amendment `[DNC Charter Art. X, §2]`

Bylaws may be adopted or amended by majority vote of:
1. National Convention; OR
2. DNC, provided ≥30 days written notice to all Committee members

## BYLAWS — Key sections for JCDEC

### Standing committees of the DNC `[DNC Bylaws Art. II, §10]`

Four standing committees:
1. **Credentials Committee** `[DNC Bylaws Art. II, §10(b)]` — receives challenges to DNC member credentials; 30-day window after selection to challenge by Registered Mail
2. **Resolutions Committee** `[DNC Bylaws Art. II, §10(c)]` — receives resolutions on Platform/principles; 21-day submission before DNC meeting; copies to members ≥14 days before meeting
3. **Rules and Bylaws Committee** `[DNC Bylaws Art. II, §10(d)]` — receives Charter/Bylaws amendments; Charter amendments must be submitted ≥60 days before regular DNC meeting; Bylaws amendments ≥30 days; conducts continuing study every four years post-presidential general
4. **Budget and Finance Committee** `[DNC Bylaws Art. II, §10(e)]` — Treasurer + National Finance Chair + ≤9 others with finance/management training; reviews DNC budget; periodic reports; ensures vendor diversity affirmative action and conflict-of-interest avoidance; oversees contracts/expenditures exceeding $100,000 annually

The DNC may create additional standing or ad hoc committees `[DNC Bylaws Art. II, §10(f)]`.

### DNC member attendance and quorum `[DNC Bylaws Art. II, §8]`

- 3 consecutive missed meetings = deemed resigned `[DNC Bylaws Art. II, §8(a)]`
- Quorum: 40% of full membership in person or by proxy, but at least 40% must be in person to establish quorum `[DNC Bylaws Art. II, §8(b)]`
- For National-ticket vacancy: quorum is majority of full membership in person `[DNC Bylaws Art. II, §8(b)]`
- 40% present in person/proxy OR 50 members in person (whichever fewer) for DNC standing committees and regional caucuses `[DNC Bylaws Art. II, §8(c)]`
- Up to 75 additional at-large DNC members and 11 at-large Executive Committee members may be elected by plurality `[DNC Bylaws Art. II, §8(d)(i)]`
- Roll call may be requested by 25% of those present and voting `[DNC Bylaws Art. II, §8(d)(ii)]`
- Each member entitled to one vote, except Democrats Abroad shares four votes among Chair, highest-ranking officer of another gender, 3 committeemen, 3 committeewomen `[DNC Bylaws Art. II, §8(e)]`
- Proxy voting permitted; proxies in writing; no DNC member may hold/exercise more than 1 proxy at a time; proxies may NOT be used to fill National ticket vacancy `[DNC Bylaws Art. II, §8(g)]`
- Chair may refer matters by mail; if 20% of full membership requests, must be presented at next meeting `[DNC Bylaws Art. II, §8(h)]`

### Regional Caucuses `[DNC Bylaws Art. II, §9]`

Four regions. **Kentucky is in the Southern region** along with AL, AR, FL, GA, LA, MS, NC, SC, TN, TX, VA, WV, plus Democrats Abroad (½ vote).

### Executive Committee composition `[DNC Bylaws Art. III, §2]`

Includes Chairs of Regional Caucuses, 4 members from each Regional Caucus (equally divided by gender), Chair/5 Vice Chairs/Treasurer/Secretary/National Finance Chair of DNC, DGA Chair or designee, Senate/House Leaders or designees, NCDMC Chair, DLCC Chair, NDMOC Chair, NDCO Chair, Y. Dems of America President, NFDW President, Hispanic/Black/AAPI/Native American/LGBTQ/Disability Caucus Chairs, Women's Caucus Chair, College Dems of America President, 4 additional ASDC members, 11 at-large members, and Chairs of standing committees.

### Removal and vacancies for DNC members `[DNC Bylaws Art. II, §§5–6]`

- A DNC member may resign by written notice to the Chair `[DNC Bylaws Art. II, §5(a)]`
- After notice + opportunity for public hearing, National Committee may remove a member by 2/3 vote `[DNC Bylaws Art. II, §5(b)]`
- Failure to affirmatively declare support for Democratic Presidential and Vice Presidential nominees within 30 days after National Convention adjournment = good and sufficient cause for removal `[DNC Bylaws Art. II, §5(c)]`
- State-apportioned vacancies filled by state/territorial Party in open meeting `[DNC Bylaws Art. II, §6(a)]`
- At-large vacancies filled by National Committee `[DNC Bylaws Art. II, §6(c)]`

## What this corpus does NOT pull from DNC

The full Delegate Selection Plan for Kentucky is approved separately under the rules of the Democratic National Convention Committee `[DNC Charter Art. II, §4]`, `[KDP Art. I.O]`, `[KDP Art. II.H]`. That plan is not in this corpus — it changes each cycle and lives with KDP staff. JCDEC has limited operational role in Delegate Selection beyond the Reorganization-flow elections that feed it.
