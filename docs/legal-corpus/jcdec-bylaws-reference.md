# JCDEC Bylaws Reference — Claude Code Skill

Reference markdown skill for Jefferson County Democratic Executive Committee (JCDEC / LDPEC) governance, structure, and procedural questions. Covers the three-tier party-law stack — DNC Charter & Bylaws, KDP Bylaws, LJCDP Bylaws — plus standing rules, Robert's Rules anchors, and the active drift between LJCDP and KDP that the Bylaws Committee needs to resolve.

This corpus is the companion to `jcdec-party-funding-laws.md`. That file covers what JCDEC can spend money on; this file covers how JCDEC is structured, elected, and governed.

## Citation format (enforced)

Every claim that turns on a day count, vote threshold, dollar amount, or quorum number must carry a citation. Use these exact forms:

| Source | Format | Example |
|---|---|---|
| DNC Charter | `[DNC Charter Art. X, §Y]` | `[DNC Charter Art. III, §5]` |
| DNC Bylaws | `[DNC Bylaws Art. X, §Y(z)]` | `[DNC Bylaws Art. II, §8(b)]` |
| KDP Bylaws | `[KDP Art. X.Y.z]` | `[KDP Art. II.B.f]` |
| LJCDP Bylaws | `[LJCDP §X.Y]` | `[LJCDP §22.1.1]` |
| Robert's Rules | `[Robert's Rules §X:Y]` | `[Robert's Rules §10:5]` |
| JCDEC standing rule | `[JCDEC Standing Rule, <date>]` | `[JCDEC Standing Rule, adopted 2025-09-11]` |

Inline use: "Filings open Oct 1 of the year prior `[LJCDP §4.2.1]`." Not: "Filings open about a year ahead."

## Two standing rules — do not violate

**1. Don't silently resolve drift.** Where LJCDP and KDP disagree, both must be surfaced. KDP is named as governing per `[LJCDP §5.3]` ("not inconsistent with those of the Kentucky and National Democratic Parties"). The drift must be flagged as a Bylaws Committee amendment need, not papered over. See `11-known-drift.md`.

**2. Don't paraphrase numbers.** Day counts, vote thresholds, dollar amounts, quorums — quote verbatim with citation. "Within thirty (30) days" stays as "within thirty (30) days `[LJCDP §21.1]`," not "about a month" or "promptly."

## Hierarchy rule

National party law governs state party law governs local party law:

```
DNC Charter & Bylaws         (governs all)
    │
    ▼
KDP Bylaws                   (must conform to DNC)
    │
    ▼
LJCDP Bylaws                 (must conform to DNC and KDP — per LJCDP §5.3)
    │
    ▼
JCDEC standing rules         (must conform to LJCDP, KDP, and DNC)
```

When a lower-tier document conflicts with a higher tier, the higher tier governs. Active conflicts between LJCDP and KDP are catalogued in `11-known-drift.md`.

### Procedural domains

- Reorganization (precinct → LD → county → state → CD → national cycle)

- Vacancies and succession across all four levels
- Committee structure (8 standing + 3 ad hoc, per JCDEC practice as of April 2026)
- Special-election nominating committees `[KDP Art. VI]`

## Maintenance notes

- **DNC Charter & Bylaws:** Reviewed every four years following each presidential general election by the DNC Rules and Bylaws Committee `[DNC Bylaws Art. II, §10(d)(v)]`. Watch for amendments after the next regularly scheduled DNC meeting cycle.
- **KDP Bylaws:** May be amended by the SCEC between State Conventions, but must be ratified at the next State Convention to remain in effect `[KDP Art. IV.A.b]`. All amendments require ≥30 days written notice to SCEC members `[KDP Art. IV.A.b]`.
- **LJCDP Bylaws:** May be amended at any meeting with quorum present, with either two weeks' written notice OR previous-meeting introduction `[LJCDP §29.1]`. Active drift between LJCDP and KDP (cataloged in `11-known-drift.md`) is the standing work for the Bylaws Committee.
- **JCDEC standing rules:** Adopted under §25 (new business) and persist until rescinded. Track in `04-jcdec-standing-rules.md`.

# Quick Reference — Most Common Lookups

**Notation in this file:** For at-a-glance table density, numerals appear as "30 days," "40%," "$500." ## Anti-discrimination — Party meetings and registration

All public meetings at every level of KDP are open to all members without discrimination based on age (if of voting age), gender identity, religion, economic status, sexual orientation, ethnic identity, or physical disability. `[KDP Art. I.E]`

KDP supports broadest possible registration without discrimination on the same grounds. `[KDP Art. I.D]`

## Democratic Primaries — endorsement prohibition

No Democratic Committee governed by KDP Bylaws, and no Democratic Party Officer acting in their official capacity, shall endorse or support one Democratic candidate over another in a Democratic Primary Election. `[KDP Art. I.I.a]`

No assets of the Democratic Party shall be used in a Dem Primary unless made equally available to all candidates. `[KDP Art. I.I.b]`

**Exception:** SCEC may, by 3/4 majority and secret ballot, endorse an incumbent Statewide KY Constitutional Officer for re-election and provide General-Election-level support during the primary. `[KDP Art. I.I.b.i]`

**Note:** This prohibition does NOT apply to nonpartisan races (Mayor + Metro Council per HB 388). LDP's 60% endorsement process for those races lives in Endorsement Process Committee policy, not in the bylaws. See `06-endorsement-process.md`.

# Known Drift Between LJCDP and KDP

Active conflicts and obsolete provisions in the LJCDP Bylaws. Documented but **not silently resolved.** This is the standing work for the JCDEC Bylaws Committee — each item below names what the LJCDP bylaw says, what KDP says (or what practice has become), which governs, and what amendment is owed.

**Standing rule for this corpus:** When LJCDP and KDP disagree, both must be surfaced. Per `[LJCDP §5.3]`, KDP governs. The drift is flagged for Bylaws Committee amendment, not papered over in copy or operational reference.

## Hierarchy anchor

`[LJCDP §5.3]`:

> "To make rules, by-laws, and regulations as it shall deem necessary to promote the progress of the Democratic party in Louisville and Jefferson County, **so long as such rules, by-laws, and regulations are not inconsistent with those of the Kentucky and National Democratic Parties.**"

When a conflict exists, KDP governs. The LJCDP text is the rule-on-the-books locally, but the operative rule is KDP.

`[DNC Charter Art. II, §2]` provides the parallel anchor at the next level up — state party rules apply unless in conflict with the DNC Charter; in such conflict, state Parties must take positive steps to bring state laws into conformity.

## Drift Item 1: At-Large model — LJCDP §§4.4, 6.6, 8 (pre-2025) vs KDP Art. II.D.c (post-2025)

### What LJCDP says (the pre-2025 LD-specific At-Large model)

**`[LJCDP §4.4]`:**
> "Legislative District Chairs and Legislative District Vice Chairs are elected on a date set by the State Central Executive Committee. **Legislative District At-Large members are elected by the Legislative District Chairs on a date set by the County Executive Committee, within thirty (30) days of the Reorganization.**"

**`[LJCDP §6.6]`:**
> "To elect Legislative District At-Large members, **one from each Legislative District wholly or partially within Jefferson County**, who are residents of the Legislative District in which they intend to serve, for a term of equal date and duration to that of Legislative District Chairs and Legislative District Vice-Chairs."

**`[LJCDP §6.6.1]`:**
> "Legislative District At-Large members shall be chosen from among those who have properly filed a Declaration and Notification within the filing period as established by these By-Laws."

**`[LJCDP §8]`** describes the LD-specific At-Large duties:
> "8.1 To assist under the direction of the Legislative District Chair in the affairs of the Democratic Party within their respective Legislative District."

### What KDP says (the post-2025 countywide At-Large model)

**`[KDP Art. II.D.c]`:**
> "**At close of the Legislative District Convention, newly elected LD Chairs and Vice Chairs shall meet to elect eighteen (18) at-large members of the County Executive Committee and Committee officers.**"

**`[KDP Art. III.D.b.c.v]`:**
> "The County Executive Committee so elected shall also elect **within thirty-five days, eighteen (18) additional members, two of whom shall be thirty-five years of age or under**, who shall serve as members of the Executive Committee for a four-year term, with the same powers and duties as members of the Executive Committee serving as Legislative District Chairs and Vice-Chairs and are subject to the same conditions of removal."

**`[KDP Art. III.D.b.c.v.1]`:**
> "The eighteen (18) additional members must be selected to reflect the proportion of registered Democrats in each Legislative District."

### What's drifted

| Issue | LJCDP says | KDP says | Status |
|---|---|---|---|
| Who elects At-Large? | Individual LD Chairs (each elects one for their LD) `[LJCDP §6.6]` | Joint vote of newly-elected LD Chairs + LD VCs `[KDP Art. II.D.c]` | **KDP governs.** Current 18 At-Large class elected per KDP model. |
| How many At-Large? | One per LD wholly or partially in Jefferson County `[LJCDP §6.6]` (= 18) | 18 additional members `[KDP Art. III.D.b.c.v]` (also 18) | Numerically aligned, but selection authority differs. |
| When elected? | Within 30 days of Reorganization `[LJCDP §4.4]` | At close of LD Convention `[KDP Art. II.D.c]` + within 35 days for the proportional 18 `[KDP Art. III.D.b.c.v]` | **KDP governs.** The "at close" + "within 35 days" framing accommodates the joint election followed by proportional selection. |
| Residency requirement? | Must reside in LD they intend to serve `[LJCDP §6.6]` | No LD-specific residency for countywide At-Large; must be registered to vote in county `[KDP Art. III.D.b.iv]` | **KDP governs.** Current 18 At-Large are countywide, not LD-tied. |
| Duties? | Assist LD Chair within LD `[LJCDP §8.1]` | Same powers and duties as LD Chairs/VCs serving on CEC `[KDP Art. III.D.b.c.v]`; countywide service per practice | **KDP governs.** Current 18 At-Large operate countywide, closer to `[LJCDP §9.x]` "non-LD At-Large" duties. |

### Practical effect

The 18 At-Large Chairs elected in June 2025 operate under the KDP model:
- Elected jointly by newly-elected LD Chairs + LD VCs at close of LD Convention
- Serve countywide (not LD-tied)
- Have same powers/duties as LD Chair/VC CEC members
- Have voting rights on CEC `[LJCDP §3.1]` (the §3.1 voting rights list does include "a number of At-Large members equal to the number of State Legislative Districts" — that count maps to the current 18; the model differs but the voting status carries through)

### Amendment owed to Bylaws Committee

1. **Repeal or amend `[LJCDP §4.4]`** to align with `[KDP Art. II.D.c]` — change "within thirty (30) days" to "at the close of the Legislative District Convention" with a 35-day window for the proportional 18 per KDP.
2. **Repeal `[LJCDP §6.6]`** — the LD-specific At-Large election by LD Chairs is no longer the operative model. Could be repealed entirely, with the At-Large election moved to a new section reflecting the post-2025 KDP model.
3. **Amend `[LJCDP §8]`** — the LD-specific At-Large duties section. Either repeal (if no LD-specific At-Large class remains) or amend to apply only to a hypothetical future LD-specific At-Large class (preserving the structure for any future bylaw change).
4. **Update `[LJCDP §6.6.1]`** — the filing-period reference would need to be updated to reflect that the joint LD Chair+VC election doesn't require pre-filing under the same rules.

**Recommended approach:** Bring §§4.4, 6.6, 6.6.1, and 8 into one amendment package, with §9 retained as the duties anchor for the current 18 countywide At-Large class (which it accurately describes).

6. Finance

8. Volunteer (renamed from "Volunteering")

**3 ad hoc:**

2. Endorsement Process

### What's drifted

| Drift point | Status |
|---|---|
| §26.1 lists "Volunteering"; practice runs "Volunteer" | Naming drift — amend to current name |
| §26.1 lists "Communication"; practice runs "Communications" | Naming drift — amend to current name |

### Practical effect

JCDEC operates under the 8 + 3 model. The §26.1 list reflects neither the names nor the slate accurately. Operationally, the County Chair appoints all committee chairs per `[LJCDP §11.6]` regardless of standing-vs-ad-hoc status, so the operational impact is limited — but the rules-on-paper don't match the actual committee structure.

### Amendment owed to Bylaws Committee

**Amend `[LJCDP §26.1]`** to reflect current practice. Two options for the Bylaws Committee to consider:

**Option A — Reflect current practice:**
> "There shall be Standing Committees which shall be Bylaws, Branding, Candidate Recruitment, Communications, Endorsement Process, Events, Facilities, Finance (which will oversee fundraising), Platform, Training, and Volunteer."

(That promotes the 3 current ad hoc committees to standing, giving them bylaw-anchored permanence.)

Ad Hoc Committees may be created by the County Chair pursuant to §11.6 as needed; current Ad Hoc Committees include Branding, Endorsement Process, and Platform."

(That formalizes the 8 standing and recognizes the 3 ad hoc within the bylaws structure.)

Either path resolves the drift. Option B preserves the County Chair's flexibility to spin up additional ad hoc committees without requiring bylaw amendment.

## Drift Item 3: Young Dems / Women's Club proxy assignment — LJCDP §23.2 vs KDP Art. III.A.a.ii

### What LJCDP says

**`[LJCDP §23.2]`:**
> "**No other member of the County Executive Committee may assign their proxy, including the President of the Jefferson County Young Democrats Club and the representative of the Jefferson County Democratic Women's Club.**"

### What KDP says

**`[KDP Art. III.A.a.ii]`:**
> "If the president of a state or local Young Democrats or Woman's Club chapter is absent from an Executive Committee meeting, **their proxy may be assigned by the president, with proper notice to the committee Secretary, to an officer of the chapter.**"

**`[KDP Art. III.A.a.iii]`:**
> "If the president of a state or local Young Democrats or Woman's Club chapter holds a voting position on the Executive Committee in their own right, then **a permanent proxy may be granted to the chapter officer whose role it is incumbent upon to exercise authority in the absence of the chapter president per the chapter's governing documents.**"

### What's drifted

KDP allows the chapter president to assign a proxy to a chapter officer with notice to the Secretary, and even allows a permanent proxy in some cases. LJCDP prohibits the chapter president from assigning a proxy at all.

### Hierarchy analysis

This is the trickier case. Two possible readings:

**Reading 1 — LJCDP is "more restrictive but not inconsistent."** Local rules can be stricter than state rules without conflict. LJCDP §5.3 says local rules may not be "inconsistent" with KDP — and a stricter rule that bars something KDP permits could be argued as restricting, not contradicting. Under this reading, **LJCDP governs locally** and the chapter presidents simply cannot assign a proxy in JCDEC meetings.

**Reading 2 — LJCDP is inconsistent with KDP.** KDP affirmatively grants the chapter president a proxy-assignment right. A local rule that strips that right is inconsistent. Under this reading, **KDP governs** and the chapter presidents can assign a proxy to a chapter officer with notice to the Secretary.

### Status

**Surfaced for Bylaws Committee determination.** The reading is unsettled in this corpus. Pending Bylaws Committee guidance, operational practice should follow the stricter reading (LJCDP §23.2) because:
1. The chapter presidents are voting CEC members in their own right per `[LJCDP §3.1]`
2. The stricter rule (no proxy) is administratively simpler
3. The LJCDP text is the explicit JCDEC rule
4. If KDP-permitted, the chapter has the option to send the president in person rather than rely on proxy

### Amendment owed to Bylaws Committee

Resolve the reading and amend `[LJCDP §23.2]` either:
- To clarify that the stricter LJCDP rule stands as a more-restrictive-but-not-inconsistent local rule, OR
- To align with KDP §III.A.a.ii by allowing proxy assignment to a chapter officer with notice to the Secretary.

## Drift Item 6: Possible County Chair removal threshold — LJCDP §16.1 vs KDP Art. III.D.f.i

### What LJCDP says

**`[LJCDP §16.1]`:**
> "The County Chair may be removed at any time during her/his term without cause by **a majority vote of the voting members of the County Executive Committee.**"

### What KDP says

**`[KDP Art. III.D.f.i]`:**
> "The Chair of the County Executive Committee may be removed without cause **by a majority vote of the entire Executive Committee.**"

### What's drifted

The texts are close but differ subtly:
- LJCDP says "majority of voting members" — ambiguous between majority of all voting members vs majority of those present and voting
- KDP says "majority of the entire Executive Committee" — clearly majority of all voting members

### Hierarchy analysis

KDP's stricter reading (majority of all voting members) governs. The vote requires more than just majority of those present at a meeting — it requires affirmative votes equal to majority of the entire voting CEC.

**Operationally:** Combined with `[LJCDP §22.1.1]` (proxies don't count for dismissal), the meeting must have 40% in person to be a valid removal vote AND the vote count must reach majority of all voting CEC members.

For a CEC with ~56 voting members, the threshold is at minimum 29 affirmative votes (more than half of 56) — and the meeting must include 40% in person of LD Chairs + LD VCs + LD At-Large.

### Status

**KDP governs.** The LJCDP wording is ambiguous; KDP clarifies. Practice should follow the KDP-strict reading.

### Amendment owed to Bylaws Committee

**Amend `[LJCDP §16.1]`** to clarify: "by a majority vote of the **entire** voting membership of the County Executive Committee." Same amendment for `[LJCDP §18.1]` (County VC removal).

## Drift Item 7: KDP "forty (40) thirty-five or under" age requirement

### What KDP says

**`[KDP Art. III.D.b.c.iii]`:**
> "No more than one-half of the membership of the County Executive Committee may be of the same gender. The County Executive Committee must also include **one person age forty (40) thirty-five or under**."

### What's drifted

The KDP text appears to contain a typo or unresolved amendment artifact — "age forty (40) thirty-five or under" is internally inconsistent. The intended threshold is unclear: 40 or 35?

Parallel provision `[KDP Art. III.D.b.c.v]` specifies "two of whom shall be thirty-five years of age or under" for the 18 At-Large — so the youth age threshold at the At-Large level is clearly 35.

`[KDP Art. I.B.n]`: "Youth shall refer to any registered Democrat under the age of 40." So general "youth" definition is under 40.

### Status

**Unresolved KDP-internal drift.** Not LJCDP drift; this is a KDP text issue.

For operational JCDEC purposes, the conservative reading is **35 or under** (the stricter standard), which would meet both 35 and 40 thresholds. Including a youth member of any age under 35 satisfies both.

### Amendment owed (not to JCDEC Bylaws Committee, but flagged for SCEC awareness)

This is a KDP-side cleanup issue. Could be raised through the SCEC By-Laws Committee per `[KDP Art. IV.O]`.

## Drift Item 8: §26.1 doesn't include the Endorsement Process as standing

### What LJCDP says

`[LJCDP §26.1]` does not list Endorsement Process Committee.

### What practice does

Endorsement Process operates as an **ad hoc committee** per current JCDEC practice. The committee:
- Owns the LDP endorsement process for nonpartisan Mayor + Metro Council races
- Adopts the 60% threshold, secret ballot via ElectionRunner, no proxies during endorsement
- Operates a January–February timeline in election years

### Status

**Not technically drift** — LJCDP §11.6 explicitly grants the County Chair authority to appoint ad hoc committees. The Endorsement Process Committee is properly constituted under that authority.

**BUT** — the endorsement process is a recurring, structurally important LDP function. Treating it as ad hoc means the policy framework (60%, ElectionRunner, no proxies) lives in committee/CEC policy rather than in the bylaws. If the County Chair declined to appoint an Endorsement Process Committee, the LDP endorsement function would lose its institutional anchor.

### Bylaws Committee should consider

Promoting Endorsement Process from ad hoc to standing, OR formally enshrining the 60% threshold and process in the bylaws as a standalone section. This would make the endorsement framework less dependent on County Chair discretion.

(This is more of a "should we change the structure?" question than a drift-correction. Bylaws Committee judgment call.)

# Decision Trees

Procedural flows for recurring questions. Each tree starts with a question, walks through the bylaws-driven branches, and ends with the operational answer + citation.

## 1. "Is this proxy valid?"

```
START
  │
  ▼
Is the meeting taking up the election or dismissal of a Party officer?
  ├── YES → INVALID. No proxies allowed for election/dismissal.
  │       [LJCDP §23.1], [KDP Art. III.A.a]
  │
  └── NO ▼
       │
       Who is assigning the proxy?
       │
       ├── LD Chair, LD Vice Chair, or LD At-Large member
       │   ▼
       │   Is the proxy in writing or electronic, specifying:
       │     (a) the specific meeting, AND
       │     (b) the specific CEC member it's assigned to?
       │   ├── NO → INVALID. [LJCDP §23.2]
       │   └── YES ▼
       │        Is the proxy assignee a member of the CEC?
       │        ├── NO → INVALID. [LJCDP §23.2]
       │        └── YES ▼
       │             Is the proxy assignee present at LJCDP HQ
       │             by the start of the meeting?
       │             ├── NO → INVALID. [LJCDP §23.3]
       │             └── YES → VALID for general business.
       │                       (May also be assigned for limited purpose
       │                        during specific meeting per [LJCDP §23.2.1])
       │
       ├── Young Democrats Club President or Women's Club President
       │   ▼
       │   LJCDP §23.2: "No other member of the County Executive Committee
       │   may assign their proxy, including the President of the Jefferson
       │   County Young Democrats Club and the representative of the
       │   Jefferson County Democratic Women's Club."
       │   →  INVALID under LJCDP.
       │   (DRIFT: [KDP Art. III.A.a.ii] would allow assignment to chapter
       │   officer with notice to Secretary. LJCDP is stricter.)
       │
       └── County Chair, County VC, Secretary, Treasurer, or any other
           non-LD-officer member
           → INVALID. [LJCDP §23.2] permits proxy only for LD Chair, LD VC,
           and LD At-Large.
```

## 2. "How do we fill this vacancy?"

```
START — vacancy exists.
  │
  ▼
What seat?
  │
  ├── County Chair, County VC, LD Chair, LD VC, or At-Large member
  │   ▼
  │   Schedule CEC meeting within 30 days [LJCDP §21.1].
  │   ├── Quorum: 40% of LD Chairs + LD VCs + LD At-Large present IN PERSON
  │   │   (proxies don't count for officer election) [LJCDP §22.1.1]
  │   ├── Vote: majority of voting CEC members present and voting [LJCDP §21.1]
  │   │   (no proxies for the vote itself) [LJCDP §23.1]
  │   ├── If County Chair vacancy + successor is different sex from incumbent
  │   │   County VC → VC seat automatically vacates [LJCDP §19.1]
  │   ├── If County VC vacancy → successor must be a current LD Chair of
  │   │   different sex than County Chair [LJCDP §10.2]
  │   └── If CEC misses 90-day envelope → SCEC Chair fills
  │       [KDP Art. III.D.h.i]
  │
  ├── Precinct Committee member
  │   ▼
  │   LD Chair notifies SCEC of vacancy [KDP Art. III.B.c].
  │   ├── CEC has 90 days from notification to fill
  │   └── If unfilled → SCEC Chair fills [KDP Art. III.B.c]
  │
  ├── Redistricting vacancy (new LD maps drawn — automatic July 1)
  │   ▼
  │   CEC has 60 days to fill [KDP Art. III.D.i.i]
  │   ├── Quorum: 50% of remaining non-vacated positions [KDP Art. III.D.i.ii]
  │   ├── Special preservation rule: County Chair retains seat even if their
  │   │   simultaneously-held LD position is vacated [KDP Art. III.D.i.iii]
  │   ├── County VC: if their LD Chair position is vacated, County VC seat
  │   │   also vacates; new VC elected from LD Chairs of different sex than
  │   │   County Chair [KDP Art. III.D.i.iv]
  │   └── If unfilled → SCEC Chair fills [KDP Art. III.D.i.i]
  │
  └── SCEC seat
       ▼
       See 02-kdp-bylaws.md → Article IV.H process at the state level.
       Not a CEC operational concern; involved only insofar as State
       Delegates from JeffCo contribute to SCEC composition.
```

## 3. "Can we adopt new business at the same meeting it's introduced?"

```
START — Motion is on the floor introducing new business.
  │
  ▼
Has the new business already been introduced at a previous CEC meeting?
  ├── YES → Adopt under simple majority vote [LJCDP §25.1] (regular process)
  └── NO ▼
       │
       Is this a new-business motion AT THE SAME MEETING it was introduced?
       └── YES ▼
            │
            Special threshold required [LJCDP §25.2]:
            "approved by as many voting members as would represent two-thirds
             (2/3) of the Legislative Districts wholly or partially within
             Jefferson County"
            │
            ▼
            Count LDs represented in present-and-voting members.
            (Voting members may be LD Chairs, LD VCs, LD At-Large, or any
             combination — per [LJCDP §25.2.1])
            │
            ▼
            Are at least 2/3 of JeffCo's LDs represented?
            (JeffCo has 18 LDs → 2/3 = 12 LDs minimum)
            │
            ├── YES → Adopt at same meeting with simple majority of those
            │         present and voting [LJCDP §25.2]
            │
            └── NO → CANNOT adopt at same meeting.
                     Refer to next meeting per [LJCDP §25.1].
```

**Practical takeaway:** Adopting new business at the same meeting requires 12 of 18 LDs to be represented in the voting members present. This is a deliberate "speed bump" — same-meeting adoption is reserved for matters with broad LD representation.

## 4. "Is this convention notice valid?"

```
START — Notice has been issued for a Precinct, LD, or County Convention.
  │
  ▼
Is the notice given between 7 and 30 days before the Convention?
  ├── NO → INVALID. Must be ≥7 days and ≤30 days before [KDP Art. II.B.f].
  │       (Note: emergency waiver available per [KDP Art. II.B.e.i] for
  │        acts of God, declared emergencies, or conflicting major events.)
  └── YES ▼
       │
       Are at least 3 of the 6 advertisement methods used?
       Methods [KDP Art. II.B.f.i] through [KDP Art. II.B.f.vi]:
         (i)   Local newspapers (PSA or news item)
         (ii)  Local radio/TV (PSA or news item)
         (iii) County party social media
         (iv)  County party website
         (v)   Mobilize / Facebook Events / web event builders
         (vi)  Email to CEC + county Dem email list, OR US Mail to CEC,
               OR phone calls to CEC
       │
       ├── NO → INVALID. Must use ≥3 of 6.
       └── YES ▼
            │
            Did the CEC Chair AND the SCEC Chair both publicize widely?
            ├── NO → Notice is procedurally incomplete; may be challenged
            │       on appeal per [KDP Art. V.A] (3 days to file)
            └── YES → VALID notice. Convention may proceed.
```

## 5. "Should this CEC vote pass?"

```
START — CEC is taking a vote.
  │
  ▼
What is the vote about?
  │
  ├── Bylaws amendment ──────────────────────────────────────────────
  │   Required: majority at meeting with quorum present
  │   AND either:
  │     (a) ≥2 weeks written notice to all CEC members, OR
  │     (b) action introduced at previous CEC meeting
  │   Cite: [LJCDP §29.1]
  │
  ├── New business adopted at same meeting it's introduced ─────────
  │   Required: 2/3 of JeffCo LDs represented in voting members present
  │   Cite: [LJCDP §25.2]
  │   See tree #3 above for details.
  │
  ├── Removal of County Chair (without cause) ──────────────────────
  │   Required: majority of voting CEC members
  │   No proxies count for this vote [LJCDP §22.1.1], [LJCDP §23.1]
  │   Cite: [LJCDP §16.1] (LJCDP), [KDP Art. III.D.f.i] (KDP)
  │   Quorum: 40% in person of LD Chairs + LD VCs + LD At-Large
  │
  ├── Removal of County VC (without cause) ─────────────────────────
  │   Same as Chair removal.
  │   Cite: [LJCDP §18.1]
  │
  ├── Removal of any Party Officer (for failure to support or disavowal)
  │   Required: majority of voting CEC, after hearing
  │   Cite: [LJCDP §15.1], [LJCDP §15.2], [KDP Art. I.K.f], [KDP Art. I.K.g]
  │   Note: removal for non-support requires findings on BOTH:
  │     (1) Did officer fail to support? AND
  │     (2) Was failure inappropriate?
  │   Per [KDP Art. I.K.f].
  │
  ├── Filling officer vacancy ─────────────────────────────────────
  │   Required: majority of voting CEC, within 30 days
  │   No proxies for the vote
  │   Cite: [LJCDP §21.1]
  │   See tree #2 above.
  │
  ├── Sanctioning a new Women's or Young Dems Club ─────────────────
  │   Required: majority of voting CEC
  │   Vote occurs 6 months after application
  │   Cite: [LJCDP §3.4]
  │
  ├── Designating honorary titles ─────────────────────────────────
  │   Required: majority of voting CEC
  │   Cite: [LJCDP §11.8]
  │
  ├── Expansion of CEC membership (counties ≤6 LDs only — not JeffCo)
  │   Required: 75% of full Executive Committee
  │   Cite: [KDP Art. III.D.b.vi.2]
  │
  ├── Electronic voting (email or other) ─────────────────────────
  │   Permitted per [LJCDP §27.1]
  │   Threshold per the underlying matter being voted on
  │
  ├── Endorsement of a Mayor or Metro Council candidate (nonpartisan)
  │   Required: 60% of votes cast, secret ballot via ElectionRunner,
  │             no proxies
  │   Cite: [LDP Endorsement Process Policy] (NOT in bylaws)
  │   See `06-endorsement-process.md`
  │
  ├── Tie vote on any matter ──────────────────────────────────────
  │   Broken by vote of State Central Committee [LJCDP §24.1]
  │
  └── Anything else covered by Robert's Rules ─────────────────────
      Apply per [LJCDP §28.1], [KDP Art. I.Q]
      Standard parliamentary motion procedure applies.
```

```
Has the County Chair held at least one CEC meeting per quarter?
(Quarters: Q1 = Jan-Mar, Q2 = Apr-Jun, Q3 = Jul-Sep, Q4 = Oct-Dec)
(Per [KDP Art. III.D.e.i.1.c] quarterly meetings shall occur during
 February, May, August, and November)
  │
  ├── NO → Office DECLARED VACANT [LJCDP §17.1]
  │       (No CEC vote required to accept — automatic mechanic.)
  │       Proceed to vacancy fill per tree #2.
  │
  └── YES ▼
       │
       Has the County Chair missed 2 successive CEC meetings
       (in person or by written proxy)?
       ├── YES → Office DECLARED VACANT [LJCDP §20.1], [KDP Art. III.D.g]
       └── NO ▼
            │
            Has the County Chair missed 50% of annual CEC meetings
            in person?
            ├── YES → Office DECLARED VACANT [LJCDP §20.1], [KDP Art. III.D.g]
            └── NO ▼
                 │
                 Has the CEC voted majority to remove for:
                   (a) Failure to support Dem nominees, after hearing
                       [LJCDP §15.1]?
                   (b) Disavowal of allegiance, after hearing
                       [LJCDP §15.2]?
                   (c) Without cause [LJCDP §16.1]?
                   (d) KDP confidentiality breach [KDP Art. I.K.a]?
                   (e) KDP intentional bylaw non-compliance [KDP Art. I.K.b]?
                 ├── YES → Office VACANT after the vote.
                 │       Proceed to vacancy fill per tree #2.
                 └── NO → Office not vacant. Status quo continues.
```

## 7. "Whose Nominating Committee fills this seat?" (special elections)

```
START — A Dem-held state-level seat has been vacated mid-term, and the
seat needs a Democratic nominee for the special election.
  │
  ▼
What office?
  │
  ├── US House (Congressional) — multi-county district
  │   → Nominating Committee = Dem County Chairs of each county in district
  │   → Chair = County Chair with largest Dem vote at preceding Presidential
  │   → Vote weighting: per [KDP Art. VI.D]
  │   → Cite: [KDP Art. VI.A]
  │
  ├── US House (Congressional) — single county, county has 7+ LDs
  │   (e.g. KY-3 entirely in JeffCo)
  │   → Nominating Committee = Chairs of every LD wholly or partially in
  │     district. Only LD Chairs vote.
  │   → Chair = County Chair (or VC; or senior LD Chair of largest Dem vote)
  │   → Cite: [KDP Art. VI.C.a]
  │
  ├── State Senate — multi-county
  │   → Nominating Committee = voting EC members of each county residing
  │     in district (or if <2 reside: PC members)
  │   → Chair = County Chair of largest Dem vote at preceding Presidential
  │   → Cite: [KDP Art. VI.B]
  │
  ├── State Senate — single county with 7+ LDs (e.g. JeffCo SD-26)
  │   → Same as multi-county Congressional in JeffCo: LD Chairs vote.
  │   → Chair = County Chair (or VC)
  │   → Cite: [KDP Art. VI.C.a]
  │
  ├── State House — multi-county
  │   → Voting EC members residing in district; PC members backup if <2
  │   → Chair = County Chair of largest Dem vote
  │   → Cite: [KDP Art. VI.B]
  │
  ├── State House — single LD wholly in one county with 7+ LDs
  │   (e.g. any HD wholly in JeffCo)
  │   → Nominating Committee = Precinct Committee members residing in LD.
  │     Only PC members vote.
  │   → Each PC member's vote = total registered Dems in their precinct
  │   → Chair = LD Chair (or VC; or senior At-Large residing in LD)
  │   → LD Chair convenes organizing committee of CEC members residing in
  │     LD to set date/time/location/process [KDP Art. VI.C.b.ii]
  │   → LD Chair notifies PC members [KDP Art. VI.C.b.iii]
  │   → Tie broken by County Chair [KDP Art. VI.C.b.v]
  │   → Cite: [KDP Art. VI.C.b]
  │
  ├── Metro Council — single LD or multi-LD within single county (JeffCo)
  │   → Nominating Committee = voting CEC members + LD VC residing within
  │     LDs comprising the MC district. (LD VC has a vote here.)
  │   → Vote weighting: proportional to Dem registration at preceding General
  │     within district AND within voter's LD
  │   → Chair = County Chair (or VC; or LD Chair of largest Dem vote)
  │   → Cite: [KDP Art. VI.C.c]
  │
  ├── State House/Senate — single county with ≤6 LDs
  │   → Voting CEC members residing in district (or if <3 reside: PC members)
  │   → Chair = County Chair (or VC)
  │   → Cite: [KDP Art. VI.C.d]
  │
  ├── County Commissioners (county-wide officers)
  │   → Vote weighting per registration formula at [KDP Art. VI.D]
  │
  └── Statewide elected office (Governor, Lt. Gov, Atty Gen, etc.)
       → Nominating Committee = voting members of SCEC at time of vacancy
       → Chair = KDP Chair (or VC)
       → Equal-weighted vote
       → Cite: [KDP Art. VI.E]
```

## 8. "Can the CEC waive a bylaw requirement?"

```
START — CEC wants to deviate from a bylaw or convention requirement.
  │
  ▼
What kind of waiver?
  │
  ├── Convention date/time waiver
  │   → CEC may petition SCEC Chair for extraordinary circumstances:
  │     (a) Act of God (tornado, flood, earthquake)
  │     (b) Officially declared state of emergency
  │     (c) Conflicting countrywide major event (no CEC control)
  │     (d) Any other event SCEC Chair agrees is extraordinary
  │   → Submit ≥15 days before SCEC-approved convention schedule
  │     (emergency waivers via phone/text/email ≥1 hour before)
  │   → SCEC Chair responds:
  │       - Foreseen: within 7 days
  │       - Unforeseen (<30 days before): within 24 hours
  │   → Cite: [KDP Art. II.B.e.i], [KDP Art. V.G]
  │
  ├── CEC rules waiver (deviation from county-level rules)
  │   → Requires CEC Chair OR 40% of sitting CEC to request
  │   → Submitted in writing to SCEC Chair
  │   → SCEC Chair calls Appellate and Waiver Committee
  │   → Decision communicated to county within 15 days of Committee decision
  │   → Cite: [KDP Art. V.H]
  │
  ├── Article III.D.b minimum CEC membership waiver (counties ≤6 LDs only)
  │   → Not applicable to JeffCo (7+ LDs)
  │   → Cite: [KDP Art. III.D.b.vi]
  │
  └── Anything else
       → Generally cannot waive bylaws. Bylaws can be amended per [LJCDP §29.1]
         (majority + 2 weeks notice OR previous-meeting introduction) or per
         [KDP Art. IV.A.b] (SCEC adopts/amends, ratified at next State Conv).
```

## 9. "Has someone been improperly removed?" (appeal procedure)

```
START — Aggrieved party believes a Convention election or CEC decision
was improper.
  │
  ▼
What is the source of the grievance?
  │
  ├── Precinct Convention or Precinct Election
  │   → File written appeal with County Chair within 3 days
  │   → County Chair files with SCEC Chair same day
  │   → CEC hearing within 3 days of receipt of appeal
  │   → County Chair presides; majority of CEC decides (final at county level)
  │   → If unsatisfied: appeal to SCEC within 5 days
  │   → SCEC hearing within 7 days; majority decides (final + conclusive)
  │   → Right to counsel + documents accessible 1 day prior
  │   → Cite: [KDP Art. V.A]
  │
  ├── LD or County Convention
  │   → Written appeal to SCEC Chair within 7 days
  │   → SCEC hearing within 15 days; majority decides (final + conclusive)
  │   → Right to counsel + documents accessible 3 days prior
  │   → Cite: [KDP Art. V.B]
  │
  ├── State Convention
  │   → Written appeal to SCEC Chair within 7 days
  │   → SCEC hearing within 15 days; majority decides (final + conclusive)
  │   → Cite: [KDP Art. V.C]
  │
  ├── CD Convention
  │   → File with any Appellate Committee member upon committee's convening
  │   → Appellate Committee convenes before State Convention
  │   → SCEC review available within 7 days of Appellate decision
  │   → SCEC hearing within 30 days
  │   → Cite: [KDP Art. V.B.d]
  │
  ├── CEC decision under Article III (not addressed in V.A)
  │   → Written appeal to County Chair within 5 days
  │   → CEC hearing within 15 days; majority decides
  │   → If unsatisfied: appeal to SCEC within 7 days
  │   → SCEC hearing within 30 days; majority decides (final + conclusive)
  │   → Cite: [KDP Art. V.D]
  │
  └── Anything else under the bylaws
       → Written appeal to SCEC Chair within 5 days
       → SCEC hearing within 30 days; majority decides (final + conclusive)
       → Documents accessible 10 days prior
       → Cite: [KDP Art. V.E]

After ALL appeals exhausted:
  → Legal action permitted, BUT only in Franklin County Circuit Court, KY
  → Cite: [KDP Art. V.F]
```

# Reorganization Cycle — Precinct → LD → County → CD → State

Cross-cutting reference for the four-year presidential-cycle Reorganization process. Pulls from `[KDP Art. II]` (the operative authority on convention mechanics), `[LJCDP §§4, 10]` (the JeffCo-specific layer), and `[DNC Charter Art. II]` (the National Convention cap).

## When is the next reorganization?

**2028.** Confirmed on presidential-year cadence going forward `[KDP Art. II.A]`.

`[KDP Art. I.R]` was a one-time shift to align Reorganization with presidential election years — sunset at the close of the 2025 State Convention. After 2025, the cadence is permanent: 2028, 2032, 2036, …

Per portal CLAUDE.md (April 21, 2026 red-pen):
> "The 2028 reorganization cycle is on a presidential-election year cadence per KDP Bylaws Article II.A."

This means JCDEC officers elected in 2025 serve through the close of the 2028 LD Convention. Terms are 4 years `[LJCDP §4.5]`, `[LJCDP §10.3]`, `[KDP Art. II.B.b]`.

## The 5 Convention Levels

| Level | Who elects | Who is elected | Eligible voters | KDP cite |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Precinct** | Open registered Dems in precinct | 2 people of different genders + 1 youth (any gender) | Any Dem residing AND registered in precinct | `[KDP Art. II.C]` |
| **Legislative District** | Newly elected Precinct Committee members in that LD | LD Chair + LD Vice Chair (different genders); delegates to CD + State | Duly elected PC members from that LD | `[KDP Art. II.D]` |
| **County (counties ≤6 LDs only — not Jefferson)** | Precinct Committee members at County Convention | CEC members (10–20); delegates to CD + State | Duly elected PC members | `[KDP Art. II.E]` |
| **Congressional District** | County Delegates | 4 SCEC members (no more than 2 same gender) + 1 youth per CD | County Delegates from County Convention | `[KDP Art. II.F]` |
| **State** | State Delegates | 9 at-large SCEC members; ratifies Chair, VC, bylaws | State Delegates from County or LD Convention | `[KDP Art. II.G]` |

**Jefferson County is a 7+ LD county.** It uses LD Conventions, not a County Convention. There are 18 LDs wholly or partially within Jefferson County.

Per `[KDP Art. I.B.f]`: "Legislative District Convention" applies to counties with seven (7) or more State House legislative districts. Per `[KDP Art. I.B.c]`: "County Convention" applies to counties with six (6) or fewer.

## Reorganization year timeline (2028 cycle)

Times are approximate placeholders pending SCEC's adopted 2028 calendar. SCEC sets dates and times per `[KDP Art. II.B.e]`. Conventions "are generally held in the spring preceding the Democratic National Convention" per `[KDP Art. II.B.e]`.

### Q4 2027 — Pre-filing window opens

- **October 1, 2027:** LD Chair, LD VC, LD At-Large pre-filing window opens at LJCDP HQ per `[LJCDP §4.2.1]`. Filings continue through 5pm on final filing date of 2028.
- Pre-filing is **optional** — candidates may also be nominated from the floor at the LD Convention `[LJCDP §4.2.1]`.
- Filings must be in person at LJCDP HQ `[LJCDP §4.2]`.
- Candidates **may not file together** — must file independently `[LJCDP §4.2.2]`. Slate campaigning is allowed; slate filing is not.
- Candidates must be a **resident of the District they intend to serve** at time of election `[LJCDP §4.3]`.

### Q1 2028 — SCEC issues Call to Convention

- SCEC issues the Call to Convention. Dates set by SCEC `[KDP Art. II.B.e]`.
- CEC Chairs determine locations of Precinct, LD, and County Conventions; SCEC Chair determines locations of CD and State `[KDP Art. II.B.e]`.

### ~7–30 days before each Convention — Advertisement

- CEC Chair + SCEC Chair advertise widely `[KDP Art. II.B.f]`.
- Must use **at least 3 of 6 methods** `[KDP Art. II.B.f.i]`–`[KDP Art. II.B.f.vi]`:
  1. Local newspapers
  2. Local radio/TV
  3. County party social media pages
  4. County party website
  5. Mobilize / Facebook Events / web-based organization tools

### Spring 2028 — Convention day

**Single day or sequenced days set by SCEC.** Typical pattern:

#### (a) Precinct Conventions

- **Eligibility to vote:** Any Democrat residing AND registered in the precinct `[KDP Art. II.C.b]`.
- **Elects:** Two people of different genders + a youth (any gender) as Precinct Committee Members `[KDP Art. II.C.a]`.
- **No proxies** at any Convention `[KDP Art. II.B.i.i]`.
- **Voting method:** If 2+ persons nominated, secret ballot. One vote per office. Tie → immediate runoff. Tied runoff → coin flip `[KDP Art. II.B.i]`.
- **Precinct Chair selected** from elected committee, by the attendees `[KDP Art. II.C.c]`.
- **Convention Chair:** A Party Officer at the applicable level (in JeffCo, the relevant LD Chair). If unavailable, the County Party Chair appoints `[KDP Art. II.B.g]`.

#### (b) Legislative District Conventions

- **Eligibility to vote:** Only duly elected Precinct Committee members from that LD `[KDP Art. II.D.b]`.
- **Elects:** LD Chair + Vice Chair (different genders) `[KDP Art. II.D.a]`; delegates to CD + State Conventions.
- **No proxies** `[KDP Art. II.B.i.i]`.
- **Open floor nominations** `[KDP Art. II.B.i.ii]`.
- **Convention Chair:** Party Officer at LD level. Per portal CLAUDE.md (April 21, 2026 red-pen): in practice, LD Chairs preside as Convention Chairs `[KDP Art. II.B.g]`.

#### (c) At Close of LD Convention — Joint Election of County Officers + 18 At-Large

**This is the contested timing point.** Per `[KDP Art. II.D.c]`:

> "At close of the Legislative District Convention, newly elected LD Chairs and Vice Chairs shall meet to elect eighteen (18) at-large members of the County Executive Committee and Committee officers."

Per `[KDP Art. III.D.b.c]`, in counties with 7+ LDs:

- Newly elected LD Chairs meet immediately after LD Convention close
- Meeting chaired by incumbent County Chair (no vote unless newly elected as LD Chair)
- Elect County Chair + Vice Chair of different genders
- **Vice Chair MUST be a newly-elected LD Chair** `[KDP Art. III.D.b.c.iii]`
- County Chair "need not" be a newly-elected LD Chair `[KDP Art. III.D.b.c.iii]`
- Only CEC members nominate / vote
- Then elect 18 additional At-Large members **within thirty-five (35) days** per `[KDP Art. III.D.b.c.v]`, proportional to Dem registration per LD, two (2) of whom must be thirty-five (35) years of age or under

**Drift:**
- `[KDP Art. II.D.c]` says At-Large elected "at close of LD Convention."
- `[KDP Art. III.D.b.c.v]` says "within thirty-five days" of CEC election.
- `[LJCDP §4.4]` says "within thirty (30) days of the Reorganization."

Within KDP itself, II.D.c says "at close" and III.D.b.c.v allows 35 days post-CEC-election. These don't necessarily conflict — II.D.c may describe the immediate election of County Chair/VC + the 18, with III.D.b.c.v providing a 35-day window for the proportional 18.

LJCDP §4.4 (30 days) conflicts with KDP. **KDP governs per `[LJCDP §5.3]`.**

**How the 2025 cycle actually ran the sequencing:** unknown without confirmation from Brook Benningfield (Secretary) or Logan Gatti (Chair). Per portal CLAUDE.md, the bylaws say it should have run per KDP Art. II.D.c; that's consistent with v1 portal content listing 18 At-Large Chairs elected in June 2025.

#### (d) Within 10 days post-Convention — Reporting

Newly elected County Party Chair submits **within ten (10) days** following Precinct/LD/County Conventions `[KDP Art. II.B.l]`:
- All Precinct, LD, and County Convention minutes
- Officer forms
- CD and State Convention delegate forms
- Required sign-in sheets
- Other requested information

To State Party Chair.

### Within 30 days of County Chair election — Secretary + Treasurer

The newly elected County Chair appoints a Secretary and a Treasurer (ex-officio non-voting CEC members) **within 30 days** of election `[LJCDP §11.3]`, `[KDP Art. III.D.b.vi]`.

These appointments serve at the pleasure of the Chair `[KDP Art. III.D.b.vi]`.

### Q2 2028 — Congressional District + State Conventions

#### Congressional District Conventions

- **Eligibility to vote:** County Delegates elected by the County Convention (or, in JeffCo's case, LD Conventions producing State Delegates that also vote at CD level per apportionment) `[KDP Art. II.F.b]`.
- **Elects:** 4 SCEC members (no more than 2 of same gender) + 1 youth per CD `[KDP Art. II.F.a]`.
- **Apportionment:** Formula based on Dem registration + Dem vote in last 2 Presidentials `[KDP Art. II.F.c]`.

#### State Convention

- **Eligibility to vote:** State Delegates elected by County / LD Conventions `[KDP Art. II.G.b]`.
- **Elects:** 9 at-large SCEC members; ratifies Chair, Vice Chair, and bylaws `[KDP Art. II.G.a]`.

### Concurrent — DNC Delegate Selection

Delegates to the Democratic National Convention are elected per the Delegate Selection Plan adopted by SCEC and approved by the DNC `[KDP Art. I.O]`, `[KDP Art. II.H]`. The plan is published each cycle and supersedes the bylaws to the extent it conflicts `[KDP Art. II.H.b]`. The 2028 Delegate Selection Plan is not in this corpus; it lives with KDP staff.

## Eligibility to run — the residency rule

For any LD-level office in JeffCo, candidate must be **resident of the District they intend to serve** at the time of election `[LJCDP §4.3]`. KDP-level: any Democrat registered to vote within the applicable Convention Level can run `[KDP Art. II.B.c]`.

A Precinct Committee candidate must be registered to vote within the precinct.

A County Chair candidate need not be a newly-elected LD Chair `[KDP Art. III.D.b.c.iii]`, but the County Vice Chair must be `[KDP Art. III.D.b.c.iii]`, `[LJCDP §10.2]`. The County Chair must be any Democrat registered to vote in Jefferson County `[LJCDP §10.1]`.

## What can stop the schedule

### Waivers (KDP Art. II.B.e + V.G)

A CEC may petition the SCEC Chair to change the date/time of a Precinct, LD, or County Convention for **extraordinary circumstances**:

- Act of God (tornado, flood, earthquake)
- Officially declared state of emergency
- Conflicting countrywide major event over which CEC has no control
- Any other event the SCEC Chair agrees is extraordinary

Submit waiver request as soon as the conflict is known. SCEC Chair responds **within 7 days** for foreseen, **within 24 hours** for unforeseen / late-notice (<30 days before Convention) `[KDP Art. II.B.e.i.1]`.

CEC must advertise the change per `[KDP Art. I.M]` notification requirements.

### Failure to reorganize (KDP Art. III.D.d)

If a county fails to properly reorganize, abide by Convention rules, meet minimum CEC membership, follow approved waivers, or conduct meetings:

1. SCEC Chair declares no valid CEC
2. Notifies Executive Director
3. Appoints ad-hoc committee (local Dems + SCEC members + adjacent county parties) for **up to 120 days** to identify + support establishment of new CEC
4. If after 120 days no CEC established: ad-hoc committee reports to SCEC Chair on identified issues + next steps

This is the failure-to-reorganize backstop and is not expected to apply to Jefferson County.

### Failure to meet minimum CEC count (Counties ≤6 LDs only)

`[KDP Art. III.D.b.vi.1]` describes a Provisional CEC + Temporary Chair process if a County Convention elects fewer than 10 CEC members but more than 6. Not applicable to Jefferson County (7+ LDs).

## Appeals from a Reorganization Convention — `[KDP Art. V]`

If something goes wrong at the Convention:

| Convention | File appeal with | Deadline | Hearing |
|---|---|---|---|
| Precinct Convention or Precinct Election | County Chair | 3 days | CEC hearing within 3 days |
| LD or County Convention | SCEC Chair | 7 days | SCEC hearing within 15 days (final and conclusive) |
| State Convention | SCEC Chair | 7 days | SCEC hearing within 15 days (final and conclusive) |
| CD Convention | Appellate Committee → SCEC | 7 days post-Appellate decision | SCEC hearing within 30 days |
| All other appeals | SCEC Chair | 5 days | SCEC hearing within 30 days |

**No member may bring legal action without first exhausting appeals. Jurisdiction + venue: Franklin County Circuit Court, KY** `[KDP Art. V.F.a]`.

## Why this cycle matters operationally

The current LDPEC was elected in 2025 (the one-time-shift cycle). Most members have never been through a presidential-year Reorganization. Per portal CLAUDE.md:

> "The current LDPEC is almost entirely first-term: KDP postponed the 2024 reorg to 2025, so most members were elected in the 2025 cycle and have never been through a presidential-year reorg. They are learning their roles in real time."

2028 is the first presidential-year reorganization in the post-Art-I.R era. The mechanics are unchanged from 2025 procedurally — but the cadence (and expectations) reset to the permanent presidential-year cycle. Treat 2028 as the first "normal" reorganization since 2016 (the most recent pre-shift presidential-year reorganization).
