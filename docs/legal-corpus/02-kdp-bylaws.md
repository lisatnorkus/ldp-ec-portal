# KDP Bylaws — Index

Kentucky state party law. Governs LJCDP and all county-level party operations in Kentucky `[LJCDP §5.3]`. Where LJCDP conflicts with KDP, KDP governs.

**Verbatim sources:**
- `/docs/bylaws/kdp-bylaws-2025-full-text.md` — full structured bylaws (Articles I–VII)
- `/docs/bylaws/kdp-bylaws-2025-article-vi.md` — Article VI verbatim (Nominating Committees for special elections)

**Authoritative text:** *By-Laws of the Kentucky Democratic Party.* Ratified by the State Convention of the Kentucky Democratic Party June 26, 2021. Last amended by the State Central Executive Committee on March 1, 2025. Published by the KDP, PO Box 694, Frankfort, KY 40602. (502) 695-4828. www.kydemocrats.org.

## Article-by-article index

### ARTICLE I — General Rules and Provisions

| Section | Title / topic | Key claim |
|---|---|---|
| I.A | Supreme Power in Party Members, Exercised According to Law | Supreme power rests in members in State Convention. Conduct per US/KY Constitutions and Party Laws. |
| I.B | Defined Terms | Definitions of KDP, At-large, County Convention (≤6 LDs), CEC, County Delegate, LD Convention (7+ LDs), Party Official, Party Leader, Party Representative, Party Officer (Chair/VC/Treasurer/Secretary), SCEC, State Delegate, Reorganization, Youth (under 40) |
| I.C | Registered Democrats are Party Members | Membership = all KY-registered Democrats |
| I.D | No Discrimination in Registration | Broadest registration without age/gender identity/religion/economic/sexual orientation/ethnic/disability discrimination |
| I.E | No Discrimination in Party Meetings | Open meetings at all levels |
| I.F | No Tests or Oaths Based on Discrimination | No oaths requiring discrimination on sex/race/color/creed/national origin |
| I.G | Publication of Party Election Procedures | KDP publishes procedures fully |
| I.H | Publication of Qualifications for Party Office | KDP publishes qualifications fully |
| I.I | Democratic Primaries | (a) No KDP Committee or Party Officer in official capacity shall endorse one Dem over another in primary. (b) No Party assets used in primary unless equal access. (b.i) SCEC may by 3/4 majority + secret ballot endorse incumbent KY Constitutional Officer for re-election. (c) Officer-candidates may list party position in campaign materials. |
| I.J | Removal of Support for a Party Nominee or Elected Official | SCEC by 3/4 majority may vote to remove party support. Motion must include cause. |
| I.K | Dismissal of Party Officers | (a) Intentional disclosure of confidential party info → vacant after hearing + majority vote. (b) Intentional failure to comply with bylaws → hearing + majority vote. (c) Precinct hearings at CEC; all other Party Officers at SCEC. (d) If CEC doesn't act in 30 days, SCEC Chair may declare vacant. (e) CEC member may be removed for cause by CEC majority. (f) Failure to support nominees → removal after hearing. (g) Disavowal of allegiance → removal. (h) **Dismissed officer barred from Party office for 2 years.** |
| I.L | Minutes of Meetings | Open to any registered Democrat upon request |
| I.M | Adequate Notices of Times and Places | Notices fully publicized; meeting locations accessible and large enough |
| I.N | House and Senate Caucuses | Their decisions not governed by these bylaws |
| I.O | Selection of Delegates to the National Convention | Per Delegate Selection Plan adopted by SCEC and DNC |
| I.P | Democratic National Committee Members | SCEC elects 2 voting members of different genders at meeting prior to DNC; total per DNC rules |
| I.Q | Rules of Order | **Robert's Rules of Order applies in any case not covered by these party laws or national party rules.** |
| I.R | Reorganization | Reorganization same calendar year as presidential elections. **2025 was a one-time shift; this section sunset at completion of 2025 State Convention.** Going forward, presidential-year cadence (2028, 2032, …). |

### ARTICLE II — Reorganization: Local and State Conventions

| Section | Title / topic | Key claim |
|---|---|---|
| II.A | Purpose | Every 4 years during presidential election years, KDP elects Party Officials at precinct/LD/county/CD/state levels |
| II.B.a | Scope | Each Convention Level follows Article II rules |
| II.B.b | Position Term | 4 years (or until death/removal/resignation or close of next applicable convention) |
| II.B.c | Eligibility to Run | Any Democrat registered to vote within applicable Convention Level |
| II.B.d | Convention Levels | Five levels: Precinct, LD (counties 7+ LDs), County (counties ≤6 LDs), Congressional District, State |
| II.B.e | Time, Place, Location | Dates/times approved by SCEC. Precinct/LD/County locations set by CEC Chairs; CD/State set by SCEC Chair. Waivers for extraordinary circumstances (act of God, state of emergency, conflicting event). SCEC Chair responds ≥7 days before; 24 hrs for late-notice. |
| II.B.f | Advertisement/Notification of Conventions | **Not less than 7 nor more than 30 days before a Convention.** CEC Chair + SCEC Chair advertise widely. **≥3 of 6 methods:** (i) local newspapers, (ii) local radio/TV, (iii) county social media, (iv) county website, (v) Mobilize/Facebook Events, (vi) email/US Mail/phone to CEC + county email list. |
| II.B.g | Convention Chair | **A Party Officer serves as Convention Chair for their applicable Convention Level.** If unavailable: appointed by County Party Chair (Precinct/LD) or State Party Chair (County/CD). |
| II.B.h | Tellers to be Appointed | Convention Chair appoints upon request by any member |
| II.B.i | Method of Voting | (i) **No proxy voting at any Convention.** (ii) Open floor nominations. (iii) Secret ballot if 2+ persons nominated; one vote per Party Office. (iv) Tie → immediate runoff; if runoff ties → coin flip. |
| II.B.j | Alternates | Elected after delegates by same process |
| II.B.k | Delegate Vacancies | Alternates succeed; unfilled = no vote. National Convention vacancies per Delegate Selection Plan. |
| II.B.l | Convention Records and Reporting Requirements | **Within 10 days following Precinct/LD/County Conventions, newly elected County Party Chair submits all minutes, officer forms, delegate forms, sign-in sheets to State Party Chair.** |
| II.B.m | Appeals/Waivers | See Article V |
| II.C.a | Precinct Convention — Purpose | Elect 2 of different genders + 1 youth (any gender) as Precinct Committee Members |
| II.C.b | Precinct Convention — Eligibility to Vote | Any Democrat residing AND registered to vote in precinct |
| II.C.c | Precinct Chair | Newly elected committee chooses one member as Precinct Chair |
| II.D.a | LD Convention — Purpose | Counties with 7+ LDs. Elect LD Chair + Vice Chair (different genders); represent LD on CEC; elect CD + State delegates |
| II.D.b | LD Convention — Eligibility to Vote | Only duly elected Precinct Committee members from that LD |
| II.D.c | **At-Large Members** | **At close of the Legislative District Convention, newly elected LD Chairs and Vice Chairs shall meet to elect eighteen (18) at-large members of the County Executive Committee and Committee officers.** — KEY DRIFT POINT with `[LJCDP §4.4]` (30 days) |
| II.E.a | County Convention — Purpose | Elect CECs and CD/State delegates |
| II.E.b | County Convention — Eligibility to Vote | Only duly elected Precinct Committee members |
| II.F.a | Congressional District Convention — Purpose | Elect 4 (no more than 2 same gender) + 1 youth to represent CD on SCEC |
| II.F.b | CD Convention — Eligibility to Vote | County Delegates elected by County Convention |
| II.F.c | Apportionment of Delegates | Formula based on Dem registration + Dem vote in last 2 Presidentials |
| II.G.a | State Convention — Purpose | Elect 9 at-large SCEC members; ratify Chair, Vice Chair, bylaws |
| II.G.b | State Convention — Eligibility to Vote | State Delegates elected by County/LD Conventions |
| II.H | DNC Delegate Selection | Per Delegate Selection Plan approved by DNC. **In conflict, Delegate Selection Plan supersedes these Bylaws.** |

### ARTICLE III — Precinct, Legislative District and County Executive Committees

| Section | Title / topic | Key claim |
|---|---|---|
| III.A.a | Proxy Voting | **Voting by proxy not permitted in election or removal of any individual to/from any office.** Otherwise allowed for regular/special business at SCEC or CEC meetings — in writing, stating how and for what purpose. Young Dems/Woman's Club president absent: proxy may be assigned by president to a chapter officer (with notice to Secretary). Permanent proxy allowed if chapter president holds voting position in own right. |
| III.A.b | Meeting Removal | Non-member may be removed per Robert's Rules. EC may, by 3/4 majority, ban an individual from meetings for cause. Ban duration in motion. No ban exceeds current term. Reconsideration possible. |
| III.B.a | Precinct Committee — Election | Per Article II |
| III.B.b | Precinct Committee — Purpose | 2 different genders + 1 youth. Primary organizers in precinct. Voter contact, volunteer recruitment, phonebanks, events. Serve as delegates to LD or County Convention. |
| III.B.c | Filling Vacancies in Precinct Committees | LD Chair (in counties with 6+ LDs) or County Chair (in all others) notifies SCEC. **CEC fills within 90 days of notification.** If vacancy >90 days, SCEC Chair fills. |
| III.C.a | LD Committee — Election | Per Article II |
| III.C.b | LD Committee — Purpose | Chair + Vice Chair (opposite genders). Represent state House District on CEC. Voter contact, volunteer recruitment, phonebanks, events. Elect delegates to CD + State Conventions. |
| III.D.a | CEC — Powers and Duties | Executive power of County Dem Party vested in CEC. Powers within county parallel SCEC powers within state. Disputes/contests: CEC hearing + ruling; appeal to SCEC per Article V. |
| III.D.b.i | Counties ≤6 LDs CEC composition | County Convention elects 10–20 CEC members |
| III.D.b.ii | Young Dems / Woman's Club Presidents | Voting members of CEC. Multiple Women's Clubs: CEC decides which president(s) are voting members. Minimum 1. |
| III.D.b.iii | Gender + age requirements | No more than 1/2 same gender. Must include 1 person 40 or under. Composition reflects age/gender/minority populations. **Note: KDP text contains "forty (40) thirty-five or under" — appears to be ambiguous/typo in source.** |
| III.D.b.iv | Residency | All EC members must live in and be registered Democrats in respective county |
| III.D.b.v | Counties ≤6 LDs CEC chair election | Newly elected members meet immediately after County Convention to elect Chair + VC of different gender; nominees must already be EC members |
| III.D.b.vi | Secretary + Treasurer appointment | **Within 30 days following election**, Chair appoints Secretary + Treasurer (ex-officio non-voting) |
| III.D.b.vi.1 | Failure to meet minimum CEC membership at County Convention | Provisional CEC + Temporary Chair process; ≥14 days notice for new election |
| III.D.b.vi.2 | Expansion of Membership | CEC may vote to expand to ≤30 members. **75% of full EC membership required.** |
| III.D.b.c | Counties 7+ LDs CEC composition | LD Chair + other LD Chairs constitute CEC. LD VC becomes voting member only when LD Chair absent (unless CEC bylaws say otherwise). Committee elects County Chair + VC of different gender. **Nominees for VC must be a Legislative District Chair.** Only CEC members nominate/vote. Young Dems + Woman's Club Presidents are voting members. **CEC elects 18 additional members within 35 days**, 2 of whom must be 35 or under. 18 must reflect proportional Dem registration per LD. Each of 18 elected by majority vote of entire CEC. |
| III.D.d | Requirements for Creating CEC between Conventions | If county fails to reorganize / abide by Convention rules / meet minimum / follow waiver / conduct meetings: SCEC Chair may declare no valid CEC; appoint ad-hoc committee for up to 120 days. |
| III.D.e.i | Duties of Chair | Chief strategist + governing officer. Calls + presides at meetings. **25% written request → meeting within 14 days, ≥5 days notice.** Must hold ≥1 meeting/quarter. **Quarterly meetings shall occur during February, May, August, November.** Record retention: documents returned within 15 days of leaving position. |
| III.D.e.ii | Duties of Secretary | Maintains records of all party personnel + minutes. **Files minutes with SCEC Chair + KDP Exec Director within 10 days of meetings.** Record retention 15 days. |
| III.D.e.iii | Duties of Treasurer | Maintains finances + records. **Files KREF reports + FEC if applicable.** Record retention 15 days. |
| III.D.f.i | Removal of Chair | **Majority vote of entire EC**, without cause |
| III.D.f.ii | Removal of CEC members for cause | Majority of CEC. For cause includes fraud, misappropriation, criminal acts, hostile environment, behavior injurious to business or member retention. |
| III.D.f.iii | Automatic vacancy on death/relocation/disability/resignation | Declared vacant by majority vote |
| III.D.g | Conditions Causing Automatic Vacancies | **Failure to attend (in person or by written proxy) 2 successive meetings OR 50% of annual meetings in person** → declared vacant by County Chair |
| III.D.h | Filling Vacancies on CEC | **CEC has 90 days; else SCEC Chair fills** + notifies new officer + furnishes contact info to CEC Secretary |
| III.D.i | Redistricting Vacancies (counties 7+ LDs) | EC member no longer residing in their LD → vacant **July 1**. **CEC has 60 days; else SCEC Chair fills.** Quorum to fill: 50% of remaining non-vacated positions. Elected fill takes office as last order of business at same meeting. County Chair retains seat if simultaneously holding LD position they no longer reside in. County VC: both LD Chair + County VC positions vacate; new County VC elected from LD Chairs of different gender from County Chair. |
| III.D.j | Tie Vote | Broken by SCEC vote |
| III.D.k | Quorum | **40% of elected committee members present (in person or proxy)** before vote. **Proxies cannot establish quorum for election/dismissal of any Party officer.** |

### ARTICLE IV — State Central Executive Committee

| Section | Title / topic | Key claim |
|---|---|---|
| IV.A | Executive Power | SCEC has jurisdiction over party organization, bylaws, convention calls, candidate nominations |
| IV.A.b | Bylaw amendment | SCEC may adopt/amend between State Conventions; must be ratified at next State Convention. **Proposals provided ≥30 days prior to vote.** |
| IV.A.c | Bylaw amendment recording | Approved amendments recorded verbatim in minutes; may be attachment |

| IV.A.f | Quorum | **20 members present in person or virtually.** Proxies allowed except for election/dismissal of Party officer. |
| IV.A.g | Polling SCEC | Chair may poll by phone, fax, mail, email; must contact all members; document dates/times; ≥20 must participate |
| IV.A.h | Majority binds SCEC | Secretary records question and individual votes; notifies members |
| IV.B | Chair Powers + Duties | Chief Executive Officer of SCEC. Implements SCEC decisions. Calls meetings. **Must call ≥1 meeting/quarter.** Tentative quarterly schedule to members before Jan 15 each year. 25% written request → meeting within 30 days, ≥7 days notice. |
| IV.B.b | DNC temporary appointments | SCEC Chair has authority to name persons to temporary DNC standing committee positions if requested prior to State Convention or KY delegation meeting |
| IV.C | Vice-Chair Powers | Presides absence of Chair. Succeeds to Chair powers on Chair death/resignation/incapacity until new Chair elected. |
| IV.D | Election of Chair + Vice-Chair | (a) Sitting Dem Governor may present individual. (b) Otherwise nominating committee: 1 from each CD, 1 at-large, 1 from Senate Caucus, 1 from House Caucus, 1 per Dem Statewide Constitutional Officer. (c) Ratified at next State Convention. **VC different gender than Chair.** Neither need be previously-elected SCEC members. (d) Either removable without cause by majority of entire SCEC. |
| IV.E | Voting Members | (a) 4 + 1 youth per CD; (b) National Committee members; (c) 9 state-at-large from State Convention; (d) Chair + VC ratified; (e) 4 General Assembly members (2 per chamber elected by Dem caucus); (f) President of KY Young Dems + Women's Club KY president; (g) Chairs of officially recognized caucuses + councils |
| IV.F | Non-Voting Members | (1) KDP Exec Director + Legal Counsel + Treasurer (appointed by Chair); (2) All KY Statewide Constitutional Officers + Dem US Congresspeople + Dem US Senators (ex-officio). Chair may appoint up to 3 additional ex-officio with Committee approval. |
| IV.G | Removal of Members | Subject to Art. I.K. Automatic vacancy on move from KY (or CD if CD rep), death, disability, resignation, missing 2 successive meetings or 50% of 12-month meetings. |
| IV.H | Filling Vacancies | (a) GA members → House/Senate Dem caucus. (b) Legal Counsel/Secretary/Treasurer → SCEC Chair. (c) CD vacancy → SCEC Chair notifies SCEC within 7 days; remaining CD reps caucus to fill by majority. Non-binding input from SCEC Chair + County Chairs in district within 14 days. Tie broken by SCEC majority. **Any vacancy not filled for 30 days → filled by majority of remaining SCEC.** (d) At-Large vacancy → similar caucus process with 14-day input window. (e) **Any vacancy not filled for 60 days → SCEC Chair appoints + majority of remaining SCEC approves.** |
| IV.I | Chair Pro Tem | If Chair + Vice-Chair both absent, SCEC members present elect one to preside |
| IV.J | Chair May Vote | Chair/VC/Chair Pro Tem cast 1 vote each if they choose |
| IV.K | Role of Secretary | Appointed by + serves at pleasure of Chair. Need not be SCEC member. Keeps proceedings + roster. Records furnished to any SCEC member on request. Secretary votes only if otherwise SCEC member. |
| IV.L | Finance Committee | Party Treasurer + Chair + Exec Director + 2 at-large + 1 per CD (chosen by CD caucus). Prepares annual budget; recommends salaries/staffing/fundraising goals; gives financial report at each SCEC meeting. |
| IV.M | Recognition of Organizations | SCEC shall not recognize any organization/committee titled "Democrat" not formed per KDP + DNC bylaws |
| IV.N | Strategic Planning Committee | KDP Exec Director + SCEC Chair + a Committee Chair + 1 SCEC selected per CD + Chair of each SCEC standing Committee + up to 3 At-Large appointed by SCEC Chair. Approves + monitors 3-year strategic plan with measurements + accountability, updated annually + revised every 3 years. |
| IV.O | By-Laws Committee | Chair appointed by KDP Chair (must be elected voting SCEC member). KDP Legal Counsel + 4–8 additional members. Serve at pleasure of KDP Chair. Reviews + recommends amendments to SCEC by majority. |
| IV.P | Appellate and Waiver Committee | SCEC Chair (or designee) + 1 per CD. Hears appeals + waivers per Article V. Also: KDP Chair (or designee) + 6 members + 6 alternates elected by SCEC. KDP Chair = chair. Convenes immediately after CD Convention close; remains in session until all CD Conventions completed + appeals adjudicated. |
| IV.Q | Other Committees | At discretion of Chair |

### ARTICLE V — Appeals and Waivers

| Section | Topic | Key timing |
|---|---|---|
| V.A | Precinct Convention + Election Appeals | **Aggrieved party: 3 days to file with County Chair.** CEC hearing within 3 days. Appeal to SCEC: 5 days; SCEC hearing within 7 days. Documents available 1 day prior to hearing. Right to counsel. |
| V.B | LD or County Convention Appeals | **7 days to file with SCEC Chair.** SCEC hearing within 15 days. **Final and conclusive.** |
| V.B.d | CD Convention Appeals | Filed with Appellate Committee, which convenes before State Convention. Appeal of Appellate decision: 7 days to file with SCEC Chair; SCEC hearing within 30 days. |
| V.C | State Convention Appeals | **7 days to file with SCEC Chair.** SCEC hearing within 15 days. **Final and conclusive.** |
| V.D | Appeals of CEC decisions under Article III (not addressed in V.A) | **5 days to file with County Chair.** CEC hearing within 15 days. Appeal to SCEC: 7 days. SCEC hearing within 30 days. |
| V.E | All Other Appeals | **5 days to file with SCEC Chair.** SCEC hearing within 30 days. Documents available 10 days prior. |
| V.F | Exhaustion of Appeal Procedures | **No member may bring legal action without first exhausting all appeals.** **Jurisdiction + venue: Franklin County Circuit Court, KY.** |
| V.G | Precinct + County/LD Convention Waivers | CEC Chair (or ad-hoc committee) may request. **SCEC Chair + Exec Director + Appellate and Waiver Committee approve.** Submitted in writing ≥15 days before SCEC-approved convention schedule. Emergency waivers via phone/text/email ≥1 hour before — Appellate Committee approval not required. |
| V.H | CEC Rules Waivers | **CEC Chair OR 40% of sitting CEC** may request. Submitted to SCEC Chair → Appellate and Waiver Committee. **Decision within 15 days of Committee decision.** |

## Drift from KDP — see `11-known-drift.md`

The active drift between KDP and LJCDP:

1. **At-Large election timing.** `[KDP Art. II.D.c]` says "at close of LD Convention." `[LJCDP §4.4]` says "within thirty (30) days of the Reorganization." KDP governs.
2. **At-Large model.** `[KDP Art. II.D.c]` + `[KDP Art. III.D.b.c.v]` establishes 18 countywide At-Large elected by joint LD Chairs + VCs. `[LJCDP §§4.4, 6.6, 8]` describes pre-2025 LD-specific At-Large elected by individual LD Chairs. KDP governs.
3. **Young Dems / Women's Club proxy assignment.** `[KDP Art. III.A.a.ii]` allows president to assign proxy to chapter officer. `[LJCDP §23.2]` says they cannot assign. LJCDP is stricter — could be interpreted as a more restrictive local rule (not inconsistent with KDP) or as drift; flagged.

Drift between KDP and DNC: none material in current versions.
