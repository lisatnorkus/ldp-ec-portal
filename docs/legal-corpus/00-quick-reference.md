# Quick Reference — Most Common Lookups

All numbers quoted from source. Where multiple sources address the same question, both are listed and KDP is named as governing per `[LJCDP §5.3]`.

**Notation in this file:** For at-a-glance table density, numerals appear as "30 days," "40%," "$500." The source bylaws use the parenthetical-numeral form ("within thirty (30) days," "forty per cent (40%)," "Purchases of $500 or less"). When drafting any external deliverable (memo, brief, motion language, portal copy), **use the verbatim source phrasing**, not the shorthand. The standing rule per `jcdec-bylaws-reference.md`:

> Day counts, vote thresholds, dollar amounts, quorums — quote verbatim with citation. "Within thirty (30) days" stays as "within thirty (30) days `[LJCDP §21.1]`," not "about a month" or "promptly."

Block quotes throughout this corpus preserve the verbatim form. The tables below are navigation aids; trace back to the cited section for verbatim language before using in any external document.

## Robert's Rules — when it applies

Robert's Rules of Order applies in any case not covered by the bylaws at every level:

- **LJCDP:** `[LJCDP §28.1]` — "The Roberts Rules of Order shall apply in any case not covered by these By-Laws…"
- **KDP:** `[KDP Art. I.Q]` — "Robert's Rules of Order shall apply in any case not covered by these party laws or national party rules."
- **DNC:** `[DNC Charter Art. IX, §14]` — "In the absence of other provisions, Robert's Rules of Order (as most recently revised) shall govern the conduct of all Democratic Party meetings."

Cite Robert's Rules as `[Robert's Rules §X:Y]` referring to the current edition (Newly Revised, 12th ed.).

## NDA and fiduciary requirement

LD Chairs, LD Vice Chairs, and At-Large members must sign a Non-Disclosure Agreement at the start of their term. Each has a **fiduciary relationship** with the CEC. `[LJCDP §2.2]`

This is not optional — it's a bylaw requirement at the start of every term.

## Anti-discrimination — Party meetings and registration

All public meetings at every level of KDP are open to all members without discrimination based on age (if of voting age), gender identity, religion, economic status, sexual orientation, ethnic identity, or physical disability. `[KDP Art. I.E]`

KDP supports broadest possible registration without discrimination on the same grounds. `[KDP Art. I.D]`

DNC Charter mirror provisions at `[DNC Charter Art. VIII, §2]` (sex, race, age, color, creed, national origin, religion, economic status, sexual orientation, gender identity, ethnic identity, disability).

## Democratic Primaries — endorsement prohibition

No Democratic Committee governed by KDP Bylaws, and no Democratic Party Officer acting in their official capacity, shall endorse or support one Democratic candidate over another in a Democratic Primary Election. `[KDP Art. I.I.a]`

No assets of the Democratic Party shall be used in a Dem Primary unless made equally available to all candidates. `[KDP Art. I.I.b]`

**Exception:** SCEC may, by 3/4 majority and secret ballot, endorse an incumbent Statewide KY Constitutional Officer for re-election and provide General-Election-level support during the primary. `[KDP Art. I.I.b.i]`

**Note:** This prohibition does NOT apply to nonpartisan races (Mayor + Metro Council per HB 388). LDP's 60% endorsement process for those races lives in Endorsement Process Committee policy, not in the bylaws. See `06-endorsement-process.md`.

## Hierarchy of authority — quick recap

When LJCDP and KDP conflict, KDP governs `[LJCDP §5.3]`.
When KDP and DNC conflict, DNC governs `[DNC Charter Art. II, §2]`.
Drift between LJCDP and KDP currently includes:
- §§4.4 / 6.6 / 8 (LD-specific At-Large model superseded by KDP Art. II.D.c)
- §26 (standing committee list — 10 listed in §26 vs 8 + 3 ad hoc in practice)
- §3.1 (expired 2000-2004 SCEC-mandated seat still listed)
- §23.2 vs `[KDP Art. III.A.a.ii]` (Young Dems / Women's Club proxy assignment)

See `11-known-drift.md` for the full catalog.
