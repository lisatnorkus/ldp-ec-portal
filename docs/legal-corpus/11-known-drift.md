# Known Drift Between LJCDP and KDP

Active conflicts and obsolete provisions in the LJCDP Bylaws. Documented but **not silently resolved.** This is the standing work for the JCDEC Bylaws Committee — each item below names what the LJCDP bylaw says, what KDP says (or what practice has become), which governs, and what amendment is owed.

**Standing rule for this corpus:** When LJCDP and KDP disagree, both must be surfaced. Per `[LJCDP §5.3]`, KDP governs. The drift is flagged for Bylaws Committee amendment, not papered over in copy or operational reference.

## Hierarchy anchor

`[LJCDP §5.3]`:

> "To make rules, by-laws, and regulations as it shall deem necessary to promote the progress of the Democratic party in Louisville and Jefferson County, **so long as such rules, by-laws, and regulations are not inconsistent with those of the Kentucky and National Democratic Parties.**"

When a conflict exists, KDP governs. The LJCDP text is the rule-on-the-books locally, but the operative rule is KDP.

`[DNC Charter Art. II, §2]` provides the parallel anchor at the next level up — state party rules apply unless in conflict with the DNC Charter; in such conflict, state Parties must take positive steps to bring state laws into conformity.

## Drift Item 1: At-Large model — LJCDP §§4.4, 6.6, 8 (pre-2025) vs KDP Art. II.D.c (post-2025)

### What LJCDP says (the pre-2025 LD-specific At-Large model)

**`[LJCDP §4.4]`:**
> "Legislative District Chairs and Legislative District Vice Chairs are elected on a date set by the State Central Executive Committee. **Legislative District At-Large members are elected by the Legislative District Chairs on a date set by the County Executive Committee, within thirty (30) days of the Reorganization.**"

**`[LJCDP §6.6]`:**
> "To elect Legislative District At-Large members, **one from each Legislative District wholly or partially within Jefferson County**, who are residents of the Legislative District in which they intend to serve, for a term of equal date and duration to that of Legislative District Chairs and Legislative District Vice-Chairs."

**`[LJCDP §6.6.1]`:**
> "Legislative District At-Large members shall be chosen from among those who have properly filed a Declaration and Notification within the filing period as established by these By-Laws."

**`[LJCDP §8]`** describes the LD-specific At-Large duties:
> "8.1 To assist under the direction of the Legislative District Chair in the affairs of the Democratic Party within their respective Legislative District."

### What KDP says (the post-2025 countywide At-Large model)

**`[KDP Art. II.D.c]`:**
> "**At close of the Legislative District Convention, newly elected LD Chairs and Vice Chairs shall meet to elect eighteen (18) at-large members of the County Executive Committee and Committee officers.**"

**`[KDP Art. III.D.b.c.v]`:**
> "The County Executive Committee so elected shall also elect **within thirty-five days, eighteen (18) additional members, two of whom shall be thirty-five years of age or under**, who shall serve as members of the Executive Committee for a four-year term, with the same powers and duties as members of the Executive Committee serving as Legislative District Chairs and Vice-Chairs and are subject to the same conditions of removal."

**`[KDP Art. III.D.b.c.v.1]`:**
> "The eighteen (18) additional members must be selected to reflect the proportion of registered Democrats in each Legislative District."

### What's drifted

| Issue | LJCDP says | KDP says | Status |
|---|---|---|---|
| Who elects At-Large? | Individual LD Chairs (each elects one for their LD) `[LJCDP §6.6]` | Joint vote of newly-elected LD Chairs + LD VCs `[KDP Art. II.D.c]` | **KDP governs.** Current 18 At-Large class elected per KDP model. |
| How many At-Large? | One per LD wholly or partially in Jefferson County `[LJCDP §6.6]` (= 18) | 18 additional members `[KDP Art. III.D.b.c.v]` (also 18) | Numerically aligned, but selection authority differs. |
| When elected? | Within 30 days of Reorganization `[LJCDP §4.4]` | At close of LD Convention `[KDP Art. II.D.c]` + within 35 days for the proportional 18 `[KDP Art. III.D.b.c.v]` | **KDP governs.** The "at close" + "within 35 days" framing accommodates the joint election followed by proportional selection. |
| Residency requirement? | Must reside in LD they intend to serve `[LJCDP §6.6]` | No LD-specific residency for countywide At-Large; must be registered to vote in county `[KDP Art. III.D.b.iv]` | **KDP governs.** Current 18 At-Large are countywide, not LD-tied. |
| Duties? | Assist LD Chair within LD `[LJCDP §8.1]` | Same powers and duties as LD Chairs/VCs serving on CEC `[KDP Art. III.D.b.c.v]`; countywide service per practice | **KDP governs.** Current 18 At-Large operate countywide, closer to `[LJCDP §9.x]` "non-LD At-Large" duties. |

### Practical effect

The 18 At-Large Chairs elected in June 2025 operate under the KDP model:
- Elected jointly by newly-elected LD Chairs + LD VCs at close of LD Convention
- Serve countywide (not LD-tied)
- Have same powers/duties as LD Chair/VC CEC members
- Have voting rights on CEC `[LJCDP §3.1]` (the §3.1 voting rights list does include "a number of At-Large members equal to the number of State Legislative Districts" — that count maps to the current 18; the model differs but the voting status carries through)

### Amendment owed to Bylaws Committee

1. **Repeal or amend `[LJCDP §4.4]`** to align with `[KDP Art. II.D.c]` — change "within thirty (30) days" to "at the close of the Legislative District Convention" with a 35-day window for the proportional 18 per KDP.
2. **Repeal `[LJCDP §6.6]`** — the LD-specific At-Large election by LD Chairs is no longer the operative model. Could be repealed entirely, with the At-Large election moved to a new section reflecting the post-2025 KDP model.
3. **Amend `[LJCDP §8]`** — the LD-specific At-Large duties section. Either repeal (if no LD-specific At-Large class remains) or amend to apply only to a hypothetical future LD-specific At-Large class (preserving the structure for any future bylaw change).
4. **Update `[LJCDP §6.6.1]`** — the filing-period reference would need to be updated to reflect that the joint LD Chair+VC election doesn't require pre-filing under the same rules.

**Recommended approach:** Bring §§4.4, 6.6, 6.6.1, and 8 into one amendment package, with §9 retained as the duties anchor for the current 18 countywide At-Large class (which it accurately describes).

## Drift Item 2: Standing Committee list — LJCDP §26.1 (10 listed) vs JCDEC practice (8 standing + 3 ad hoc)

### What LJCDP says

**`[LJCDP §26.1]`:**
> "There shall be Standing Committees which shall be Bylaws, Facilities, Finance (which will oversee fundraising), Events, Volunteering, Youth, Communication, Labor, Training and Candidate Recruitment."

That's **10 standing committees**:
1. Bylaws
2. Facilities
3. Finance
4. Events
5. Volunteering
6. Youth
7. Communication
8. Labor
9. Training
10. Candidate Recruitment

### What JCDEC actually runs (April 2026)

**8 standing:**
1. Bylaws
2. Candidate Recruitment
3. Communications (renamed from "Communication")
4. Events
5. Facilities
6. Finance
7. Training
8. Volunteer (renamed from "Volunteering")

**3 ad hoc:**
1. Branding
2. Endorsement Process
3. Platform

### What's drifted

| Drift point | Status |
|---|---|
| §26.1 lists "Volunteering"; practice runs "Volunteer" | Naming drift — amend to current name |
| §26.1 lists "Communication"; practice runs "Communications" | Naming drift — amend to current name |
| §26.1 lists "Youth" as standing; not running | Dropped — amend to either remove OR start a Youth standing committee |
| §26.1 lists "Labor" as standing; not running | Dropped — amend to either remove OR start a Labor standing committee |
| Branding runs as ad hoc; not in §26.1 | New committee — amend to add as standing OR formal recognition as ad hoc |
| Endorsement Process runs as ad hoc; not in §26.1 | Same as Branding |
| Platform runs as ad hoc; not in §26.1 | Same as Branding |

### Practical effect

JCDEC operates under the 8 + 3 model. The §26.1 list reflects neither the names nor the slate accurately. Operationally, the County Chair appoints all committee chairs per `[LJCDP §11.6]` regardless of standing-vs-ad-hoc status, so the operational impact is limited — but the rules-on-paper don't match the actual committee structure.

### Amendment owed to Bylaws Committee

**Amend `[LJCDP §26.1]`** to reflect current practice. Two options for the Bylaws Committee to consider:

**Option A — Reflect current practice:**
> "There shall be Standing Committees which shall be Bylaws, Branding, Candidate Recruitment, Communications, Endorsement Process, Events, Facilities, Finance (which will oversee fundraising), Platform, Training, and Volunteer."

(That promotes the 3 current ad hoc committees to standing, giving them bylaw-anchored permanence.)

**Option B — Keep ad hoc flexibility:**
> "There shall be Standing Committees which shall be Bylaws, Candidate Recruitment, Communications, Events, Facilities, Finance (which will oversee fundraising), Training, and Volunteer. Ad Hoc Committees may be created by the County Chair pursuant to §11.6 as needed; current Ad Hoc Committees include Branding, Endorsement Process, and Platform."

(That formalizes the 8 standing and recognizes the 3 ad hoc within the bylaws structure.)

Either path resolves the drift. Option B preserves the County Chair's flexibility to spin up additional ad hoc committees without requiring bylaw amendment.

## Drift Item 3: Young Dems / Women's Club proxy assignment — LJCDP §23.2 vs KDP Art. III.A.a.ii

### What LJCDP says

**`[LJCDP §23.2]`:**
> "**No other member of the County Executive Committee may assign their proxy, including the President of the Jefferson County Young Democrats Club and the representative of the Jefferson County Democratic Women's Club.**"

### What KDP says

**`[KDP Art. III.A.a.ii]`:**
> "If the president of a state or local Young Democrats or Woman's Club chapter is absent from an Executive Committee meeting, **their proxy may be assigned by the president, with proper notice to the committee Secretary, to an officer of the chapter.**"

**`[KDP Art. III.A.a.iii]`:**
> "If the president of a state or local Young Democrats or Woman's Club chapter holds a voting position on the Executive Committee in their own right, then **a permanent proxy may be granted to the chapter officer whose role it is incumbent upon to exercise authority in the absence of the chapter president per the chapter's governing documents.**"

### What's drifted

KDP allows the chapter president to assign a proxy to a chapter officer with notice to the Secretary, and even allows a permanent proxy in some cases. LJCDP prohibits the chapter president from assigning a proxy at all.

### Hierarchy analysis

This is the trickier case. Two possible readings:

**Reading 1 — LJCDP is "more restrictive but not inconsistent."** Local rules can be stricter than state rules without conflict. LJCDP §5.3 says local rules may not be "inconsistent" with KDP — and a stricter rule that bars something KDP permits could be argued as restricting, not contradicting. Under this reading, **LJCDP governs locally** and the chapter presidents simply cannot assign a proxy in JCDEC meetings.

**Reading 2 — LJCDP is inconsistent with KDP.** KDP affirmatively grants the chapter president a proxy-assignment right. A local rule that strips that right is inconsistent. Under this reading, **KDP governs** and the chapter presidents can assign a proxy to a chapter officer with notice to the Secretary.

### Status

**Surfaced for Bylaws Committee determination.** The reading is unsettled in this corpus. Pending Bylaws Committee guidance, operational practice should follow the stricter reading (LJCDP §23.2) because:
1. The chapter presidents are voting CEC members in their own right per `[LJCDP §3.1]`
2. The stricter rule (no proxy) is administratively simpler
3. The LJCDP text is the explicit JCDEC rule
4. If KDP-permitted, the chapter has the option to send the president in person rather than rely on proxy

### Amendment owed to Bylaws Committee

Resolve the reading and amend `[LJCDP §23.2]` either:
- To clarify that the stricter LJCDP rule stands as a more-restrictive-but-not-inconsistent local rule, OR
- To align with KDP §III.A.a.ii by allowing proxy assignment to a chapter officer with notice to the Secretary.

## Drift Item 4: Expired 2000-2004 SCEC-mandated At-Large seat — LJCDP §3.1

### What LJCDP says

**`[LJCDP §3.1]`:**
> "The voting members of the County Executive Committee shall be… **the one At- Large member mandated by the State Central Executive Committee for the 2000-2004 term, which said mandated position and term shall expire at the 2004 Reorganization**…"

### What's drifted

This seat **expired at the 2004 Reorganization** per the section's own language. Yet the seat remains in the voting-members list 22+ years later.

### Status

The seat is **not operative.** No one holds it. JCDEC operations proceed without it. This is pure rules-on-paper drift — a clean-up amendment owed.

### Amendment owed to Bylaws Committee

**Remove the expired clause from `[LJCDP §3.1]`.** This is a simple cleanup amendment. Could be bundled with other §3 / §4 / §6 At-Large amendments addressing Drift Item 1.

## Drift Item 5: Minutes filing — LJCDP §13.4 vs KDP Art. III.D.e.ii.1

### What LJCDP says

**`[LJCDP §13.4]`:**
> "To fill [file] all minutes of the County Executive Committee meetings **with the Chair of the State Central Executive Committee within ten (10) days of such meetings.**"

### What KDP says

**`[KDP Art. III.D.e.ii.1]`:**
> "The Secretary shall file the minutes of all committee meetings **with the Chair of the SCEC AND the Executive Director of the KDP within ten (10) days of such meetings.**"

### What's drifted

LJCDP says file with SCEC Chair only. KDP says file with SCEC Chair AND KDP Executive Director.

### Status

**KDP governs.** Practice should honor both filings (SCEC Chair + KDP Executive Director). Minor administrative drift — likely already honored in practice but the LJCDP text should be updated.

### Amendment owed to Bylaws Committee

**Amend `[LJCDP §13.4]`** to add "and the Executive Director of the Kentucky Democratic Party."

## Drift Item 6: Possible County Chair removal threshold — LJCDP §16.1 vs KDP Art. III.D.f.i

### What LJCDP says

**`[LJCDP §16.1]`:**
> "The County Chair may be removed at any time during her/his term without cause by **a majority vote of the voting members of the County Executive Committee.**"

### What KDP says

**`[KDP Art. III.D.f.i]`:**
> "The Chair of the County Executive Committee may be removed without cause **by a majority vote of the entire Executive Committee.**"

### What's drifted

The texts are close but differ subtly:
- LJCDP says "majority of voting members" — ambiguous between majority of all voting members vs majority of those present and voting
- KDP says "majority of the entire Executive Committee" — clearly majority of all voting members

### Hierarchy analysis

KDP's stricter reading (majority of all voting members) governs. The vote requires more than just majority of those present at a meeting — it requires affirmative votes equal to majority of the entire voting CEC.

**Operationally:** Combined with `[LJCDP §22.1.1]` (proxies don't count for dismissal), the meeting must have 40% in person to be a valid removal vote AND the vote count must reach majority of all voting CEC members.

For a CEC with ~56 voting members, the threshold is at minimum 29 affirmative votes (more than half of 56) — and the meeting must include 40% in person of LD Chairs + LD VCs + LD At-Large.

### Status

**KDP governs.** The LJCDP wording is ambiguous; KDP clarifies. Practice should follow the KDP-strict reading.

### Amendment owed to Bylaws Committee

**Amend `[LJCDP §16.1]`** to clarify: "by a majority vote of the **entire** voting membership of the County Executive Committee." Same amendment for `[LJCDP §18.1]` (County VC removal).

## Drift Item 7: KDP "forty (40) thirty-five or under" age requirement

### What KDP says

**`[KDP Art. III.D.b.c.iii]`:**
> "No more than one-half of the membership of the County Executive Committee may be of the same gender. The County Executive Committee must also include **one person age forty (40) thirty-five or under**."

### What's drifted

The KDP text appears to contain a typo or unresolved amendment artifact — "age forty (40) thirty-five or under" is internally inconsistent. The intended threshold is unclear: 40 or 35?

Parallel provision `[KDP Art. III.D.b.c.v]` specifies "two of whom shall be thirty-five years of age or under" for the 18 At-Large — so the youth age threshold at the At-Large level is clearly 35.

`[KDP Art. I.B.n]`: "Youth shall refer to any registered Democrat under the age of 40." So general "youth" definition is under 40.

### Status

**Unresolved KDP-internal drift.** Not LJCDP drift; this is a KDP text issue.

For operational JCDEC purposes, the conservative reading is **35 or under** (the stricter standard), which would meet both 35 and 40 thresholds. Including a youth member of any age under 35 satisfies both.

### Amendment owed (not to JCDEC Bylaws Committee, but flagged for SCEC awareness)

This is a KDP-side cleanup issue. Could be raised through the SCEC By-Laws Committee per `[KDP Art. IV.O]`.

## Drift Item 8: §26.1 doesn't include the Endorsement Process as standing

### What LJCDP says

`[LJCDP §26.1]` does not list Endorsement Process Committee.

### What practice does

Endorsement Process operates as an **ad hoc committee** per current JCDEC practice. The committee:
- Owns the LDP endorsement process for nonpartisan Mayor + Metro Council races
- Adopts the 60% threshold, secret ballot via ElectionRunner, no proxies during endorsement
- Operates a January–February timeline in election years

### Status

**Not technically drift** — LJCDP §11.6 explicitly grants the County Chair authority to appoint ad hoc committees. The Endorsement Process Committee is properly constituted under that authority.

**BUT** — the endorsement process is a recurring, structurally important LDP function. Treating it as ad hoc means the policy framework (60%, ElectionRunner, no proxies) lives in committee/CEC policy rather than in the bylaws. If the County Chair declined to appoint an Endorsement Process Committee, the LDP endorsement function would lose its institutional anchor.

### Bylaws Committee should consider

Promoting Endorsement Process from ad hoc to standing, OR formally enshrining the 60% threshold and process in the bylaws as a standalone section. This would make the endorsement framework less dependent on County Chair discretion.

(This is more of a "should we change the structure?" question than a drift-correction. Bylaws Committee judgment call.)

## What this corpus does NOT do

This corpus **does not silently resolve any of these drift items in operational copy.** When portal copy, briefings, talking points, or analytical documents reference the rules:

1. Cite the LJCDP rule as written: `[LJCDP §X.Y]`
2. Note the KDP-governing rule: `[KDP Art. X.Y]`
3. State that KDP governs per `[LJCDP §5.3]`
4. Flag the amendment owed to the Bylaws Committee

This is the standing rule per portal CLAUDE.md (April 21, 2026 update):

> "If you find new drift between KDP and LJCDP, surface it in copy as a flagged amendment need; don't silently resolve."
