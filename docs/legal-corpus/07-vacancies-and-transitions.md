# Vacancies and Transitions

How vacancies arise, how they get declared, who fills them, and on what timeline — across all four levels of party structure.

## At-a-glance: the 90-day rule, the 30-day rule, the 60-day rule, July 1 redistricting

| Tier | Vacancy filled within | Backstop | Cite |
|---|---|---|---|
| **LJCDP officer vacancies** (County Chair, County VC, LD Chair, LD VC, At-Large) | **30 days** | n/a — LJCDP has no escalation backstop, but KDP overlay applies | `[LJCDP §21.1]` |
| **KDP CEC vacancies** (same officers) | **90 days** | SCEC Chair fills if not done in 90 days | `[KDP Art. III.D.h.i]` |
| **KDP Precinct Committee vacancies** | **90 days** | SCEC Chair fills | `[KDP Art. III.B.c]` |
| **KDP redistricting vacancies** (counties with 7+ LDs) | **60 days** | SCEC Chair fills. Triggered by new LD maps on **July 1** | `[KDP Art. III.D.i.i]` |
| **KDP SCEC CD vacancies** | **30 days (caucus)** then **60 days** | SCEC Chair appoints + majority of remaining SCEC approves | `[KDP Art. IV.H.c]`, `[KDP Art. IV.H.e]` |
| **KDP SCEC At-Large vacancies** | Caucus process, then **60 days** | SCEC Chair appoints + majority | `[KDP Art. IV.H.d]`, `[KDP Art. IV.H.e]` |

**Operating rule:** `[LJCDP §21.1]` says "within thirty (30) days"; `[KDP Art. III.D.h.i]` says "within ninety (90) days"; KDP governs the outer envelope. The CEC should target the thirty (30) day LJCDP standard internally, but cannot be declared in violation of KDP unless ninety (90) days pass without action. If the LJCDP §21.1 thirty (30) day standard is missed, the seat is not automatically forfeited — the CEC retains the chance to fill it through day ninety (90), after which the SCEC Chair fills.

## How vacancies arise — the triggers

### Resignation in writing (LJCDP)
Per `[KDP Art. III.D.f.iii]`: "If any Committee member dies, moves from the County or Legislative District, becomes disabled or disqualified to serve the area from which they were elected, or notifies the County Executive Committee in writing that they are resigning, their office shall be declared vacant by a majority vote of the members of the County Executive Committee."

LJCDP is silent on the mechanic of accepting a resignation `[LJCDP §15]`–`[LJCDP §21]` but the practice follows KDP: the resignation is in writing to the CEC; the CEC declares the seat vacant by majority vote.

### Automatic vacancy — attendance failure
**Two triggers, either is sufficient** `[LJCDP §20.1]`, `[KDP Art. III.D.g]`:
1. Fails to attend, **in person or by written proxy**, **two successive meetings**
2. Fails to attend **50% of annual meetings** in person

Per KDP, the County Chair declares the vacancy `[KDP Art. III.D.g]`. The seat is "deemed and declared vacant" — meaning no CEC vote is needed to accept the vacancy once attendance fails.

**Practice note:** "Annual meetings" means scheduled regular meetings in the calendar year, not all meetings ever held. With the §11.2 + KDP III.D.e.i.1.b ≥1 meeting/quarter requirement, "annual" is at minimum 4 meetings — so 50% is at minimum 2 missed in person.

### Automatic vacancy — County Chair fails to meet quarterly
`[LJCDP §17.1]`: "The office of the County Chair shall be declared vacant should the County Chair fail to hold at least one meeting per quarter of the County Executive Committee."

This is a hard mechanic on the County Chair specifically. Quarterly meetings per `[KDP Art. III.D.e.i.1.c]` shall occur during February, May, August, and November.

### Automatic vacancy — gender-balance rule for County VC
`[LJCDP §19.1]`: "The office of the County Vice-Chair shall be declared vacant when a person of the same sex as the incumbent County Vice-Chair is elected to serve out an unexpired term of County Chair."

This preserves the opposite-sex requirement between County Chair and County VC `[LJCDP §10.2]`. Example: if the County Chair (male) leaves mid-term and the CEC elects a female to serve out the Chair term, AND the sitting County VC is also female — the VC seat is automatically declared vacant. A new VC must be elected from the LD Chairs of a different gender than the new (female) County Chair.

### Automatic vacancy — redistricting (counties with 7+ LDs)
`[KDP Art. III.D.i]`: "In years when the general assembly draws new legislative district boundaries, the position of any Executive Committee member who no longer resides in the legislative district they were elected to represent, will become vacant on July 1."

This is automatic on July 1 of the redistricting year. 60 days for CEC to fill; else SCEC Chair fills `[KDP Art. III.D.i.i]`. Quorum for redistricting vacancy votes is 50% of remaining non-vacated CEC positions `[KDP Art. III.D.i.ii]`.

**Special County Chair preservation rule** `[KDP Art. III.D.i.iii]`: If a County Chair also holds an LD position they no longer reside in, **only the LD position is vacated, NOT the County Chair position.** This protects the County Chair from being knocked out by redistricting.

**Special County VC redistricting rule** `[KDP Art. III.D.i.iv]`: If County VC no longer resides in the LD they were elected as LD Chair → **both** the LD Chair and the County VC positions are vacated. After LD Chair vacancies are filled, the CEC elects a new County VC from among the LD Chairs of a different gender than the County Chair.

### Removal for cause (KDP)
A CEC member may be removed for cause by majority vote of the CEC `[KDP Art. III.D.f.ii]`. "For cause" includes "fraud, misappropriation, other criminal acts, creating a hostile environment or behavior in which the local executive committee deems injurious to doing business or engaging and retaining members."

Hearing required. KDP Article V appeals process applies.

### Removal for failure to support Democratic nominees
`[LJCDP §15.1]`, `[KDP Art. I.K.f]`: Majority vote of voting CEC after hearing. Officer is removed and office declared vacant.

KDP detail `[KDP Art. I.K.f]`: For removal, the Executive Committee must find by majority vote in the affirmative on TWO questions: (1) Did the officer fail to support the nominee? (2) Was the failure to support the nominee inappropriate?

This guards against the case where an officer doesn't support a nominee they were never reasonably positioned to support (e.g., for residency, conflict-of-interest, or personal circumstance).

### Removal for disavowal of allegiance
`[LJCDP §15.2]`, `[KDP Art. I.K.g]`: Majority vote of voting CEC after hearing. Single question: Did the officer disavow allegiance to either the National or KY Democratic Parties?

### Removal — County Chair without cause
`[LJCDP §16.1]`: "The County Chair may be removed at any time during her/his term without cause by a majority vote of the voting members of the County Executive Committee."

KDP parallel `[KDP Art. III.D.f.i]`: "The Chair of the County Executive Committee may be removed without cause by a majority vote of the entire Executive Committee."

KDP says "entire Executive Committee" (majority of all elected members, not just present-and-voting). LJCDP says "voting members" without specifying entire vs present-and-voting. The stricter reading per KDP is majority of ALL voting CEC members. This is the rule operating in tension with the §22.1 quorum-allowing-proxies framework, BUT note `[LJCDP §22.1.1]`: proxies cannot establish a quorum for dismissal of any Party officer. So in practice, the meeting must have 40% in person to even take this vote.

### Removal — County VC without cause
`[LJCDP §18.1]`: parallel rule for County VC.

### Intentional disclosure of confidential party info
`[KDP Art. I.K.a]`: Office declared vacant after hearing + majority vote of appropriate EC. Hearings for Precinct Officers at CEC; all other Party Officers at SCEC `[KDP Art. I.K.c]`.

### 2-year bar after dismissal
`[KDP Art. I.K.h]`: Any person dismissed for the above reasons is **prohibited from holding any office or position within the Party for two years.**

## Filling vacancies — the procedure

### LJCDP officer vacancies — 30-day target
`[LJCDP §21.1]`: "Vacancies due to removal, resignation, or any other cause, in the office of County Chair, County Vice Chair, Legislative District Chair, Legislative District Vice Chair, and At-Large member, shall be filled by a majority vote of the voting members of the County Executive Committee within thirty (30) days of such vacancy."

The CEC schedules a meeting (regular or special). Quorum requirements apply per `[LJCDP §22.1]`. Because filling an officer vacancy is an election event, **no proxies count for the vote itself** `[LJCDP §22.1.1]`, `[LJCDP §23.1]`.

### KDP CEC vacancies — 90-day envelope
`[KDP Art. III.D.h.i]`: CEC has 90 days from vacancy. After 90 days, SCEC Chair fills, notifies the new officer, and furnishes contact info to CEC Secretary.

The 30-day LJCDP target is internal. The 90-day KDP envelope is the outer deadline; missing it triggers SCEC Chair appointment.

### Precinct Committee vacancies — 90 days
`[KDP Art. III.B.c]`: When a vacancy exists on any Precinct Committee, the LD Chair (in counties with 6+ LDs — JeffCo qualifies) notifies the SCEC. The CEC has 90 days from notification of SCEC. After 90 days, SCEC Chair fills. Procedure also applies to Precinct Committee positions not filled at the Precinct Convention.

### County Chair vacancy — special considerations

The election of a successor County Chair is governed by `[LJCDP §10.1]` + `[KDP Art. III.D.b.c]`. Successor is elected by LD Chairs.

Per `[LJCDP §10.2]`: County VC must be opposite sex of Chair. So electing a successor Chair of a different sex than the incumbent VC triggers `[LJCDP §19.1]` — the VC seat is automatically declared vacant, and a new VC is elected from the LD Chairs.

### County VC vacancy — must come from LD Chair pool
`[LJCDP §10.2]`: County VC "must be a newly elected Legislative District Chair." Same rule applies to mid-term VC vacancy filling — the successor must be an LD Chair `[KDP Art. III.D.b.c.iii]`.

If no LD Chair of the appropriate gender is available, the seat may remain vacant pending the next LD-level filling. This is rare but possible.

### Method of vote — proxies not permitted for election
Filling a vacancy is an election event. **Proxies do not count** for the vote itself per `[LJCDP §23.1]`. Proxies also cannot count toward quorum for an election or dismissal per `[LJCDP §22.1.1]`, `[KDP Art. III.D.k]`.

In practice: the CEC must have 40% of LD Chairs + LD VCs + LD At-Large present **in person** to hold a vacancy vote. The vote itself is by simple majority of those present and voting `[LJCDP §21.1]`.

## SCEC-level vacancies (in case JCDEC needs to know)

### Congressional District SCEC vacancy `[KDP Art. IV.H.c]`
1. SCEC Chair notifies SCEC within **7 days**
2. Remaining CD representatives caucus to fill by majority of caucus
3. Non-binding input from SCEC Chair + County Chairs in CD within **14 days**
4. Tie broken by SCEC majority
5. Any vacancy not filled for **30 days** → filled by majority of remaining SCEC
6. Any vacancy not filled for **60 days** → SCEC Chair appoints + majority of remaining SCEC approves `[KDP Art. IV.H.e]`

### SCEC At-Large vacancy `[KDP Art. IV.H.d]`
Similar caucus process. Same 60-day backstop.

### Legal Counsel / Secretary / Treasurer of SCEC `[KDP Art. IV.H.b]`
Filled by SCEC Chair.

### General Assembly members of SCEC `[KDP Art. IV.H.a]`
Filled by vote of the Dem members of the House or Senate from which the member was originally elected.

## DNC-level vacancies (for context)

Per `[DNC Bylaws Art. II, §6]`:
- State-apportioned vacancies → filled by state/territorial Party in open meeting after public notice `[DNC Bylaws Art. II, §6(a)]`
- Vacancy from removal/resignation of state Chair or highest-ranking officer of another gender → filled only by their successors `[DNC Bylaws Art. II, §6(b)]`
- At-large vacancies → filled by National Committee `[DNC Bylaws Art. II, §6(c)]`
- Constituency-organization vacancies → filled by selecting authority or body responsible for interim operations `[DNC Bylaws Art. II, §6(d)]`

## Special-election nominating committees — different process

When a Democratic state-level officeholder dies, resigns, or vacates mid-term and the seat needs a Democratic nominee for the special election, the **Nominating Committee** structure under `[KDP Art. VI]` governs — NOT the CEC vacancy procedure above.

This is distinct from filling a CEC seat. The Nominating Committee selects the replacement nominee for the special election ballot; CEC seat-filling is for replacing internal party officers.

See `02-kdp-bylaws.md` Article VI section for the full breakdown by district type. Quick summary for JeffCo:

| Seat | Who's on the Nominating Committee | Vote weighting |
|---|---|---|
| State Senate (wholly JeffCo, e.g. SD-26) | LD Chairs of every LD wholly or partially in district | Per `[KDP Art. VI.D]` proportional |
| State House (wholly JeffCo) | Precinct Committee members residing in LD | Vote of each PC member = total registered Dems in their precinct |
| Metro Council (single LD or multi-LD within JeffCo) | Voting CEC members + LD VC residing in MC district's LDs. VC has a vote. | Proportional to Dem registration within district + LD |
| US House (multi-county) | Dem County Chairs of each county in district | Per `[KDP Art. VI.D]` proportional, chaired by County Chair with largest Dem vote at preceding Presidential |
| Statewide | Voting members of SCEC at time of vacancy | Equal weight |

**Authority:** All Nominating Committees are governing authority `[KDP Art. VI.F]`. By majority vote, may include other Democratic Party Officials as voting members.

**Process:** LD Chair (for State House districts wholly in JeffCo) convenes an organizing committee of CEC members residing in the LD to determine date/time/location of the nominating committee meeting and establish the nomination process `[KDP Art. VI.C.b.ii]`. The LD Chair then notifies PC members `[KDP Art. VI.C.b.iii]`.

## Transitions — start of term

When a new officer takes office (after election in the standard cycle OR after vacancy fill):

| Transition action | Deadline | Cite |
|---|---|---|
| County Chair appoints Secretary + Treasurer | Within 30 days of election | `[LJCDP §11.3]`, `[KDP Art. III.D.b.vi]` |
| Outgoing officer returns documents to CEC | Within 15 days of leaving position | `[KDP Art. III.D.e.i.2]` (Chair/VC), `[KDP Art. III.D.e.ii.2]` (Secretary), `[KDP Art. III.D.e.iii.2]` (Treasurer) |
| Incoming officer signs NDA (LD Chairs, LD VCs, At-Large) | Start of term | `[LJCDP §2.2]` |
| Update KDP records | After fill — Secretary notifies SCEC Chair | `[KDP Art. III.D.h.i]` (implied) |

## Tracking transitions in the portal

The portal's `transitions` Supabase table (per CLAUDE.md data model) captures:
- `seat_code` (e.g. "LD33_VC")
- `previous_holder_name`, `previous_holder_id`
- `departed_date`, `departure_reason`
- `successor_id`, `elected_date`
- `status` (VACANT | FILLED)

This gives the operational view of where the CEC is in the 30-day / 90-day windows. The bylaws set the rules; the transitions table tracks the live state.

## Audit checks

When auditing a vacancy fill for procedural compliance, confirm:

1. **Was the vacancy properly declared?** Trigger met per §15, §17, §19, §20, KDP III.D.f, or III.D.g.
2. **Did the CEC meeting have proper quorum?** 40% of LD Chairs + LD VCs + LD At-Large in person (proxies don't count for officer election).
3. **Was the vote conducted without proxies?** Per `[LJCDP §23.1]`.
4. **Was the timeline met?** LJCDP §21.1 30-day standard; KDP §III.D.h.i 90-day envelope.
5. **Was the gender-balance rule preserved?** Per `[LJCDP §10.2]`, `[LJCDP §19.1]`, `[KDP Art. III.D.b.c.ii]`.
6. **Was residency verified?** Per `[LJCDP §4.3]` (LD positions), `[KDP Art. III.D.b.iv]` (county-level).
7. **Did the County VC vacancy backflow correctly?** If a same-sex Chair was elected mid-term, was the VC seat declared vacant per §19.1?
