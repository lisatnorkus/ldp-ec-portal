# Reorganization Cycle — Precinct → LD → County → CD → State

Cross-cutting reference for the four-year presidential-cycle Reorganization process. Pulls from `[KDP Art. II]` (the operative authority on convention mechanics), `[LJCDP §§4, 10]` (the JeffCo-specific layer), and `[DNC Charter Art. II]` (the National Convention cap).

## When is the next reorganization?

**2028.** Confirmed on presidential-year cadence going forward `[KDP Art. II.A]`.

`[KDP Art. I.R]` was a one-time shift to align Reorganization with presidential election years — sunset at the close of the 2025 State Convention. After 2025, the cadence is permanent: 2028, 2032, 2036, …

Per portal CLAUDE.md (April 21, 2026 red-pen):
> "The 2028 reorganization cycle is on a presidential-election year cadence per KDP Bylaws Article II.A."

This means JCDEC officers elected in 2025 serve through the close of the 2028 LD Convention. Terms are 4 years `[LJCDP §4.5]`, `[LJCDP §10.3]`, `[KDP Art. II.B.b]`.

## The 5 Convention Levels

| Level | Who elects | Who is elected | Eligible voters | KDP cite |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Precinct** | Open registered Dems in precinct | 2 people of different genders + 1 youth (any gender) | Any Dem residing AND registered in precinct | `[KDP Art. II.C]` |
| **Legislative District** | Newly elected Precinct Committee members in that LD | LD Chair + LD Vice Chair (different genders); delegates to CD + State | Duly elected PC members from that LD | `[KDP Art. II.D]` |
| **County (counties ≤6 LDs only — not Jefferson)** | Precinct Committee members at County Convention | CEC members (10–20); delegates to CD + State | Duly elected PC members | `[KDP Art. II.E]` |
| **Congressional District** | County Delegates | 4 SCEC members (no more than 2 same gender) + 1 youth per CD | County Delegates from County Convention | `[KDP Art. II.F]` |
| **State** | State Delegates | 9 at-large SCEC members; ratifies Chair, VC, bylaws | State Delegates from County or LD Convention | `[KDP Art. II.G]` |

**Jefferson County is a 7+ LD county.** It uses LD Conventions, not a County Convention. There are 18 LDs wholly or partially within Jefferson County.

Per `[KDP Art. I.B.f]`: "Legislative District Convention" applies to counties with seven (7) or more State House legislative districts. Per `[KDP Art. I.B.c]`: "County Convention" applies to counties with six (6) or fewer.

## Reorganization year timeline (2028 cycle)

Times are approximate placeholders pending SCEC's adopted 2028 calendar. SCEC sets dates and times per `[KDP Art. II.B.e]`. Conventions "are generally held in the spring preceding the Democratic National Convention" per `[KDP Art. II.B.e]`.

### Q4 2027 — Pre-filing window opens

- **October 1, 2027:** LD Chair, LD VC, LD At-Large pre-filing window opens at LJCDP HQ per `[LJCDP §4.2.1]`. Filings continue through 5pm on final filing date of 2028.
- Pre-filing is **optional** — candidates may also be nominated from the floor at the LD Convention `[LJCDP §4.2.1]`.
- Filings must be in person at LJCDP HQ `[LJCDP §4.2]`.
- Candidates **may not file together** — must file independently `[LJCDP §4.2.2]`. Slate campaigning is allowed; slate filing is not.
- Candidates must be a **resident of the District they intend to serve** at time of election `[LJCDP §4.3]`.

### Q1 2028 — SCEC issues Call to Convention

- SCEC issues the Call to Convention. Dates set by SCEC `[KDP Art. II.B.e]`.
- CEC Chairs determine locations of Precinct, LD, and County Conventions; SCEC Chair determines locations of CD and State `[KDP Art. II.B.e]`.

### ~7–30 days before each Convention — Advertisement

- CEC Chair + SCEC Chair advertise widely `[KDP Art. II.B.f]`.
- Must use **at least 3 of 6 methods** `[KDP Art. II.B.f.i]`–`[KDP Art. II.B.f.vi]`:
  1. Local newspapers
  2. Local radio/TV
  3. County party social media pages
  4. County party website
  5. Mobilize / Facebook Events / web-based organization tools
  6. Email / US Mail / phone calls to CEC + county Democrats on email list

### Spring 2028 — Convention day

**Single day or sequenced days set by SCEC.** Typical pattern:

#### (a) Precinct Conventions

- **Eligibility to vote:** Any Democrat residing AND registered in the precinct `[KDP Art. II.C.b]`.
- **Elects:** Two people of different genders + a youth (any gender) as Precinct Committee Members `[KDP Art. II.C.a]`.
- **No proxies** at any Convention `[KDP Art. II.B.i.i]`.
- **Voting method:** If 2+ persons nominated, secret ballot. One vote per office. Tie → immediate runoff. Tied runoff → coin flip `[KDP Art. II.B.i]`.
- **Precinct Chair selected** from elected committee, by the attendees `[KDP Art. II.C.c]`.
- **Convention Chair:** A Party Officer at the applicable level (in JeffCo, the relevant LD Chair). If unavailable, the County Party Chair appoints `[KDP Art. II.B.g]`.

#### (b) Legislative District Conventions

- **Eligibility to vote:** Only duly elected Precinct Committee members from that LD `[KDP Art. II.D.b]`.
- **Elects:** LD Chair + Vice Chair (different genders) `[KDP Art. II.D.a]`; delegates to CD + State Conventions.
- **No proxies** `[KDP Art. II.B.i.i]`.
- **Open floor nominations** `[KDP Art. II.B.i.ii]`.
- **Convention Chair:** Party Officer at LD level. Per portal CLAUDE.md (April 21, 2026 red-pen): in practice, LD Chairs preside as Convention Chairs `[KDP Art. II.B.g]`.

#### (c) At Close of LD Convention — Joint Election of County Officers + 18 At-Large

**This is the contested timing point.** Per `[KDP Art. II.D.c]`:

> "At close of the Legislative District Convention, newly elected LD Chairs and Vice Chairs shall meet to elect eighteen (18) at-large members of the County Executive Committee and Committee officers."

Per `[KDP Art. III.D.b.c]`, in counties with 7+ LDs:

- Newly elected LD Chairs meet immediately after LD Convention close
- Meeting chaired by incumbent County Chair (no vote unless newly elected as LD Chair)
- Elect County Chair + Vice Chair of different genders
- **Vice Chair MUST be a newly-elected LD Chair** `[KDP Art. III.D.b.c.iii]`
- County Chair "need not" be a newly-elected LD Chair `[KDP Art. III.D.b.c.iii]`
- Only CEC members nominate / vote
- Then elect 18 additional At-Large members **within thirty-five (35) days** per `[KDP Art. III.D.b.c.v]`, proportional to Dem registration per LD, two (2) of whom must be thirty-five (35) years of age or under

**Drift:**
- `[KDP Art. II.D.c]` says At-Large elected "at close of LD Convention."
- `[KDP Art. III.D.b.c.v]` says "within thirty-five days" of CEC election.
- `[LJCDP §4.4]` says "within thirty (30) days of the Reorganization."

Within KDP itself, II.D.c says "at close" and III.D.b.c.v allows 35 days post-CEC-election. These don't necessarily conflict — II.D.c may describe the immediate election of County Chair/VC + the 18, with III.D.b.c.v providing a 35-day window for the proportional 18.

LJCDP §4.4 (30 days) conflicts with KDP. **KDP governs per `[LJCDP §5.3]`.**

**How the 2025 cycle actually ran the sequencing:** unknown without confirmation from Brook Benningfield (Secretary) or Logan Gatti (Chair). Per portal CLAUDE.md, the bylaws say it should have run per KDP Art. II.D.c; that's consistent with v1 portal content listing 18 At-Large Chairs elected in June 2025.

#### (d) Within 10 days post-Convention — Reporting

Newly elected County Party Chair submits **within ten (10) days** following Precinct/LD/County Conventions `[KDP Art. II.B.l]`:
- All Precinct, LD, and County Convention minutes
- Officer forms
- CD and State Convention delegate forms
- Required sign-in sheets
- Other requested information

To State Party Chair.

### Within 30 days of County Chair election — Secretary + Treasurer

The newly elected County Chair appoints a Secretary and a Treasurer (ex-officio non-voting CEC members) **within 30 days** of election `[LJCDP §11.3]`, `[KDP Art. III.D.b.vi]`.

These appointments serve at the pleasure of the Chair `[KDP Art. III.D.b.vi]`.

### Q2 2028 — Congressional District + State Conventions

#### Congressional District Conventions

- **Eligibility to vote:** County Delegates elected by the County Convention (or, in JeffCo's case, LD Conventions producing State Delegates that also vote at CD level per apportionment) `[KDP Art. II.F.b]`.
- **Elects:** 4 SCEC members (no more than 2 of same gender) + 1 youth per CD `[KDP Art. II.F.a]`.
- **Apportionment:** Formula based on Dem registration + Dem vote in last 2 Presidentials `[KDP Art. II.F.c]`.

#### State Convention

- **Eligibility to vote:** State Delegates elected by County / LD Conventions `[KDP Art. II.G.b]`.
- **Elects:** 9 at-large SCEC members; ratifies Chair, Vice Chair, and bylaws `[KDP Art. II.G.a]`.

### Concurrent — DNC Delegate Selection

Delegates to the Democratic National Convention are elected per the Delegate Selection Plan adopted by SCEC and approved by the DNC `[KDP Art. I.O]`, `[KDP Art. II.H]`. The plan is published each cycle and supersedes the bylaws to the extent it conflicts `[KDP Art. II.H.b]`. The 2028 Delegate Selection Plan is not in this corpus; it lives with KDP staff.

## Who calls vs who chairs — clarification

This was contested in v1 portal copy and resolved per portal CLAUDE.md:

| Role | Who does it |
|---|---|
| **Calls + advertises Precinct + LD Conventions** | CEC Chair `[KDP Art. II.B.f]` |
| **Presides as Convention Chair at Precinct + LD Conventions** | Party Officer at applicable level. In practice for JeffCo: LD Chairs `[KDP Art. II.B.g]` |
| **If LD Chair unavailable** | County Party Chair appoints a Convention Chair `[KDP Art. II.B.g]` |
| **Calls + advertises County / CD / State Conventions** | SCEC Chair `[KDP Art. II.B.e]`, `[KDP Art. II.B.f]` |
| **Presides at County / CD Conventions** | Party Officer. If unavailable: State Party Chair appoints `[KDP Art. II.B.g]` |
| **Presides at State Convention** | KDP Chair (or VC) |

**v2.0 portal update:** LD Chair one-pager no longer says "you call" conventions. It says "you preside."

## Eligibility to run — the residency rule

For any LD-level office in JeffCo, candidate must be **resident of the District they intend to serve** at the time of election `[LJCDP §4.3]`. KDP-level: any Democrat registered to vote within the applicable Convention Level can run `[KDP Art. II.B.c]`.

A Precinct Committee candidate must be registered to vote within the precinct.

A County Chair candidate need not be a newly-elected LD Chair `[KDP Art. III.D.b.c.iii]`, but the County Vice Chair must be `[KDP Art. III.D.b.c.iii]`, `[LJCDP §10.2]`. The County Chair must be any Democrat registered to vote in Jefferson County `[LJCDP §10.1]`.

## Filing window — recap (for LD Chair / LD VC / LD At-Large)

| Date | Action |
|---|---|
| **Oct 1, 2027** | Pre-filing window opens at LJCDP HQ `[LJCDP §4.2.1]` |
| Through 5pm on final filing date of 2028 (per Jefferson County Clerk schedule for local partisan offices) | Pre-filing window closes |
| **LD Convention day** | Floor nominations allowed `[LJCDP §4.2.1]` |

**Precinct Committee candidates:** No pre-filing. Elected at the Precinct Convention itself by eligible voters in the precinct `[KDP Art. II.C]`.

**County Chair + Vice Chair + 18 At-Large:** No separate pre-filing. Elected at the post-LD joint meeting from among newly-elected LD officers (and any qualifying Democrat for County Chair) `[KDP Art. II.D.c]`, `[KDP Art. III.D.b.c]`.

## What can stop the schedule

### Waivers (KDP Art. II.B.e + V.G)

A CEC may petition the SCEC Chair to change the date/time of a Precinct, LD, or County Convention for **extraordinary circumstances**:

- Act of God (tornado, flood, earthquake)
- Officially declared state of emergency
- Conflicting countrywide major event over which CEC has no control
- Any other event the SCEC Chair agrees is extraordinary

Submit waiver request as soon as the conflict is known. SCEC Chair responds **within 7 days** for foreseen, **within 24 hours** for unforeseen / late-notice (<30 days before Convention) `[KDP Art. II.B.e.i.1]`.

CEC must advertise the change per `[KDP Art. I.M]` notification requirements.

### Failure to reorganize (KDP Art. III.D.d)

If a county fails to properly reorganize, abide by Convention rules, meet minimum CEC membership, follow approved waivers, or conduct meetings:

1. SCEC Chair declares no valid CEC
2. Notifies Executive Director
3. Appoints ad-hoc committee (local Dems + SCEC members + adjacent county parties) for **up to 120 days** to identify + support establishment of new CEC
4. If after 120 days no CEC established: ad-hoc committee reports to SCEC Chair on identified issues + next steps

This is the failure-to-reorganize backstop and is not expected to apply to Jefferson County.

### Failure to meet minimum CEC count (Counties ≤6 LDs only)

`[KDP Art. III.D.b.vi.1]` describes a Provisional CEC + Temporary Chair process if a County Convention elects fewer than 10 CEC members but more than 6. Not applicable to Jefferson County (7+ LDs).

## Appeals from a Reorganization Convention — `[KDP Art. V]`

If something goes wrong at the Convention:

| Convention | File appeal with | Deadline | Hearing |
|---|---|---|---|
| Precinct Convention or Precinct Election | County Chair | 3 days | CEC hearing within 3 days |
| LD or County Convention | SCEC Chair | 7 days | SCEC hearing within 15 days (final and conclusive) |
| State Convention | SCEC Chair | 7 days | SCEC hearing within 15 days (final and conclusive) |
| CD Convention | Appellate Committee → SCEC | 7 days post-Appellate decision | SCEC hearing within 30 days |
| All other appeals | SCEC Chair | 5 days | SCEC hearing within 30 days |

**No member may bring legal action without first exhausting appeals. Jurisdiction + venue: Franklin County Circuit Court, KY** `[KDP Art. V.F.a]`.

See `00-quick-reference.md` for full day counts.

## Why this cycle matters operationally

The current LDPEC was elected in 2025 (the one-time-shift cycle). Most members have never been through a presidential-year Reorganization. Per portal CLAUDE.md:

> "The current LDPEC is almost entirely first-term: KDP postponed the 2024 reorg to 2025, so most members were elected in the 2025 cycle and have never been through a presidential-year reorg. They are learning their roles in real time."

2028 is the first presidential-year reorganization in the post-Art-I.R era. The mechanics are unchanged from 2025 procedurally — but the cadence (and expectations) reset to the permanent presidential-year cycle. Treat 2028 as the first "normal" reorganization since 2016 (the most recent pre-shift presidential-year reorganization).
