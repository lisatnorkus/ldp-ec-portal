## Standing committee anatomy (per `[LJCDP §26]`)

### How a standing committee is created
By the bylaws themselves. §26.1 lists them. To add or remove a standing committee, the bylaws must be amended per `[LJCDP §29.1]`:
- Majority vote at meeting with quorum
- Either ≥2 weeks written notice OR previously introduced

### How a standing committee chair is appointed
The County Chair appoints the Standing Committee Chairs per `[LJCDP §11.6]`:

> "To appoint members of the County Executive Committee to any Standing, Regular, Select, Special, Ad-Hoc, or any other committees of the County Executive Committee."

This is a Chair-discretion appointment. No CEC vote required to appoint a Standing Committee Chair.

### How committee members are appointed
Same authority — County Chair appoints `[LJCDP §11.6]`. In practice, members may volunteer, the Committee Chair may recruit, and the County Chair formalizes appointments.

### The County Chair's voting role on all committees
`[LJCDP §11.7]`: "To serve as an ex-officio voting member of all Committees of the County Executive Committee."

This means the County Chair has a seat at the table on every committee — Bylaws, Finance, Endorsement Process, all of them — and may vote on committee business. This is not a notional privilege; it's a bylaw-mandated ex-officio voting seat.

### How committee duties are determined
`[LJCDP §26.1.1]`: "The duties of the Standing Committees are to be determined by the County Chair and the Standing Committee Chair for each Standing Committee."

Each committee's scope and operating procedure is jointly set by the County Chair and the Standing Committee Chair. Not by the CEC as a whole. This gives committees significant operational flexibility.

### Facilities Committee
- Manages LJCDP HQ (the physical Dem headquarters)
- Carolyn Benedict = Facilities Chair (also LD35 VC)
- Authority anchor: `[LJCDP §26.1]` standing committee

### Finance Committee
- Mike Ward = Finance Chair (per portal CLAUDE.md)
- Adopts **Fiscal Policy**, reviewed annually in **January** per `[LJCDP §26.2]`
- Oversees ALL purchases per `[LJCDP §26.3]`:

- Authority anchor: `[LJCDP §26.1]` standing committee + bylaw-mandated authority per `[LJCDP §26.2]` and `[LJCDP §26.3]`

### Training Committee

## How the County Chair structures committees

`[LJCDP §11.6]` gives the County Chair broad authority to appoint members and chairs to any "Standing, Regular, Select, Special, Ad-Hoc, or any other committees."

The five committee-types named in §11.6:
- Standing — listed in §26.1
- Regular — undefined; appears to mean ongoing committees that aren't standing
- Select — typically narrow-scope, time-limited committees
- Special — typically project-specific, time-limited committees
- Ad-Hoc — short-term, problem-specific committees

In practice, the categorization between Regular, Select, Special, and Ad-Hoc has blurred. JCDEC currently labels the non-standing committees (Branding, Endorsement Process, Platform) as "ad hoc."

## Cross-roles — members holding multiple committee positions

Per portal CLAUDE.md, the following dual-role examples are documented:
- **Carolyn Benedict** — LD35 VC AND Facilities Chair
- **Jessica Haggy** — Volunteer Chair AND Jefferson County Democratic Women's Club President (the latter is a voting CEC seat per `[LJCDP §3.1]`, `[LJCDP §3.2]`)

`[LJCDP §11.6]` allows a member to hold multiple committee roles. The `ec_members` data model in the portal captures this with `committee_chair_codes` and `committee_member_codes` arrays.

## Quorum within a committee

The bylaws are silent on committee-level quorum. Robert's Rules of Order applies as fallback per `[LJCDP §28.1]`. Per Robert's Rules, a majority of the committee membership constitutes quorum unless otherwise specified.

This means a committee with 5 members has quorum at 3. A committee chair may set a stricter rule by committee policy.

## Committee removal of chair / member

The bylaws are silent. Per `[LJCDP §11.6]`, the County Chair appoints; the implication (under Robert's Rules and standard committee practice) is that the County Chair can also un-appoint, since the appointment is at the Chair's discretion.

For KDP-level parallel: `[KDP Art. IV.O]` says the Bylaws Committee chair "shall serve at the pleasure of the KDP Chair." The LJCDP analog is implied but not stated.

If the CEC wants to remove a committee chair against the County Chair's wishes, the only path is the §16 removal of the County Chair (without cause) and election of a new County Chair who would then make different appointments.

## Standing rule on committee structure for this corpus

When discussing a JCDEC committee, **lead with the bylaw text** (`[LJCDP §26.1]`'s 10-committee list) and **then describe the practice** (8 standing + 3 ad hoc). Do not silently substitute practice for bylaw.

For renaming drift specifically:
- Use "Communications Committee" in operational copy (it's the active name)
- But when citing the bylaw, cite `[LJCDP §26.1]` and use the bylaw text "Communication"

This preserves both the rule-on-paper and the operational reality. The amendment to align them is owed to the Bylaws Committee.
